# FEY FAIRE — Court hierarchies + player standing

Which fey answers to whom · how the player's actions shift the
Courts' regard · what happens at CLOSING NIGHT depending on who
respects the player and who doesn't.

See also `_FEY_FAIRE_DESIGN.md` for the master overview,
`_FEY_FAIRE_MECHANICS.md` for combat/death/checkpoints, and
`_FEY_FAIRE_ROUTES.md` for endings.

## Three courts, one wildcard

- **THE SEELIE COURT** — kind but capricious.  Ruled by Titania.
- **THE UNSEELIE COURT** — cruel but honorable.  Ruled by Oberon.
- **THE WILDFEY** — unbound.  No monarch.  Deferential to the
  Green Man; frightened of Queen Mab; entertained by Puck; suspicious
  of Morgan le Fey.
- **MORGAN LE FEY** — Arthurian, no Court, appears only on Night 6
  if the protagonist is unseelie-aligned.  Her arrival changes the
  entire endgame.

## Seelie Court hierarchy (top → bottom)

```
                       TITANIA  (queen)
                      /    |    \
              ARIEL   ONDINE    (herald / gate-keeper split)
             /    \   /    \
        Peaseblossom  Salt Sisters
          Mustardseed   Nixies
            Cobweb
             Moth
```

- **Titania** is monarch.  Everyone below her defers, though
  Ariel resents his position and Ondine is technically on
  loan from the Sea Court.
- **Ariel** is Titania's herald in the human world.  He runs
  the Ferris wheel because he owes her (specifically) six
  turns of the wheel, and he's been running it for centuries.
- **Ondine** is Titania's gate-keeper for the Faire.  She's
  the FIRST fey the protagonist meets · not because she's the
  most important, but because she's the most patient.
- **Peaseblossom / Mustardseed / Cobweb / Moth** are Titania's
  personal retinue from A Midsummer Night's Dream.  They gossip
  about Titania to whoever will listen.  Recruiting any one of
  them means Titania hears everything said in your presence.
- **Salt Sisters** are Ondine's cousins.  Three-in-one.  Not
  formally subordinate to anyone but they follow Ondine's lead.
- **Nixies** are Ondine's siblings.  Plural, unnamed
  individually · one Nixie is any Nixie.

### Seelie internal politics
- Titania and Oberon are married and quarreling.  The subject
  of the quarrel is a changeling in Oberon's court.  Titania
  wants him back.
- Ariel resents his indefinite service to Titania.  He would
  serve YOU if you asked correctly · but doing so is a
  DEFECTION and Titania will remember.
- Ondine is technically loyal to a Sea Court that isn't in
  this game.  Her interest in the Faire is her own.

### How to gain Seelie standing
- Recruit any Seelie fey (+1 per tier-1, +2 per tier-2, +3 per tier-3).
- Fight and DEFEAT any Unseelie fey (+1).
- Recite a Midsummer or Tempest quote to a Seelie fey correctly (+1).
- Refuse an Unseelie prize offer publicly (+2).

### How to lose Seelie standing
- KO any Seelie fey · even in defense (-2 per tier-1, -4 tier-2, -6 tier-3).
- Recruit an Unseelie fey while a Seelie fey is watching (-2).
- Take a Seelie fey's true name and use it against them (-4).
- ACCEPT Oberon's request-of-loyalty (-8, locks Unseelie).

## Unseelie Court hierarchy (top → bottom)

```
                       OBERON  (king)
                      /    |    \
                 ERLKING  BAOBHAN SÌTH
                   |         (courtesan)
                 SLUAGH
                   |
                 REDCAP
                 NUCKELAVEE  (uncaged asset · not a subordinate)
                 CALIBAN     (recent · unclear rank)
```

- **Oberon** is monarch.  Everyone below him defers, though
  Nuckelavee is not TECHNICALLY his — he keeps the horror-
  fey on retainer, not on payroll.
- **Erlking** is Oberon's Huntmaster.  He runs the wheel of
  fortune to identify hunt-worthy children.  Sector 16 (CHILD)
  is the sector he prays lands during the spin.
- **Baobhan Sìth** is a courtesan · titled position, formally
  ranked below Erlking, informally rivals him for Oberon's
  ear.  She runs the kissing booth.  She is very good at her job.
- **Sluagh** is not a she but a THEY · a host of dead-that-fly.
  Oberon commands them but doesn't like them.  Nobody likes them.
  Sluagh commands Redcap (through the wind).
- **Redcap** is a footman.  Cheerful.  Murderous by tradition.
  Deferential to everyone above.
- **Nuckelavee** is an asset.  Kept nearby.  Not deployed.
  Nuckelavee is not welcome in Oberon's court but is welcome
  at his side when Oberon travels.
- **Caliban** is very new to this arrangement.  Was recently
  Prospero's servant · Prospero freed him or he escaped, unclear.
  He is not yet ranked.  He is looking for a new master.

### Unseelie internal politics
- Erlking and Baobhan Sìth compete for Oberon's favor.  If the
  protagonist plays them off each other, both grant favors.
- Oberon and Titania's quarrel is the CENTRAL POLITICAL EVENT
  of the game.  Every faction has an opinion.
- Nuckelavee is a scandal.  Oberon's public face is honor and
  cruelty in equal measure; Nuckelavee is only cruelty.
  Oberon uses him and is embarrassed by it.
- The Sluagh's loyalty is not Oberon's, exactly · they follow
  the west wind.  If the wind changes, the Sluagh does not.

### How to gain Unseelie standing
- Recruit any Unseelie fey (+1 tier-1, +2 tier-2, +3 tier-3).
- Fight and DEFEAT any Seelie fey (+1).
- Recite a Macbeth or Titus Andronicus quote to an Unseelie
  fey correctly (+1).
- Accept Erlking's boon in the wheel-of-fortune scene (+3).
- ACCEPT Oberon's request-of-loyalty (+8, locks Unseelie).

### How to lose Unseelie standing
- KO any Unseelie fey (-2 tier-1, -4 tier-2, -6 tier-3).
- Recite a Midsummer quote in Oberon's presence (-2, seelie-coded).
- ACCEPT Titania's loyalty at closing (-8, locks Seelie).
- Refuse Erlking's boon (-3).

## Wildfey — non-hierarchical, but with weight

The Wildfey do not have a monarch.  They have INFLUENCE and
DEFERENCE.  If a Wildfey speaks, others listen based on that
individual fey's weight.

Weight (approximate):
```
GREEN MAN                                    (elder · everyone defers)
QUEEN MAB · LEANAN SÍDHE                     (feared · avoided)
PUCK · PPOOKA                                (respected · liked)
KELPIE · SELKIE · MORGAN LE FEY (rare)       (independents · deep)
OSSORY WOLF                                  (not-really-a-fey · man)
BOGGART · WILL-O'-WISP · HOB · CRICKET       (small · overlooked)
NIXIES (also Seelie-adjacent)                (background)
```

- **Green Man** is elder.  Not a leader but a WEIGHT.  Every
  Wildfey checks with the Green Man before doing something.
  He usually says nothing.  His silence is answer enough.
- **Queen Mab** is FEARED.  She rides dreams · she can visit
  any Wildfey's sleep, and she does, and they don't like it.
  Wildfey give her a wide berth.
- **Leanan Sídhe** is likewise feared — her deal (brilliance
  for years) is bad news for the fey ecosystem.  She recruits
  Wildfey too, sometimes, and their friends never see them again.
- **Puck** is universally respected because he could be doing
  much worse things than the mild pranks he actually does.
- **Ossory Wolf** is the only fey who is arguably still human.
  Wildfey are unsure how to treat him.  He is treated well but
  quietly.
- **Cricket** is the smallest fey in weight AND size, but her
  position at the Gate makes her important.  She is the FIRST
  and LAST fey the protagonist meets, and she remembers.

### How to gain Wildfey standing
- Recruit any Wildfey (+1 tier-1, +2 tier-2, +3 tier-3).
- NEGOTIATE (not fight, not recruit) any Wildfey to a stalemate (+1).
- Bring the Green Man his specific prize (a dried leaf from
  outside the Faire) (+4).
- Refuse both Court alignments at Night 5 (+5).

### How to lose Wildfey standing
- KO any Wildfey (-2, -4, -6 by tier).
- ACCEPT either Court's exclusive loyalty (-4).
- Use the Green Man's name in THREATEN (impossible · attempting
  it dismisses the fight and -10 Wildfey).

## Court disposition tracked per fey

Each fey also has a PERSONAL disposition toward the player,
tracked separately from Court standing.  A Seelie fey may
personally like a player who's aligned Unseelie · Ondine
particularly likes protagonists with sympathy for the sea
regardless of their Court.

Personal disposition:
```
LOVED    (+4 to negotiation checks, will fight for you unpaid)
LIKED    (+2 · will trade fairly)
NEUTRAL  ( 0 · default)
WARY     (-2 · asks for extra prize)
HATED    (-4 · will not negotiate at all · attacks on sight)
```

Personal disposition shifts by:
- Direct interactions (a good conversation +1, an insult -2)
- Fulfilling promises (each fulfilled promise from this fey +2)
- Fighting them (any combat -3, KO -6)
- Being seen doing something they hate (Titania sees you kiss
  Baobhan Sìth: -3 Titania personal, -1 Seelie standing)

## Standing gates content

Certain content is LOCKED behind standing thresholds:

- **Titania's House of Roses** requires Seelie ≥ 5 to enter.
- **Oberon's throne room** (backstage Big Top night 4+) requires
  Unseelie ≥ 5 to enter.
- **Green Man's true realm** (the Green, mirror 4) requires
  Wildfey ≥ 5 or the Green Man's personal disposition ≥ LIKED.
- **Morgan le Fey's arrival on Night 6** requires Unseelie ≥ 10.
- **The BECOME-THE-FAIRE ending** requires Cricket LOVED + Court
  standings all below +3 (i.e., you didn't align with any Court).
- **The BRING-BACK-THE-LOST ending** requires Wildfey ≥ 8 AND
  Green Man LOVED AND at least one specific promise fulfilled
  (varies by which "lost person" the player named at boot).

## Ending map (Court standing → outcome)

At CLOSING NIGHT the game evaluates:

- Highest Court standing determines primary ending direction:
  - Seelie ≥ 10 · SEELIE ENDING
  - Unseelie ≥ 10 · UNSEELIE ENDING
  - Wildfey ≥ 10 · WILDFEY ENDING
- If NO Court reaches 10, the ending is one of:
  - REFUSE_THE_FAIRE (walked out before Night 3)
  - BECOME_THE_FAIRE (Cricket path, Court-neutral)
  - YOU_FORGET_WHY_YOU_CAME (six deaths without recruit)
- Hidden endings layer on top:
  - BRING_BACK_THE_LOST (Wildfey path with specific promise)
  - THE_STAG_ANTLER (Wildfey but stayed too long)
  - QUEEN_MAB_KEEPS_YOU (Dream mirror completion without a return)

## Notes for implementation

- `factions.json` will store the base court/tier/personal disposition
  per fey.  Each fey's entry gets a `personal_disposition_base` and
  a `court_shift_on_recruit` block that the standing tracker consumes.
- Standing itself lives in run-state as a simple 3-int record
  (`seelie: int, unseelie: int, wildfey: int`) plus a
  `personal_disposition: Dict<fey_id, int>`.
- Every world-action that shifts standing is a small named event
  emitted through a signal, so the standing tracker + scrapbook
  + achievement layer can all subscribe to the same source.
- The disposition tables above are DRAFT.  Balancing will happen
  during authoring.
