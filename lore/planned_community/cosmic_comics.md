# COSMIC COMICS
## Linden Strip Mall · Harmony Creek Estates, TX · est. 1991 · Rick

A single-storefront comic shop in the strip mall on Linden, between
the Sally Beauty and the Great Clips. Painted Galactus on the front
window — laid down by Rick's then-girlfriend in 1996, faded but not
repainted, the violet still throws long bars across the floor in
the late-morning sun. Brass bell above the door from the 1994 estate
sale of a small bookseller in Beaumont; the previous owner heard
something in it, by Rick's account, and recommended it as the
welcome bell at any future shop. The bell has been chiming arrivals
in this room for thirty-one years.

The shop is the volume's most-frequented interior. By the end of
vol6, every named young person in Harmony Creek who matters has
crossed the threshold — Sam, Jesse, Ben, Diego (in his July
absence, by Wren's testimony, asking if she'd seen Maya), Mrs.
Pimentel (delivering cookies in late June), Skip Donnelly (once,
in early August, to buy a single back-issue of *Doctor Strange*
#39 for reasons he did not share), and — on a Saturday in August
— a small grey sedan parked across the lot whose driver did not
get out and whom Curtis, watching from inside, identified by gait
alone as the host of *Whispers from the Liminal* and elected, by
quiet decision, not to mention.

## the staff

### Rick (proprietor, since 1991)

Sixty-two. Grey-shot beard. Denim jacket over a tour shirt from a
regional band that no longer exists. Reading glasses on a chain.
Lives in a small apartment over the shop. Has, in his ownership of
the shop, also become the shop's archivist — the back-room zine
shelves are his project, started in 1991 as a side practice and
since continued without interruption. Took two weeks off in mid-
August for a family thing he did not, with Maya, fully describe;
Ch. 12 of vol6 is the first day she runs the shop in his absence.

He is canonically also the answerer of a phone call from a man
in Beaumont who has been calling him every two-to-seven weeks
since 2002. Rick does not, by editorial discipline, tell anybody
about the call. The shop's landline — the old AT&T beige two-line
unit on the back counter — is the line the call comes in on.

### Curtis (floor manager since 2007)

Fifty-six. Tall, thin, short grey ponytail. Comes in at 10:15
sharp. Different t-shirt every weekday, all from regional small-
press comics runs: MIRACLEMAN (Tuesdays), HELLBOY (Wednesdays),
LOVE AND ROCKETS (Thursdays), CEREBUS (Fridays), the BLACK PANTHER
"World of Wakanda" tour shirt from 1979 (Saturdays), 100 BULLETS
(Sundays). The Monday shirt rotates and is, by Curtis's quiet
policy, never the same as the Sunday shirt.

Has been Rick's number two for nineteen years. Has, in those
nineteen years, never been given the title. Knows the subscription
list by memory. Has, by Rick's quiet acknowledgment to Maya in
July, been owed an assistant-manager appointment for fifteen years
that Rick has never made. Curtis stays.

### Wren (employee, since spring 2025)

Sixteen. Long black braid. Glasses. Lives with her mother in the
apartment complex on the south side of Harmony Creek. Plays the
SCUMM machine at Pizza Pirate on her Tuesday afternoons. *(See
the milk_and_honey/scumm_machine.md file: Wren is canonically the
receiver who, in Act III, gets the message "Wren, the substrate
is leaking. Tell Tem." from the SCUMM machine. She has, in vol6,
not yet received it. She works at Cosmic in vol6 and will, by
the end of vol7, be the bridge between the two coastlines.)*

Wren's role at Cosmic in vol6 is the small specific job of helping
Maya not be the only teenager in the shop. They have, since June,
developed the careful peer-friendship that two sixteen-year-old
girls develop when both have decided not to be best friends in
order to remain friends.

### Maya Daigle (employee, since April 2025)

Sixteen. Curly hair dyed dark purple at the tips. Black-frame
glasses. Lives with her grandmother **Connie Daigle** at lot 7 of
phase I. Daughter of **Jimmy Daigle** *(canonical: The Devil from
vol5, currently in New Orleans, occasionally calls)*. Mother in
Graustark with second husband.

In April Rick hired her on a hunch he has, since, declined to
fully explain even to himself. She has been at the shop four months
by Ch. 12. She works after school during the school year, the
mid-shift on weekdays in summer, and either Saturday or Sunday but
not both. Rick pays her cash and has, in his own ledger but not in
any official record, classified her hours under *"INSTRUCTION"*
rather than wages.

Maya is, by the codex's canonical line, *"load-bearing tissue of
the volume."* The Glitch Report extension, the F.T. correspondence,
the archive of her grandmother's vacuum-pattern beat, the
documentation of the Suburban Blight tape's circulation, the
careful slow construction of the volume's evidence chain — Maya
does this work in the back office of Cosmic Comics, on Rick's IBM
Wheelwriter 3, on the Mr. Coffee–brewed coffee, in the hours when
the shop is slow.

## the layout

### front room

Long. Narrow. The painted Galactus on the front window throws bars
of purple across the floor mid-morning. Three rows of long-box racks
down the center (Marvel, DC, Indie). Single tall back wall of new-
arrival comics behind the counter. The counter itself is a glass
display case holding small Funko Pop figures Rick orders four at a
time and that sell at a rate of approximately one every two months,
plus a small revolving rack of TPBs Rick has marked down.

The register is a Sharp UP-700 from 1998 that has, three times in
Rick's ownership, been on the verge of being replaced and that has,
three times, refused. The receipt roll is the small narrow kind.
The cash drawer is the small old-school kind. The bell on the
drawer rings when the drawer opens. The bell, on Rick's hearing,
has the same tone as the brass bell over the front door, two
octaves up.

### the YA section

In the back-left corner. Maya re-shelved this section in May,
without being asked, into a categorization the rest of the staff
has agreed to maintain. The categorization is by mood rather than
by author. There are eight categories. Maya wrote them on small
index cards in marker and taped them to the shelf edges. The
categories include *FOR WHEN YOU NEED TO BE BRAVE* (which holds
*Nimona*, *Persepolis*, the *Avatar* TPBs), *FOR WHEN NOTHING IS
WORKING* (*Hilda*, *Lumberjanes*, *Bone*), *FOR WHEN YOUR PARENTS
ARE BEING WEIRD* (*The Prince and the Dressmaker*, *Stargazing*,
*Roller Girl*), and *FOR WHEN YOU CANNOT EXPLAIN IT* (a small
shelf containing exactly four titles: *Anya's Ghost*, *Through
the Woods*, *Daytripper*, and *Sandman: The Dream Hunters*).
Maya has not, in three months, moved any of the four. The
section's most reliable repeat customer is a fourteen-year-old
named Calvin who Sam, on a delivery run in early July, recognized
as one of Jen the day manager's nephews.

### the back room

Behind the curtain. Long. Concrete floor. Two banks of grey metal
shelving along the side walls; a long-box dolly at the back; the
photocopier in the corner; the Mr. Coffee on the file cabinet; the
back-office desk under the small high window that faces the alley;
the back door with its three locks.

The shelving holds the shop's overflow and Rick's zine archive.
The zine archive — by Rick's own count in his 2023 inventory —
contains approximately eleven thousand individual zines, sorted by
year, labeled in his hand. The shelf labeled *1985–1991* holds the
*BEAUMONT 9* collective issues, the early *General Store* minicomics
made by Maya's grandfather, and — in a small acid-free sleeve at
the front of the shelf, separate from the year sorting — the
single hand-bound copy of *THE NINE-WAY POTLATCH OF AUGUST* from
August 1986.

*(The Beaumont 9 and the Potlatch are canonical to the deck's
broader history — Rick's collaborators included the young
Triumvirate (Jacob, Wagner, Faust from vol1) and the future
Evergreen Company members. Maya has not, in vol6, been told this.
Rick will tell her, eventually. The chapter is patient.)*

The Henderson Donation boxes are on the lowest shelf at the back.
There are seven of them. Maya has, by mid-August, opened three.

### the practice space

The very back of the back room. Twelve by sixteen feet. Painted
black at some point in 2008, by Rick, with help from his late
husband Dean. Has, against one wall, a small carpeted riser the
band uses as a stage. Has, against the opposite wall, three
folding chairs and a green velvet couch Rick salvaged from an
estate sale in 2014. Suburban Blight uses this space Tuesday and
Thursday nights from seven to ten when Jesse's parents have
imposed any kind of garage embargo, which has happened twice this
summer.

The lights in the practice space are the small specific kind of
fluorescent that Rick selected in 2009 because they would not, by
their wavelength, interfere with the sleep cycle of anyone whose
night shift began at midnight. Rick has not, since 2009, explained
this selection to anybody. The lights are, by every band that has
used the room, beloved.

### the bathroom

Single occupant. Door has a sticker on the inside that reads, in
Maya's hand: *"if the toilet running is the only sound you can
hear right now, you are okay. — m."* Maya put it there in early
June. Wren put a second sticker beside it in late June: *"and if
it isn't, you are also okay. — w."* The two stickers have not
been removed.

## the inventory of small recurring things

The shop accumulates small specific items the meta-game permits
the player to surface. Highlighted:

### the brass bell

Above the front door. Chimes on every entrance and exit. The bell
has, on careful inspection, a small inscription on its inner rim:
*"for the room. — Adelaide."* Adelaide was the previous shop's
proprietor, the Beaumont bookseller. Adelaide's shop closed in 1994
after she died; her sister handled the estate sale. Rick bought
the bell because he had been visiting Adelaide's shop since he was
fourteen and because the bell, every time he had entered her shop,
had been the small specific welcome he had needed. The inscription
is not visible from below; you have to take the bell down to see
it. Rick has taken the bell down exactly twice — once when he hung
it in 1996, once when he polished it in 2014. Maya, in late July,
took it down to clean a small spider web that had attached to it.
She read the inscription. She put the bell back up. She has not,
since, mentioned it to Rick. The bell is, by the meta-game's
careful accounting, the shop's keystone object.

### the Mr. Coffee

On the file cabinet in the back office. Since 2008. Brewed by Rick
in the morning, by Curtis at noon, by Maya at three, by Wren at
five. Has, in seventeen years, never been replaced. The carafe has
been replaced twice (once by Rick in 2013 when Curtis dropped it,
once by Curtis in 2019 when Curtis dropped it again, which Rick
has not, by tact, mentioned). The filter basket has the small
specific yellow-brown of seventeen years of coffee residue and is,
by Maya's quiet observation, a small artifact in its own right.

### the IBM Wheelwriter 3

Back-office desk. Rick bought it new in 1989 for a journalism
class he was, at the time, taking at the community college. He
never finished the class. He kept the typewriter. It has been on
this desk since 1991. Maya uses it for the Glitch Report drafts
because the typewritten layout matches the original Selectric
editions she is, in publication, honoring. The Wheelwriter's
ribbon has been replaced four times in 2025 — Maya pays for the
ribbons out of her tip jar. Rick has noticed. Rick has not, by
quiet decision, offered to reimburse her. Maya is doing the work.
The ribbons are part of the work.

### the photocopier

Konica Minolta Bizhub C284e. Rick bought it refurbished in 2018
for the small zine print runs he was, at the time, supporting for
local creators. It is the photocopier that prints the Glitch
Report, the Suburban Blight Press Release, and — once, on a Sunday
afternoon in July when nobody was in the shop — a single double-
sided copy of a single page Wren made and immediately took with her
and which Rick has, on the small recorded register Curtis maintains
of the photocopier's monthly usage, noted only as: *"WREN — 1 page
× 2 sides — personal — 0.10 against the running tab."*

The page was, by Wren's careful private acknowledgment to herself,
a letter she had written and could not send. The letter is in a
shoebox under Wren's bed.

### the back-room landline

The 1989 AT&T beige two-line desk phone. The line the gentleman
from Beaumont calls on. The line Rick answers, always, on the
second ring. Curtis knows not to pick up this line. Maya has been
told, by Rick on her first day, that the line "rings sometimes for
a specific person and that person is me — please let me handle
it." Maya has, in four months, not picked up the line. Wren has,
on her one Tuesday in late July when Rick was at lunch, picked it
up. The voice on the other end said: *"Wren. This is a friend of
Rick's. Tell him I called, that the answer is yes, and that I
will see him on the date we discussed. Thank you, kid."* Wren told
Rick. Rick thanked her. Rick has not, since, mentioned the
specific date he had discussed with the voice on the line. Wren
has not, since, asked.

## meta-game integration

Cosmic Comics is the meta-game's primary physical hub for Act II.
The shop's accumulated detritus is, by quantity, vast. Notable
unlock gates:

- **Reading the YA section's mood categories** → unlocks Maya's
  index-card map as an accessible inventory of YA recommendations
  the player can give to other NPCs (Calvin, in early August,
  asks Sam for a YA recommendation; the player can hand him one
  of Maya's, which unlocks a small Calvin interaction)
- **Opening the Henderson Donation boxes in order** → unlocks the
  Glitch Report issues one at a time; the third box contains the
  three rolls of undeveloped 120 film
- **Brewing the Mr. Coffee at all four hours (morning Rick, noon
  Curtis, three Maya, five Wren)** → unlocks the small recognition
  that the staff is the shop's keystone; surfaces a dialogue tree
  where the four staff have a small Friday-evening conversation
  none of them had quite expected
- **Reading the inscription on the brass bell** → unlocks the
  Beaumont 9 archive's first sub-shelf, which is otherwise gated
- **The back-room landline ringing once during play** → if the
  player picks it up, the gentleman from Beaumont's voice
  delivers a single line directed at the player by name; if the
  player does not pick it up, Rick does, and the line is one of
  his nine standard phrases. Either outcome is meaningful.
- **The practice space, on a Tuesday or Thursday at seven** →
  Suburban Blight is there; the player can sit on the green
  velvet couch and listen; the music-player surfaces the band's
  current setlist for that practice as a live in-room recording
  the meta-game permits the player to keep
- **The small grey sedan in the parking lot on the Saturday in
  August** → if the player has previously read Elicia Duchane's
  characterization in the high_priestess.json hooks, Curtis's
  quiet identification surfaces; the meta-game permits the
  player to cross the lot and knock on the sedan's window;
  Elicia rolls the window down only halfway; the conversation
  that follows is reserved for the second half of vol6

The shop is the chapter's safest interior. It is also the
chapter's busiest archive. It is also the building most likely,
by the volume's end, to be quietly under surveillance. Rick has,
by his own private accounting, noticed an unmarked white panel
van parked across Linden three times in the last eleven days,
between 2 AM and 4 AM, with its headlights off. Rick has not, as
of vol6 chapter twelve, told anybody. Rick is the kind of person
who does not tell anybody until he is sure.

He is, by the volume's end, going to be sure.
