# HARMONY CREEK GLITCH REPORT
## a found zine series + Maya Daigle's ongoing archive (vol6)

A three-issue mimeographed/photocopied zine series originally
published in Harmony Creek between **1987 and 1990** by an
anonymous editorial collective, and recovered — issues #1 and #2
of Volume 1 — by **Maya Daigle** in mid-July of the present
chapter, in the Henderson Donation box at Cosmic Comics. Issue #3
is presumed lost. The collective's collaboration ended, by the
zine's own final editorial note, *"in circumstances that the
remaining members have agreed not to put in print."*

Maya, who is sixteen, has decided to extend the series. The
fourth issue of Volume 1 — *Volume 1, Number 4, Summer 2025* — is
in progress on the back-office computer at Cosmic Comics, drafted
in the same single-column typewritten layout the original
collective used, on the same cream-colored paper stock Rick
ordered ten reams of in 1996 and has not yet finished. Maya is
not, in the masthead, naming herself. The masthead reads, in the
collective's original phrasing: *"by the editors."*

## the found issues

### VOLUME 1, NUMBER 1 (Spring 1988)

> **HARMONY CREEK GLITCH REPORT**
> *a quarterly · by the editors · no advertising · no subscription
> · find us in the manila envelopes around town · we will not
> find you.*

Twelve pages. Single column. Typewritten on an IBM Selectric.
Photographs reproduced as halftones via a method the editors
clearly improvised — the halftones are coarse, the contrast is
high, the subjects in the photographs are mostly recognizable
only by their backgrounds. The cover shows the NexCorp™ Spiral
logo in negative — white on black — with a single line of static
laid across it.

The issue's contents, by Maya's careful inventory:

- **Editorial** (p. 1) — *"why we are starting this. there has
  been a quietness in harmony creek estates for the eight years
  since the development opened. the quietness is not, by our
  observation, the quietness of a community at peace. it is the
  quietness of a community whose discomforts are being absorbed
  by something. we have decided to find out what."*
- **The Substation Hum** (pp. 2–4) — the editors' first
  documentation of the NexCorp power-substation hum, with
  amateur frequency analysis (a chart of bars labeled in
  pencil) and a list of streets where the hum is most audible.
  The chart shows a peak at approximately 47 Hz — a number
  Maya will recognize from a different zine, the *Static
  Truths* manuscript that Lena Vargas will be writing in
  Smolvud six weeks from now.
- **Anomalous Radio Interference** (pp. 5–7) — *the issue's
  central feature*. Multiple residents near Zone Gamma
  perimeter report severe AM/FM interference, particularly
  during evening hours (19:00–04:00 CST). Standard broadcasts
  replaced by bursts of white noise, number station-like
  sequences ("Appendix A: Transcript Excerpt — *Nine. Locust.
  Seventeen. Door.*"), and what one resident describes as
  *"Cumbia music played by angry ghosts underwater."*
- **HOA Compliance Patterns** (pp. 8–9) — a small data set on
  citation patterns, broken down by lot, suggesting that the
  HOA's "Non-Standard Lawn Ornamentation" citations are issued
  with a higher rate of incidence on lots whose residents have,
  separately, complained about the substation hum.
- **A Walk** (pp. 10–11) — *signed by E.A.* — a first-person
  prose piece describing a walk taken at night, alone, along
  the perimeter of Zone Gamma. The piece is approximately 800
  words. The piece ends with the writer turning back at the
  southeast corner of the fence and not, since, having walked
  that route again.
- **Letters to the Editors** (p. 12) — three letters; one from
  *"a concerned neighbor"* about the lawnmower-sound ordinance;
  one from *"R.B. in New Auburn"* asking whether the editors
  intend to publish in other parts of Harmony County; one
  anonymous, single line: *"please be careful."*

### VOLUME 1, NUMBER 2 (Winter 1988)

> **HARMONY CREEK GLITCH REPORT · Vol. 1, No. 2 · Winter '88**

Sixteen pages. The issue Maya found first, in the Henderson
Donation box, in mid-July. The cover shows the NexCorp™ Spiral
glitched out, dissolving into TV static. The editors have, by
this issue, taken on a small sardonic confidence the first issue
did not have. They have also published the first piece of news
that the meta-game's careful reader will recognize as load-
bearing.

Selected contents:

- **Editorial** — *"we received fourteen letters in response to
  issue number one. we have read all fourteen. we are
  publishing four. we have, separately, become aware that one
  of the fourteen was written by an employee of NexCorp™
  Residential Solutions in a personal capacity. we are not
  publishing that one. we are not, by editorial principle,
  refusing to publish materials from NexCorp™ employees; we
  are refusing to publish materials that would, by their
  appearance in this zine, get a specific NexCorp™ employee
  fired. we hope the writer understands. — the editors."*
- **Anomalous Radio Interference Patterns (cont'd)** (pp.
  2–5) — *the report Maya quotes from in vol6's chapter
  thirteen.* Multiple residents near Zone Gamma perimeter
  continue to report interference. The article includes the
  transcript excerpt that becomes a recurring motif across
  the volume: *"Nine. Locust. Seventeen. Door."* NexCorp™
  official response continues to attribute phenomena to
  "unshielded transformer resonance." The editors note,
  drily: *"transformers don't usually whisper your name."*
- **Disappearance — Henderson, M.** (p. 6) — *the chapter's
  load-bearing find.* Margaret Henderson, sixteen, aspiring
  photographer, vanished on December 12, 1988, after telling
  friends she was going to capture *"weird light effects"*
  near Zone Gamma fence line. Police report cites "runaway."
  Neighbors report seeing an unfamiliar black van (no
  plates, NexCorp™ Logistics logo?) near the Henderson
  residence the evening prior. The editors close: *"Connection?
  Maybe. Maybe it's just Tuesday in Harmony Creek. Keep yr
  eyes open. Stay unplugged."*
- **The Editors' Question** (pp. 7–8) — a single page asking,
  in seventeen short paragraphs, *what NexCorp Residential
  Solutions actually does.* The page is the most direct piece
  of writing in the zine. The page has been, on Maya's copy,
  underlined and starred in a hand that is not the editors'.
- **Map of Reported Anomalies** (pp. 9–11) — a foldout. A
  hand-drawn map of Harmony Creek Estates, with thirty-one
  numbered locations and a small legend identifying each as
  *"resident-reported interference,"* *"resident-reported
  light anomaly,"* *"resident-reported equipment failure,"*
  or *"resident-reported visitation by unmarked vehicle."*
  The map's eastern boundary terminates at Zone Gamma's
  perimeter fence. The map's western boundary terminates at
  the NexCorp™ Residential Solutions regional office on
  Magnolia Court. The numbered locations form, on close
  inspection, a slow concentric drift inward from the
  perimeter toward the substations. Maya, the first time
  she unfolded the map, sat with it open on the floor for
  the better part of an hour.
- **A Walk (II)** (pp. 12–13) — *signed by E.A. again.* The
  same writer. A second night-walk. A different stretch of
  the perimeter. The piece is shorter than the first. The
  piece's final paragraph reads: *"I do not yet know what is
  in the field beyond the fence. I do know that whatever is
  in the field has noticed me. I am not, on the next walk,
  going to be alone."*
- **In Memoriam — Margaret Henderson** (p. 14) — a single
  paragraph. A small grainy halftone of a sixteen-year-old
  girl holding a camera. The caption: *"Margaret was a member
  of the Harmony Creek Camera Club. She had been planning to
  contribute to this zine. We had not, before her
  disappearance, met her in person. We had only her letters.
  We will print one of them next issue, with her family's
  permission."*
- **Classifieds** (pp. 15–16) — small ads in the back: a
  1991 Ford Bronco for sale (low miles, runs well, garaged,
  phone number); a sewing-machine repair service in New
  Auburn; an offer of free clarinet lessons in exchange for
  yard work; a single anonymous ad reading only: *"if you
  know who is in the field, please write to the editors. we
  will protect your identity."*

### VOLUME 1, NUMBER 3 (Spring/Summer 1989)

**Missing.** No copy in the Henderson Donation box. No copy in
Rick's back-room zine archive. No copy in the Cosmic Comics
inventory. No copy in the Houma library, which Maya checked.
No copy in the small private collection of one of the original
editors that Maya, by careful correspondence, located in
August 2025 and asked about.

The editor — who declined to be named in Maya's email exchange
but who signed off as *"E.A., still walking"* — replied with
the following:

> *"Maya. I do not have a copy of Volume 1, Number 3. I do not
> believe any copy exists. We printed thirty-eight copies. We
> distributed twelve. We left the remaining twenty-six in the
> shed at the back of the property where we did the printing.
> The shed burned down on June 11, 1989. The fire was, by the
> Harmony Creek Fire Department's investigation, electrical.
> The fire was, by our private understanding, not. We did not
> publish a Volume 1, Number 4. We did not, after that summer,
> remain in regular contact with each other. Three of us still
> live in Harmony Creek County. Two have moved away. One we
> have lost. We have, separately, in the years since, thought
> about putting a fourth issue out. We have decided, separately,
> not to.*
>
> *I am very glad you have decided to.*
>
> *— E.A."*

Maya printed the email at Cosmic Comics. She put the printout in
the same folder where she keeps the F.T. correspondence. She has
not, since printing it, told anyone about the email.

## Volume 1, Number 4 (in progress, summer 2025)

Maya's continuation. Eighteen pages, by current draft count. The
content tracks the editorial mandate of the original collective
but moves forward in time by 36 years. She is writing — in the
same single-column Selectric-style typewritten layout the
originals used, which she has approximated on Rick's IBM Wheelwriter
3 in the back office — about the present-day disappearances, the
present-day Spiral-logo vans, the present-day NexCorp
infrastructure that has, since 1988, learned to be quieter. She
is also writing about Diego.

The issue's working table of contents:

- **Editorial: From the New Editors** *(she means "from the new
  editor," but the byline conceit is the original collective's,
  and she has decided to honor it. She is, by editorial decision,
  pretending to be plural.)*
- **The Substation Hum (cont'd, 36 years later)** — a small
  update including her grandmother's vacuuming pattern, the
  4/4 beat that Connie does not, when asked, know she is doing.
- **Anomalous Radio Interference (cont'd)** — the static-Cumbia
  pattern is the same pattern. The number-station sequence is
  the same sequence. *Nine. Locust. Seventeen. Door.*
- **The Spiral Vans (1988 → 2025)** — a side-by-side comparison
  of the 1988 editors' "unmarked black van (no plates, NexCorp™
  Logistics logo?)" with Diego's 2025 sightings of "the black
  van with the NexCorp Spiral logo." Maya's careful inference:
  the vans are the same vans. The logo has been quietly
  rebranded. The route is the same route.
- **A Walk (III)** — Maya's own night-walk, in early August,
  alone, along the same stretch of the perimeter E.A. walked in
  1988. The piece is the longest in the issue. The piece ends
  with Maya turning back at the southeast corner of the fence,
  which is the same corner E.A. turned back at in Issue #1, and
  which Maya did not know was the same corner until she
  re-read Issue #1 on her way home.
- **In Memoriam — Margaret Henderson (37 years later)** — a
  letter Margaret had written to the editors in late 1988, which
  the original collective had announced they would publish in
  Issue #3 with her family's permission, and which Maya has
  recovered — via E.A.'s small correspondence — and is, with
  Margaret's surviving sister's permission, finally publishing.
  The letter is two pages long. The letter describes the light
  effects Margaret had been photographing near Zone Gamma. The
  letter ends with the sentence: *"I think there is something
  in the field that wants to be photographed. I am going to let
  it."*
- **A Letter from D.R.** — a fictional letter Maya has drafted
  as if from Diego. The letter is the issue's most reckless
  decision. Maya has not yet decided whether to publish it. The
  letter is, in current draft, six paragraphs long. The letter
  is, on its face, addressed to *"the editors."* The letter is,
  in fact, addressed to whoever has taken Diego — a small
  careful act of editorial bait. Maya does not know whether the
  bait will land. She is also not sure she should set it. As of
  mid-August she is asking Rick, after work hours, what he
  would do.
- **Classifieds** — Maya is leaving the classifieds page open
  for resident submissions, in the original collective's style.
  She has, so far, received one: a small advertisement from
  Carla Vega at lot 47, offering to provide audio recordings of
  the substation hum to any interested party for the cost of a
  blank cassette. *(Carla is canonical only as resident of lot
  47 in the prior project files; she is a vol6 reserved figure;
  Maya's receipt of her letter is, by Maya's quiet decision, the
  first contact the canonical layer has made with Carla.)*

## the Henderson Donation box itself

The cardboard box Maya found Issue #2 in is itself a recurring
object the meta-game permits the player to revisit. The box is
labeled, in Rick's handwriting, **"HENDERSON DONATION — FILE
UNDER 'POSSIBLY INTERESTING DETRITUS'?"**.

The box arrived at Cosmic Comics in 2017, when **Mr. Henderson
Sr.** — Jesse's father, then five years into his camera-collecting
phase but not yet into the optical-implant phase — periodically
cleared out the basement and brought boxes of his late mother's
things to the shop. He had asked Rick to take them off his hands.
Rick had agreed without examining the box. The box had sat,
unopened, on the back-room shelf labeled MISC. UNSORTED for eight
years.

When Maya opened it in July she found, in order from top to
bottom:

- a small stack of 1970s comic-book promotional posters
- a brittle copy of *The Whole Earth Catalog* (1972)
- three rolls of undeveloped 120 film in their original canisters
  *(Maya has not, at the time of vol6, developed these. They sit
  in the second drawer of the back-office desk. They are, by the
  meta-game's eventual reveal, photographs taken by Margaret
  Henderson in the autumn of 1988.)*
- the two issues of HARMONY CREEK GLITCH REPORT
- a folded piece of letter paper, in a hand Maya did not, at
  first, recognize, that reads: *"Rick — I am sorry I did not
  tell you about Margaret before now. I am sorry I did not look
  for her. I am sorry I did not, when the camera club asked, go
  to the field with them. I have been sorry for thirty-five
  years. I do not know what to do with the sorry. I have
  decided that the box is what I am doing. — H.H."* Maya
  recognized the H.H. handwriting later, on the back of a
  photograph in Mr. Henderson Sr.'s study, when she was at
  Jesse's house in mid-August and Mr. Henderson had stepped out
  of the room. H.H. was Mr. Henderson Sr.'s father — Margaret's
  older brother — Jesse's grandfather. Margaret was Jesse's
  great-aunt. Jesse, by Maya's quiet decision, does not yet
  know this, and Maya has not yet decided whether to tell him.

## meta-game integration

The Glitch Report functions in the meta-game as the **historical
substrate** for everything Maya and Sam discover. Reading the
issues in order:

- **Issue #1** → unlocks the substation-hum frequency mapping
  on the gallery's map of Harmony Creek (47 Hz baseline) and
  surfaces the 1988 lot-citation pattern as a comparable layer
  to NexCorp's present-day resident-payment scheme
- **Issue #2** → unlocks Margaret Henderson as a named historical
  figure across the deck, populates the personnel graph's
  "predecessors" panel, and unlocks the fold-out map's 31
  numbered anomaly locations as clickable points the player can
  cross-reference with present-day Diego sightings, Sam's
  license-plate observations, and Jesse's hand-drawn map of
  active strip-mall units
- **The E.A. email** → unlocks the dormant chapter of the
  original collective's dispersal; the meta-game permits the
  player to write back to E.A. through Cosmic Comics' postal
  address; E.A. responds, eventually
- **Volume 1, Number 4 (in progress)** → unlocks Maya as the
  primary editorial author of vol6's documentary spine. The
  player can read each section of #4 as Maya drafts it across
  the volume, including the small key moment in late August
  when Maya decides — or does not — to publish the letter from
  "D.R."
- **The three rolls of undeveloped 120 film** → unlocks, when
  developed by the player via a small Cosmic Comics back-office
  interaction, Margaret Henderson's final photographs. The
  photographs are: nine images of light effects on the field
  beyond the perimeter fence; three images of an unmarked black
  van on Magnolia Court; one image, the last on the roll, of a
  man Margaret photographed approaching her from across the
  field. The man's face is not in clear focus. The man's hand
  is. The hand is the watch-tan wrist that appears in vol6's
  later Polaroids from Diego's archive. The hand has not aged
  in thirty-seven years.

The Glitch Report is the volume's quietest argument that the
chapter has been the same chapter for a very long time, and
that the editors who started it in 1988 and the editor who has
inherited it in 2025 are, in some sense the deck respects
without rendering explicit, the same editors.
