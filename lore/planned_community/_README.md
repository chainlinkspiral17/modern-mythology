# PLANNED COMMUNITY · accumulation files

Per-character documents describing the zines, comix, mixtapes, and
detritus of suburban entertainment accumulation belonging to the
primary vol6 cast. Each file lays out the physical artifacts the
meta-game surfaces — track listings, page-by-page zine contents,
panel-by-panel comix descriptions, junk-drawer inventories — and
the gallery / music-player unlock conditions that gate them.

## the artifact registers

Across the five files, the same five categories of accumulation appear
for each character — though the proportions differ. Some characters
make zines. Some only buy them. Some make comix. Some keep them. The
meta-game's L3 personnel-graph lights up when artifacts from different
characters cross-reference.

### I. ZINES
Photocopied, stapled, hand-distributed. Sold by hand at Cosmic Comics
or through the manila envelope at the Kwik Stop. Half-letter and
quarter-letter sizes. Cosmic Comics' photocopier paper stock is
recognizable across the scene — any zine made on it has been printed
at Rick's machine, which the player can use as an authorship clue.

### II. COMIX
Underground-comix register. Pseudonymous attribution is common.
The Cosmic Comics back counter holds inventory. Maya's *GENERAL STORE*
is the most ambitious project; Diego's *PHASE III* is the most prescient;
Rick's *THE BEAUMONT BARGAINER* is the most carefully unfinished.

### III. MIXTAPES
Maxell XLII cassettes, 90 minutes. Case inserts hand-cut from various
papers. Track lists in careful block print. Side A and Side B
distinctions matter. Several mixtapes share specific tracks — the
unreleased fourth-record song from the Gallatin Band appears on three
separate mixes (Diego's last, Maya's "for dad," Sam's "for the 3 PM
shift"), establishing the band's bassist as a quiet network.

### IV. DETRITUS — *the suburban entertainment accumulation*
Junk drawers, closet boxes, locker contents, corkboards, the wire
basket of left-behind objects at the Kwik Stop register. Receipts,
ticket stubs, drumsticks, Polaroids, ceramic frogs, lottery tickets,
loyalty cards, butcher-paper maps, brown manila envelopes.

The **ceramic frog** is the chapter's quiet recurring object. Marble-
sized, intact, green. Sam has one. Diego has one. Maya has one. Skip
has one. Rick has Dean's. The chapter accumulates ninety-seven frogs
across the deck — the medium has been distributing them for forty
years.

### V. THE CHAPTER-SPECIFIC OBJECT
Each character has one or two objects that don't fit the standard
categories — Rick's phone ledger, Skip's 1999 mixtape never given,
Maya's grandfather's funeral-parlor inkwell, Diego's eleven NexCorp
badge clips, Sam's license-plate notebook. These are the items the
meta-game's L4 layer treats as keystones.

## the music-player meta-game integration

When the player listens to a track in the Music Room that references
items in these files (e.g., the Gallatin Band's *"Sammy"* — track 7 on
Diego's first mixtape for Sam), the music player surfaces a small
inline note: *"references → sam_miller.md / mixtape side A."*

The reverse is also true: opening an artifact in the gallery surfaces
the relevant music-player track at the bottom of the artifact's
description card, with a *"play this"* button that queues the track.

The meta-game's central puzzle is: identify, by cross-referencing the
artifacts and the music, the chain of custody that connects the
characters, the medium, NexCorp, and the building on the east side
of Phase III where Diego is being held.

## tone

The artifacts are suburban Gulf-Coast in the careful Lynch-meets-zine-
scene register: detailed, slightly funny, slightly unsettling, often
sincere, always specific. The objects are *real* in the chapter's
internal economy — the meta-game does not handwave the materiality.
The wire basket under the Kwik Stop register contains, by Sam's
inventory, exactly seven items, each with its own provenance, each
with its own narrative weight.

The chapter has been held.
