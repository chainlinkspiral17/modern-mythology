# COMMUNITY PLANNED · Weekly Scenarios

Third sibling doc alongside `_COMMUNITY_PLANNED_SPEC.md` (mechanics)
and `_COMMUNITY_PLANNED_LORE.md` (story). This doc plots the
summer week by week — the strategic events, the BBS focal points,
the mechanics introduced or escalated, the named cast moves, and
the arc each week serves.

The summer is fourteen weeks. Memorial Day weekend to Labor Day.
Six quiet strategic days and one long BBS night per week. The
mechanics layer in across the summer; the player who closes the
BBS at Labor Day has, by then, learned to read a complete board.

## The summer's overall arc

A four-act shape, played in fourteen weeks:

- **Act I · Strategy (W1-6).** The player learns the loop. The
  network speaks. The first mechanics — dispatch, BBS reading, DMs,
  hidden boards, tilt detection, agent refusal — are introduced
  one per week. Stakes are low. Losses are recoverable. The summer
  feels manageable.
- **Act II · Horror (W7-9).** The substrate flickers. The first
  demon turns. A migrant family commits. The conversion mechanic
  becomes the thing the player is actually playing against. The
  summer's worst losses fall in this stretch.
- **Act III · Faith (W10-13).** The Cathedral Letters mechanic
  surfaces. PALOMINO returns. Mrs. Pimentel chooses. The
  Vargas-Quintana family is delivered. Frasier sees her. The
  spiritual undercurrent that has been quiet all summer becomes,
  briefly, audible.
- **Act IV · Benediction (W14).** Labor Day. The interlude shelf
  binds. The BBS closes. *End of Summer Begins* in the book is
  now available to turn to.

## Mechanic introduction schedule

One new mechanic or escalation per week, until the end-game
trusts what has been built.

| W  | Mechanic introduced / escalated                            |
|----|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1  | Basic dispatch · basic BBS reading · agent + demon roster   |
| 2  | DM threads · DM reply choices · demon burn accrual          |
| 3  | Hidden board discovery (dial-up clues) · POV unlocks        |
| 4  | TILT DETECTION — reading the BBS for register, not content  |
| 5  | SLOW WITNESS — Mackenzie's dispatch yields archive, not gain|
| 6  | AGENT REFUSAL — override / accept / rewrite                 |
| 7  | SUBSTRATE FLICKER — tower-state events shift strategic rules|
| 8  | MIGRANT BRANCHING — named families' fates branch            |
| 9  | DEMON CONVERSION COMPLETE — first agent lost to the other   |
| 10 | THE CATHEDRAL LETTERS — workbench-presence as a currency    |
| 11 | CIRCLE VOCABULARY — the sysop coded language unlocks legibly|
| 12 | ENDGAME PUSH — rapid simultaneous deliveries, fewer turns   |
| 13 | THE SIGHTING — one on-screen Aria moment                    |
| 14 | BINDING — the interlude shelf assembles in real-time        |

By the end of W6 the player can run the full strategic loop. From
W7 onward the mechanics either elaborate existing systems or are
single-use special events.

## The weeks

### Week 1 · Memorial Day & First Light

*Theme:* the prologue. The summer's pieces are placed.

**Strategic events:**

- Sunday (Memorial Day): the boat's Friday dinner is cancelled
  because Sammy is at his sister's funeral. Graustark feels the
  small absence. The player has nothing to do with this event;
  it sets the tonal floor.
- Tuesday: NexCorp brochures arrive at three Graustark addresses
  Frasier is watching, including Mrs. Theresa Boudreaux's.
- Thursday: Carl Reno hosts the HCE Phase I welcome breakfast.
  The guest list has one new name on it — Lillian Mercer, who is
  scouting.
- Friday: Hal Mercer's auto-parts shop on the Graustark riverfront
  has a customer pay in cash whose origin Frasier (via the
  cicada) traces to an HCE buyer's deposit. Hal does not know.

**BBS night:** mostly MAINSTREET and THE_BAR. STEEPLE posts one
short check-in. WIRE_MOTHER posts a crawfish-boil recipe that is
also, by careful read, an inventory of which boards are safe this
summer. No DM replies yet.

**Mechanic:** the player learns the strategic loop — survey,
dispatch, end-day. The BBS is read-only this week.

**Cast moves:** Mrs. Boudreaux receives the brochure; the Mercers
introduced; Carl Reno introduced.

### Week 2 · The First DMs

*Theme:* the network speaks.

**Strategic events:**

- Monday: Elicia DMs Frasier on the BBS for the first time this
  summer. She has a tape she'd like him to listen to.
- Wednesday: Dante DMs. He's been talking about closing the boat
  again. He's not yet ready, but the conversation is now on the
  thread.
- Thursday: A Graustark problem escalates — the cathedral's
  electrical bill is overdue. Frasier's own node; he has to
  dispatch the moth to handle it cleanly (or pay it himself, in
  sanity).
- Saturday: First demon dispatch payoff — the vagrant returns from
  the riverfront with a piece of mail (a NexCorp brochure stripped
  from a wall by the egret nest). The brochure has a hand-written
  number on its back that the player can dial later.

**BBS night:** first substantial DM exchange. Frasier replies to
Elicia (one-sentence choice). PALOMINO posts about a thunderstorm
in Santa Fe; the patient reader catches the second layer.

**Mechanic:** DM threads + reply choices. Demon burn accrual
becomes legible.

**Cast moves:** Elicia speaks. Dante speaks. The Boudreaux thread
continues on MAINSTREET (she's posting about the brochures now,
in a register the player can register but not yet read).

### Week 3 · The Hidden Door

*Theme:* the surface of the BBS is just the surface.

**Strategic events:**

board. POV unlock: a letter to the dead from someone who signs
themselves "S" — Sammy, the patient reader will eventually

**Mechanic:** hidden board discovery via dial-up clues + POV
document unlocks on the shelf.

**Cast moves:** Carl Reno fully introduced; Lillian Mercer's first
HCE breakfast attended; Otis Vandermeer's first dispatch lands;
Maya's first letter.

### Week 4 · The First Tilt

*Theme:* the conversion becomes visible.

**Strategic events:**

- Tuesday: a demon's BBS post reads slightly off. THE_HEAT (the
  cicada) posts about Harmony Creek Phase II in word choices that
  have a half-degree of NexCorp register. Frasier registers it.
  The cicada's tilt is at 2. The player learns to read tilt.
- Wednesday: Skip Donnelly drives the NexCorp route. Frasier knows
  where he is. Frasier does not dispatch anyone to talk to him
  this week.
- Thursday: an HCE problem escalates. One of Paul's two HOA
  candidate proxies makes a small unforced move that suggests his
  tilt is rising fast. Norman Lott has been seen at the
  candidate's house.
- Friday: Diego Ramos goes missing for the first time this summer.
  (Canon-locked from *Planned Community*.) The Ramos household
  begins its quiet panic. Frasier reads the news Saturday morning
  from MAINSTREET.

**BBS night:** the player reads the cicada's post carefully and
decides whether to intervene (counter-exposure work, costing one
demon-week Frasier doesn't have to spare) or let it ride. STEEPLE
has posted about a similar drift in his own roster — they compare
notes.

**Mechanic:** TILT DETECTION. The player begins reading the BBS
not just for content but for *register*. Half-degree tilts surface
as subtle textual differences. A tooltip system reveals the tilt
rating for any text the player highlights and *manually inspects*
— not before. Reading the room is the new skill.

**Cast moves:** Skip introduced via his route; Norman Lott
introduced; Diego goes missing (canonical); the cicada's tilt is
the first the player explicitly tracks.

### Week 5 · The Loom Knows

*Theme:* not all dispatch is strategic. Some is testimony.

**Strategic events:**

- Monday: Frasier asks Mackenzie to spend a week in Harmony Creek.
  She agrees. She drives over Wednesday morning.
- Wednesday-Friday: Mackenzie's dispatch — no strategic effect.
  The player sees her on the board but cannot direct her. She is
  *registering*.
- Saturday: Mackenzie returns. The loom in her studio has, by

  The player will see this only at Labor Day in the Migration

**BBS night:** Mackenzie's DM thread updates with a single
sentence: *"the basil pot at the model home is fake."* Frasier
and the player both register the implication. The strategic layer
now has a flagged HCE node: the model home at Lot 47. (Carla
Vega's real basil pot is, three doors down, in her kitchen.)

**Mechanic:** SLOW WITNESS. Mackenzie's dispatch yields not
strategic gain but archive-quality information that surfaces in
the interlude shelf. Players learn that some dispatches *pay out
much later*.

**Cast moves:** Mackenzie dispatched for the first time; Carla
Vega introduced (lives next door to the model home); the model
home flagged as a Dean infrastructure node.

### Week 6 · Filly Refuses

*Theme:* the network is collaborators with their own ideas about
the work.

**Strategic events:**

- Monday: Frasier dispatches Filly to carry a sealed letter from
  Nicola to Mrs. Theresa Boudreaux. Filly is asked to deliver it
  without being seen.
- Tuesday: Filly does not arrive at the dispatch point. Filly
  posts to the BBS instead, on a board Frasier didn't know Filly
  used.
- Wednesday: Filly explains, in three short paragraphs, that the
  letter's contents will harm Mrs. Boudreaux's grandchildren even
  if it reaches her cleanly. Filly is refusing.
- **Player's choice:**
  - **Override.** Filly goes. The letter is delivered. The cost is
    real: Filly's obligation-to-Frasier rises sharply; future
    Filly dispatches will be refused more readily.
  - **Accept refusal.** The letter sits. Nicola's authority
    dispatch goes unsent this week. Mrs. Boudreaux receives no
    correspondence; her HCE decision proceeds unmodified.
  - **Rewrite the letter.** Nicola revises. The week is consumed
    in the revision. Frasier loses one sanity. Filly delivers
    the rewrite next Monday.
- Friday: whichever path the player chose, a small ripple
  registers on the HCE map. Carl Reno cancels his Saturday
  breakfast for no stated reason.

**BBS night:** THE_BACKCHANNEL. WIRE_MOTHER posts a long thread
about her own filly equivalent from a decade ago. PALOMINO posts
cryptically: *"the colt has its own field now."*

**Mechanic:** AGENT REFUSAL. Demons (and humans, on certain
dispatch types) can refuse. The choice has lasting ledger
consequences. The Refusal Suite begins binding entries.

**Cast moves:** Filly's voice introduced; Nicola's authority
dispatch challenged; Carl Reno cancels (the *why* is held).

### Week 7 · The Substrate Flickers

*Theme:* the world is not stable.

**Strategic events:**

- Tuesday morning: the tower brightness chart, which the player
  has been able to read since W4, goes **dim** for the first time
  this summer. Three days of dim follow.
- Tuesday-Thursday: during the dim window the strategic rules
  shift. Problem escalation clocks slow. Demon corruption rates
  spike. Frasier's dispatches sometimes arrive at the wrong space
  (or arrive *and report from* a space they were not sent to).
- Friday: the tower comes back **bright**. Normal rules resume.
  Demons dispatched during the dim window are tilted up by 1 each
  — a hidden cost the player will discover by reading their posts
  carefully over the next two weeks.
- Saturday: Otis Vandermeer reports from Small Wood that one of
  the local NexCorp surveying flags has, this week, *moved on
  its own*.

**BBS night:** PALOMINO has gone silent. Three weeks of silence
begin. WIRE_MOTHER and STEEPLE both post about it on
THE_BACKCHANNEL without naming her directly. THE_QUARRY posts a
single question: *"did anyone hear it last night?"*

**Mechanic:** SUBSTRATE FLICKER. Tower-state events alter strategic
rules for one week at a time. The player learns to read brightness
as a forecast. The Tower Watch suite begins binding entries.

**Cast moves:** PALOMINO's silence begins; Otis sees the flag
move; Carla Vega's 47 Hz recordings spike (audible faintly through
one BBS thread Frasier reads).

### Week 8 · The Mercer Decision

*Theme:* a migrant family commits.

**Strategic events:**

- Monday: Hal Mercer's auto-parts shop has a customer pay him in
  cash that came from an HCE buyer's deposit. Hal does not know
  this; Frasier does (the cicada reported it pre-tilt-rise).
- Wednesday: Lillian Mercer has signed paperwork with Diane Pell.
  The Mercers will be moving to HCE Phase II by mid-August. Bea
  Mercer doesn't know yet.
- Thursday: Bea Mercer reads a flyer at her school for a summer
  photography workshop in Lincoln City, OR. The flyer is in her
  hand by the end of the day. Frasier (via Otis Vandermeer's
  Smolvud network) ensured the flyer was at her school.
- Friday: **Player's branching choice:**
  - **Counter-move via Nicola's letter.** Lillian receives a
    handwritten letter from Nicola about the *succession house* in
    Graustark. Lillian considers staying. Hal may or may not
    accept.
  - **Counter-move via Maya's mail-drop.** Maya, who has met Bea
    once at Cosmic Comics, sends a postcard. Bea writes back. A
    small correspondence begins. Bea's Oregon plan strengthens
    independent of her parents.
  - **Let them go.** The Mercers move to HCE. Bea later extracts
    to Oregon on her own three days after the move.
  - **Active block.** Frasier dispatches the moth to subtly
    disrupt the Pell paperwork. (Costs the moth one week. The
    moth is dispatched this week regardless of next week's
    needs.)

**BBS night:** Maya Daigle's letter to F.T. arrives — and this
one mentions, in passing, *"a girl named Bea I met at the comix
counter."* Frasier reads twice. Decides.

**Mechanic:** MIGRANT BRANCHING. Player choices on individual
migrants branch their fates. Each branch yields a different
Migration Suite vignette at Labor Day.

**Cast moves:** The Mercers' decision week; Diane Pell on the
strategic map; Bea Mercer becomes a named figure for the player;
Maya's correspondence becomes load-bearing.

### Week 9 · A Demon Turns

*Theme:* the loss the player will remember.

**Strategic events:**

- Monday: Frasier dispatches the moth to Small Wood to disrupt a
  planning meeting Dean's network has called about the timber
  mill closures. (Canon-adjacent: the mill closures are a
  Dean-network engineered event.)
- Wednesday: The moth arrives. The meeting is not disrupted. The
  moth posts to its usual board that it succeeded. The cicada,
  having dispatched a side-watch unprompted, tells Frasier on a
  private board that the post's register is wrong.
- Thursday: The moth has not, in fact, succeeded. The moth has
  been *turned*. Frasier confirms by dispatching the vagrant to
  the same town for cross-check. Vagrant reports back: the moth
  has been seen on the timber-mill catwalk three nights in a row,
  talking to Vance Holcombe.
- Friday: Frasier loses the moth. It does not come home. The
  moth's BBS handle stops posting except in ways that read
  entirely normal — Frasier can no longer trust the moth's posts.
- Saturday: a Small Wood plant attempt that depended on the moth's
  intel falls apart. The room above Wagner's Hardware is, by
  Sunday, no longer Frasier's — Vance has terminated the lease.

**BBS night:** Frasier writes one line on the demon-rest board
(THE_BASEMENT) — the last post he will write to the moth. The
player gets to read it. The Conversion Suite gains its first
entry, written in the moth's voice, post-turn: a paragraph that
sounds like the moth and is also exactly wrong.

**Mechanic:** DEMON CONVERSION COMPLETE. First time the player
loses an agent to the other side. The strategic map updates to
show the moth as an *enemy agent under its original name*.

**Cast moves:** Vance Holcombe steps into the open as Dean's
Small Wood handler; the Wagner's Hardware lease ends; Otis arranges
an emergency replacement room above Sister Kell's mission garage.

### Week 10 · The Letters

*Theme:* the spiritual undercurrent surfaces.

**Strategic events:**

- Monday: Maya Daigle's letter arrives. It contains a small
  drawing of a girl in a yellow rainslicker at a bus stop, blue
  curls visible. Maya did not see this girl in person; she
  *dreamed* her. First Aria sighting through a third party in the
  game.
- Tuesday: Frasier reads the drawing. Spends the next two days at
  the workbench without dispatching anyone.
- Thursday: A Cathedral Letter entry the player will only see at
  Labor Day is composed. (Mechanically: the entry exists in the
  gallery shelf at the end *if Frasier was present at the
  workbench Tuesday and Wednesday*.)
- Friday: Skip Donnelly drives the NexCorp route. He notices, this
  week, that his manifest is one item short. He does not mention
  it. Frasier knows (via the cicada). Frasier waits.
- Saturday: Nicola's letter to Mrs. Boudreaux arrives at Mrs.
  Boudreaux's address. Mrs. Boudreaux reads it twice. The
  Boudreaux decision is now made; the player learns it Monday.

**BBS night:** a letter on THE_LIBRARY board from Elicia
containing a recording of the bungalow at dusk on a Tuesday. The
recording is, by itself, just cicadas and a screen door. The
patient reader hears, three minutes in, a very faint voice saying
a single word. Elicia does not comment on the recording.

**Mechanic:** THE CATHEDRAL LETTERS. The player learns that
certain Cathedral Letters entries are gated on Frasier being
*present at the workbench* on specific days (not dispatched, not
on BBS, not in transit). Time-at-the-workbench becomes a currency.
The Cathedral Letters suite begins accumulating entries the player
will only see at Labor Day.

**Cast moves:** Aria's first in-game sighting (third-party,
through Maya's drawing); Skip notices his short manifest; Mrs.
Boudreaux decides (revealed Monday W11); Elicia's recording
surfaces.

### Week 11 · Mrs. Pimentel

*Theme:* the faith layer becomes audible.

**Strategic events:**

- Monday: PALOMINO posts for the first time in three weeks. The
  post is short, in vocabulary Frasier has been parsing for years.
  The player will not understand it on first read. It is on
  THE_BACKCHANNEL, dated August 4. Read carefully, it is about
  Aria.
- Tuesday: Mrs. Pimentel, on the HCE welcome committee, refuses
  to deliver a specific welcome basket to a specific new resident.
  Carl Reno notices. Mrs. Pimentel does not explain.
- Wednesday: Carl Reno reports Mrs. Pimentel to NexCorp Residential
  Solutions. The report goes nowhere; she's a volunteer and she's
  old and the welcome committee can absorb one strange Tuesday.
- Thursday: Mrs. Pimentel mails a wax-paper packet of cookies to
  the new resident she didn't welcome. The packet contains, taped
  inside the wax paper, a note. The note says: *"leave by Friday.
  tell no one. the road north is open."*
- Friday: the new resident — a young woman traveling alone,
  unnamed in this game, **not on Frasier's board until now** —
  leaves HCE for the Oregon coast that night. Frasier learns
  about her existence only by Saturday morning.
- Saturday: Mrs. Pimentel's tilt rating, still hidden from the
  player, holds at 4. She has refused enough instructions over a
  long enough period that her arc is, at the game's window,
  *recoverable*. The player can see, in retrospect, that she has
  been quietly resisting all summer.

**BBS night:** PALOMINO's August 4 post is read closely. WIRE_MOTHER
replies, also coded, also about Aria. STEEPLE adds a single
sentence. THE_QUARRY does not post. Maya Daigle's letter this week
says, in passing: *"I think I've seen her again."*

**Mechanic:** CIRCLE VOCABULARY. The sysop circle's coded language
for Aria becomes legible to the player who has been paying
attention. A small in-game *glossary* unlocks (showing the patient
reader how to parse the prior weeks' posts). Re-reading earlier
threads with the glossary changes what the player thought they
were reading.

**Cast moves:** PALOMINO speaks; Mrs. Pimentel's most surprising
move surfaces; a new migrant (unnamed, off-board) reaches Oregon
because of her — a delivery Frasier did not make.

### Week 12 · The Vargas-Quintana Push

*Theme:* the summer's most important delivery.

**Strategic events:**

- Monday: the Vargas-Quintana family receives, in the same week, a
  NexCorp brochure (Dean's pressure) and an unsigned note from a
  Beaumont post-office box (Frasier's network) suggesting an
  alternative route to the Oregon coast. They are now choosing.
- Tuesday: Carmen Vargas calls Connie Daigle (Maya's grandmother).
  Connie, who has been receiving NexCorp envelopes for years,
  tells Carmen she is, this week, *returning all of them
  unopened*. (Connie's own choice. The player does not direct it.)
- Wednesday: Carmen and Esteban decide. They will go to the coast.
- Thursday: the drive begins. Three days, two states, one rest
  stop in Boise where they pass a young woman in a yellow hoodie
  at the gas station who they do not register seeing.
- Saturday: they arrive in Smolvud. They check into a motel
  Petra's mother holds a back-room key for. Lena, their young
  daughter, is asleep in the back seat.
- Sunday: the summer's most important delivery is complete. The
  player does not, until Labor Day, know its full significance.

**BBS night:** a short MAINSTREET thread about gas prices includes
one post from a handle Frasier doesn't recognize that mentions, in
passing, *"a yellow hoodie at a Boise gas station."* The post is
deleted by Wednesday morning. Frasier saves the cache.

**Mechanic:** ENDGAME PUSH. The strategic layer's final two weeks
are characterized by rapid simultaneous deliveries — multiple
migrants moving at once, fewer turns to react. The player must
trust their setup. The strategic dispatch UI compresses to one
panel showing all active migrant arcs.

**Cast moves:** the Vargas-Quintana family arrives in Smolvud;
Connie Daigle makes her own choice; the Boise sighting (will
become Frasier's 12th if the river-window sighting in W13 also
lands).

### Week 13 · The Sighting

*Theme:* Frasier sees her.

**Strategic events:**

- Monday: Frasier walks across the warehouse to the BBS terminal.
  Through the river window, in the gap between two iron struts
  where the bird-watcher used to stand, he sees — *for a
  quarter-second* — a young woman in a yellow rainslicker at the
  river bank. Blue curls. Purple-rimmed glasses. She is looking up
  at the iron struts.
- Tuesday: Frasier does not write about this in any BBS thread. He
  composes a Cathedral Letter entry that the player will see at
  Labor Day. (Mechanically: the entry is *the gravitational center*
  of the Cathedral Letters suite. It is short. The player will
  recognize its weight by its position in the suite.)
- Wednesday: a small Small Wood crisis: Otis Vandermeer is
  hospitalized for two days. He is going to be fine. He goes back
  on the network Friday.
- Thursday: a Harmony Creek crisis: Norman Lott, the surveyor,
  comes within four feet of Carla Vega's recording equipment
  without seeing it. Carla does not move. Norman does not see.
  Carla's 47 Hz recordings remain in her possession.
- Friday: Bea Mercer mails herself a postcard from Smolvud. She
  has applied to the Lincoln City photography workshop and been
  accepted. She will leave HCE three days after her family settles
  in.
- Saturday: the Cathedral floor's BBS terminal hums for ninety
  seconds in a register Frasier has not heard in three years. He
  listens. He does not know what he listened to.

**BBS night:** STEEPLE posts a thread titled *"what we saw this
week."* Multiple sysops contribute. None of them describe Aria.
All of them describe seeing her. The reader who has decoded the
circle's vocabulary will know.

**Mechanic:** THE SIGHTING. The game allows ONE on-screen Aria
moment, from Frasier's POV, in the river window. The visual is
the canon brief — yellow, blue, purple. She is not addressed. She
is not approached. She is seen. The sighting is a single quarter-
second of animation: she is there; she is not there. Frasier
moves to the BBS terminal as if nothing happened, because nothing,
on the surface, did.

**Cast moves:** Frasier sees her — his 12th sighting; the count
updates in the Tower Watch suite as a single digit changing. Bea
Mercer makes her own escape. Otis briefly hospitalized. Carla
Vega protected by Frasier's quiet network.

### Week 14 · End of Summer Begins

*Theme:* the seam closes. The book continues.

**Strategic events:**

- Monday: final accounting. The strategic map shows what was
  held, what was lost, what was delivered. Several Graustark
  nodes have, by end-of-day, stabilized. Several HCE nodes have,
  by end-of-day, fallen further than Frasier wanted them to. Small
  Wood has either the room above Sister Kell's mission garage or
  no room at all — depending on Otis's W9 recovery.
- Tuesday: the Mercers move to HCE. Bea leaves three days later
  for Lincoln City.
- Wednesday: the moth, converted, makes one final BBS post on its
  old handle. It is one sentence and it is about the autumn
  coming. Frasier reads it once and does not respond. The post
  goes to the Conversion Suite as its closing entry.
- Thursday: Helen Soong's silence is, by now, three weeks old.
  Her conversion is complete. The player understands this
  implicitly without being told. The Conversion Suite gains an
  entry written in her voice, which is also exactly her voice
  and exactly wrong.
- Friday: the Vargas-Quintana family's first week in Smolvud
  closes. They have rented an apartment above the Salty Tome.
  (Lena will, in vol 7, be Petra's customer for years.)
- Saturday: Frasier composes the final Cathedral Letter of the
  summer. The letter is one paragraph. The player will see it
  Sunday.
- Sunday (Labor Day): the BBS night doubles in length. All seven
  visible board threads update one final time. The interlude
  shelf binds. The phosphor-green CRT goes dark when the player
  closes the last board.

**BBS night:** all boards. All threads. All DMs. The closing read.

**Mechanic:** BINDING. The interlude shelf assembles its suites
*in real-time* as the player closes the BBS for the final time.
Each suite plays a single chord — a small audio sting unique to
that suite. The order is fixed: Migration → Conversion → Seed →
Refusal → Backchannel → Tower Watch → Cathedral Letters. The
Cathedral Letters suite plays last and longest. When all seven
chords have sounded, the gallery returns to the main menu. The
COMMUNITY PLANNED tile remains permanently lit on the gallery —
the player can re-read the shelf any time. The next part of
*Planned Community* — *End of Summer Begins* — is now available
to turn to.

**Cast moves:** all endings. All seeds planted. All conversions
accepted. All Cathedral Letters complete. The summer closes.

## Cross-week recurring threads

Some narrative threads run across multiple weeks rather than
landing in a single one. These give the summer its texture
between the per-week beats.

### The Maya correspondence

Maya Daigle writes Frasier ~1-2 letters every two weeks. Each
letter is short, hand-drawn, signed *M*. The cumulative letters
form their own POV document in the shelf at Labor Day. Maya's
letters carry, increasingly across the summer, Aria sightings she
herself doesn't recognize as Aria sightings.

### The Cathedral Letters

Composed weekly when Frasier is at the workbench. Each entry is
two-to-four short paragraphs. The accumulating suite spans the
summer; the W13 entry is its gravitational center. The W14 entry
is its valediction.

### Tower brightness chart

Updated weekly. The player can read the chart from W4 onward. The
W7 dim event, the W10 flicker, the W13 silent hum, and the W14
final reading are the chart's structural moments. The Tower Watch
suite renders the chart as the suite's central image.

## Difficulty / pace controls

Two player-side controls to let the summer play at the right
tempo:

- **Player turn pacing.** No timer. A turn lasts as long as the
  player wants. A long careful turn (15+ minutes) gives the
  player time to read a BBS thread carefully and catch tilts
  early. A short turn (1-2 minutes) misses things. Most players
  will, by W4, find their natural pacing.
- **BBS depth toggle.** A gallery setting that lets the player
  choose between "surface read" (boards summarized, DMs gisted)
  and "deep read" (every post visible, vocabulary glossary on).
  Both modes are valid. The deep read is the intended experience
  for the patient player; the surface read is the intended
  experience for the player who wants the strategy without the
  homework.

## What this scaffold is not

This week-by-week is a *scaffold*, not a script. Each beat is
load-bearing for the arc; the *specifics* of how each scene
plays — exact dialogue, exact BBS post text, exact strategic-map
problem flavor — are authoring decisions that will happen when
the spec's phase 1 (the spreadsheet sim) and phase 2 (the BBS
shell) ship.

The scaffold is what the writer can sit down to next and start
filling in. Each week is a small writing project. Each named cast
move is a small page or two of authored content. The summer
totals to a writer's-room volume of work in the range of a short
novella, plus the scaffolding of strategic events around it.

That's the work. That's the season. That's the seam in the book.
