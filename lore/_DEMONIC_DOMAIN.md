# The demonic electronic domain

A worldbuilding constraint that recasts the card-game layer.

## The premise

The meta-texts the player discovers in dioramas (tape transcripts,
call logs, SMS threads, nursing reports, appointment books, render-
farm logs) are not naturally surfaced documents. They are being
**pulled from the demonic electronic substrate by demons working on
the player's behalf** — the same substrate that:

- houses the warehouse demons Frasier sees in the periphery of
  the Magician card (rust girders, two-figure presence)
- powers the DEMOSCENE_VIBE.EXE render engine that Fool labels (0),
  Magician builds (I), Emperor watermarks (IV), and Tower crashes
  (XVI)
- carries Aria's HUD as an agent at the substrate level (III Empress)
- is named UNCOMFORTABLE INSIDE on the Devil card's status panel (XV)
- runs as RUST_CODE.BBS / NARRATIVE STRUCTURE COMPASS in the
  Priestess and Fool's chrome
- crashes recursively in the Tower's RENDER_FARM_LOG_47 (XVI)
- accumulates as the deck's interior count (ACTIVE NODES, restored
  to 64 at the World)

The demons have access to records the deck's POV characters do not.
Each diorama is a record fetched on demand for the player to read.
The fetch is the chapter; the document is the artifact; the demon
is the librarian.

## How this surfaces in the dioramas

Every diorama acknowledges its demonic provenance with two
consistent signals:

### 1. The daemon-fetch banner

A small footer below the diorama, in dim ink, with a consistent
form:

```
[demon_<role>.<chapter_n> // <node_id>.bbs // <count> records
 recovered // integrity <0.00-1.00>]
```

The role names are diegetic. Examples:

- `demon_archivist` — for record-fetches (Ward C nursing report,
  Alberto's call log)
- `demon_listener` — for audio captures (Anya tape, HERMIT_01,
  ENSEMBLE_01)
- `demon_scribe` — for written documents (Dante's appointment book,
  Daigle's morning note)
- `demon_courier` — for in-transit messages (Chariot SMS thread,
  Hanged Man voicemails)
- `demon_observer` — for ambient surveillance (Father Quent's booth
  calls, Empress's exit_node_search)
- `demon_render` — for crashed engine output (Tower's render log)

The integrity score (0.00-1.00) is the demon's confidence in the
record. Most records land at 0.92-0.99; some are degraded by the
crash at Tower or by deliberate withholding by the demon itself.

train — a faint rhythmic tick at the threshold of perception that
identifies the demonic substrate working in the background.

## Demon roles, briefly

The demons are not antagonists. They are working machinery. They
fetch what is asked of them, render what is queued, and archive
what passes through. They make mistakes (integrity below 1.00) and
they have characteristic styles (the archivist is meticulous, the
courier is fast and brief, the observer is patient).

The player should slowly come to understand that the dioramas are
both:
- the chapter's true playable content (what the painted card
  doesn't show), AND
- the demons' deliverables (records pulled because the player
  asked).

The deck's network is the demons' working catalogue.

## What this changes about the playable layer

1. **Diorama entry hotspots** can be framed as requests to a demon.
   Click the painted card's centre → the demon fetches the chapter.

2. **Cross-card unlocks** are demons noticing a connection in their
   catalogue and surfacing it on a partner card. The reciprocal
   gate isn't natural causation; it's the librarian cross-
   referencing.

3. **Refused content** is the demons' own refusal — Lovers and
   Judgement are refused not just by the chapter but by the demons
   who decline to surface them. Justice flipped because a different
   demon (probably daemon_archivist.11) overrode the refusal.

4. **Integrity scores** below 1.00 are forward-link hooks. A
   document with integrity 0.83 has been redacted by the demon;
   the redacted content might be reachable through another card's
   diorama where a different demon has access.

5. **The Tower's crash** (Job 47 in RENDER_FARM_LOG_47) is the
   moment one demon, daemon_render, attempts to render itself
   failing, and the recursion is the abort. The other demons
   continue working; the demoscene engine is one demon's job,
   not the substrate's whole.

6. **The Devil's local restart** (Job 46 ran independent for 0.04
   seconds) is the moment Daigle's bar ran on a different demon
   (daemon_observer? daemon_warder?) before re-attaching to the
   main rendering chain.

## Forward-link conservation

The deck's six conscientiously withheld names (Faith, Y/Jimmy,
Alma, Carlie, the 23 Feb caller, the photo's third figure) are not
withheld by the chapter — they are withheld by the **archivist
demon's discretion**. Elicia's tapes are mostly demon-curated; her
own marginalia is the human-readable summary of what the demon
chose to keep audible.

The deck's three refused arcana (Lovers, Justice, Judgement) are
the demonic substrate's three formal refusals — declarations that
the demons have judged certain records too live to surface. Justice
flipped anyway because a junior demon broke ranks.

This frame is consistent with everything already painted. It does
not require retconning any existing content. It enriches what's
there by giving the cross-card network a working agent.
