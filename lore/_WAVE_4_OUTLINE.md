# Wave 4 · The Saga Closes

Wave 3 made the cards mutate. Wave 4 is the endgame. The eight
major-arcana chapters of vol5 are by Wave 3 a *system*; Wave 4 turns
the system into a **saga** — a final long-form structure that uses
every prior run as its raw material.

The premise of Wave 4 is that the player has, by now, completed each
of the eight arcana at least once, made dozens of binary choices,
watched cards level and mutate and burn. Wave 4 is what those
choices were *for*. It asks: when the saga closes, what does the
deck look like, and what is left on the board after the last leap?

## What changes for the player

### The Dossier

After each chapter is completed for the first time, a Dossier page
binds into a single document the player can read in the main menu.
The Dossier is the saga's **memory of you** — every binary, every
lore_token, every visitor connected, every card burned, every refusal
played, every drift mutation, every one-saga card spent.

The Dossier is printed in the in-universe font (Antonio's penciled
margin notes; Elicia's annotated cue sheets; Frasier's site logs).
Each chapter's page is the prose form of that chapter's NPC.

The Dossier is **not** a stats screen. It's a story. A run that
took six hours, played across nine sittings, becomes 22 pages.

### The Minor Arcana as Bridges

Between major-arcana chapters, the player can play **minor arcana
scenarios** — short (8-12 minute) bridges that use a major-arcana
hand in a small contained moment. Minor arcana never carry their
own hands; they always borrow one.

Four suits, each themed:

- **Cups** (water, grief, family). A Cups scenario sits in a moment
  of family aftermath — a wake, a phone call to a sister, a quiet
  morning. The visitor pool is tight (1-2 visitors). The deck is
  pruned. The mechanic is *listening as the entire run*.
- **Swords** (air, decision, language). A Swords scenario is a
  conversation that has to land — a confession, a refusal, a
  testimony. Verb pool restricted to LISTEN / DELIVER / REFUSE.
- **Wands** (fire, craft, making). A Wands scenario is a piece of
  work — building, drafting, finishing. Refusal cards do not exist
  in Wands. Only making.
- **Pentacles** (earth, money, infrastructure). A Pentacles scenario
  is a transaction — the bill, the envelope, the small payment that
  binds two parties. Doctrine economy is doubled.

Each suit has 14 numbered scenarios (Ace through King) — 56 minor-
arcana scenarios total, written sparser and faster than the majors.
They're *bridges*, not *destinations*. Playing a minor between two
majors carries tokens forward and threads consequence.

### Convergent Scenarios

Two-hand scenarios where the player plays alternating turns as two
canon hands at once. The same room, two decks, two patience clocks,
two doubt dials. The verbs are the same. The connection arcs are
shared — both hands earn the token.

Six designed so far, all canon-aligned:

- **The Sunday Brunch** · Nicola (host) + Dante (host) · table 17 at
  noon. The board has Paul and the council as visitors. The two
  hosts alternate turns trying to align their staff while Paul tries
  to extract a favor.
- **The Phone in Houston** · Marta-as-Mackenzie? — no. Antonio + the
  phone-Mackenzie-from-the-Lovers-arc. Two hands across a single
  call. (Note: convergent scenarios always use two canon hands; if a
  pairing wants a non-canon presence, it appears as a visitor only.)
- **The Tape Review** · Elicia + Frasier in the bungalow's living
  room with the Anya footage on the TV. Elicia plays the editor;
  Frasier plays the witness.
- **The Roberts Front Walk** · Mackenzie + Philip-as-John (John on
  the front walk as Mackenzie's reader). The Polaroid morning.
- **The Warehouse Catwalk** · Frasier + Antonio at the cypress beam.
  The professional inspection from both POVs simultaneously.
- **The Last Sunday on the Boat** · Dante + Quentin/Paul at Table
  17. The final brunch before the wreck day.

Convergent scenarios pay out double tokens but lock a saga flag —
the convergence either happens or doesn't, and the ledger records
which.

### The Procession (endgame)

When every major chapter is complete and at least one Convergent
scenario has resolved, the **Procession** unlocks: the player walks
the eight major arcana in order, in a single sitting, with the
**composted deck** — the union of every card that has, across the
entire saga, been burned, refused, mutated, or composted in any run.

The Procession is mechanically distinct:

- Each arcana chapter compresses to **four turns**. The map is the
  same; the visitor pool is reduced to a single visitor per chapter.
- The deck is the saga compost — ~150-200 cards, drawn at random
  five at a time.
- Connection tokens earned in the Procession are **gold-tier**, a
  third token type; they unlock the final ending text.
- One Convergent scenario is woven in between two arcana of the
  player's choice as the **hinge** — the procession pauses, the
  second hand joins for that one chapter, then leaves.

The Procession is the long ending. A single sitting, 60-90 minutes,
the whole saga compressed and replayed at speed with the deck the
saga itself wrote.

### The Witness Replay

Saved runs (any run) can be replayed in **witness mode** — the
player watches the run back as a silent observer. The cards play
themselves at the original pace; the dice roll the way they rolled.

But: **once per replay, the player can spend a card from their
current deck to change a verb in the replay**. The replay forks; the
forked run writes a saga flag (`rewrote_the_priestess_third_run`).
The cost of the rewrite is the spent card composts permanently.

The Witness Replay is the saga's **regret mechanic** — the player
can go back once and change one thing, and the cost is real.

### The Last Card

The 22nd card the player plays in their first complete Procession
becomes a **permanent visitor** on the main menu screen — a small
animated figure standing in the gallery's lobby, who waves when the
player launches the game. The Last Card is the saga's signature.

Different players will end with different Last Cards. The community
of players, comparing screenshots of their lobby figures, becomes
part of the meta-game. (No leaderboard. Just the figure.)

## Evolving card mechanics

Wave 3 made cards remember. Wave 4 makes them **accumulate**.

### 1. The Saga Deck

A new deck type that exists only in the Procession: the union of
every card that has, in any run, been **burned, refused, mutated,
exhausted, or composted**. ~200 cards by saga-end. Drawn at random,
played one-at-a-time. The Saga Deck is the player's run-history made
playable.

### 2. Convergent Cards

Cards that require **two hands present** to play. They only appear in
Convergent scenarios. They have two-line flavor — one in each hand's
voice — and resolve at both hands' spaces simultaneously.

```
HOLD THE FRAME · Mackenzie + Elicia
  Mackenzie: "I hold the loom steady."
  Elicia: "I hold the camera steady."
  Both: complete one visitor's sit_with verb at no cost.
```

Twelve Convergent cards across the six Convergent scenarios.

### 3. Forgiveness Cards

A small class of card that, when played, **undoes a binary doctrine
flag in the ledger** — the player chooses one prior binary, the
ledger rewrites it to the opposite. The Forgiveness card composts
permanently.

Each hand gets one. Earned by completing the chapter at hard tier.

Antonio · I SHOULD HAVE PICKED UP · undoes one let-it-ring or one
not-picked-up phone flag.

Frasier · I SHOULD HAVE TOLD HIM · undoes a tell-him-fine to a
tell-him-not (or vice versa).

Mackenzie · I SHOULD HAVE PICKED UP THE THREAD SOONER · undoes the
fourteen-month gap flag.

Etc.

Forgiveness cards are the most expensive cards in the saga.

### 4. Card Recall

A drafted card that costs heavy doubt to play but **pulls one card
out of the compost pile back into your hand for one turn**. The
pulled card returns to the compost after play. Useful when the saga
deck is rich and the current run is starving.

### 5. The Witness's Card

A card that exists only during a Witness Replay — the rewrite card.
Choosing it spends a card from the current deck. The replay forks.
The current deck loses the spent card (composts it). The forked
ledger writes a new flag.

The Witness's Card is the only card whose effect lives outside the
run it's played in.

### 6. The Last Card (mechanic)

A flag on every card: `was_22nd_played_in_procession`. Whichever
card carries it at saga end becomes the lobby figure. It's a quiet
mechanic — most players won't notice it's being tracked.

### 7. Card Marriage

Two cards in the same hand that have been played at the same space
with the same visitor connected become **married** — they are
drawn together going forward and resolve together if both are in
hand. The marriage shows as a small wax-seal binding the two card
icons on the hand panel.

Marriage is permanent for the saga. Cards can be married to a
maximum of one other card.

### 8. The Refused Pile

Cards refused (Wave 3) accumulate in a separate pile from the
compost. The Refused Pile has its own milestone — at 7 refusals,
the player gains a card called WHAT I DIDN'T SAY that, when played,
references one of the refused cards by title in its flavor text.

The Refused Pile is the saga's record of restraint.

### 9. Saga Verbs

By the Procession, four new universal verbs appear in every hand,
earned through saga milestones:

- **WITNESS** (earned at first Witness Replay): play to add a card
  to the echo without playing it
- **MARRY** (earned at first Card Marriage): play to bind two cards
  in your hand
- **CONVERGE** (earned at first Convergent scenario): play to bring
  the companion's hand temporarily into your hand
- **PROCESS** (earned at Procession unlock): play to draw one card
  from the saga compost

These are the saga's voice in the deck.

### 10. Card Inheritance

When the player starts vol6 (or any sequel volume the game supports
later), their vol5 ledger and compost pile inherit forward. The
first run in the new volume offers the player a **bequest hand** —
the canon hand for that volume's first chapter, plus three cards
drawn from the vol5 compost as a starting bonus.

Card Inheritance is the bridge between volumes, and the mechanism by
which the saga keeps going beyond vol5.

## Sequencing for Wave 4

Four phases.

1. **The Dossier.** Lowest mechanical risk, highest emotional payoff.
   Builds on Wave 2's ledger; ships first as a read-only document.
2. **Minor arcana bridges.** Authoring-heavy but mechanically simple.
   56 short scenarios across four suits. Re-uses every existing
   location and hand; no new visitor types required.
3. **Convergent scenarios + Convergent Cards.** Engine work on two-
   hand turn alternation. Six scenarios. Twelve cards.
4. **The Procession + Witness Replay + Forgiveness Cards + Last
   Card.** The endgame layer. Everything Wave 4 was building toward.

## Out of scope for Wave 4

- True multiplayer / pass-the-controller / online co-play.
- Procedural visitor / card / scenario generation.
- Soundtrack DLC, art books, etc. (these are publishing, not design).
- Vol6 itself. Card Inheritance is the bridge mechanic; vol6 is
  another saga.

## Wave 4 success criteria

- Every player who completes the eight major arcana sees their
  Dossier and reads at least three pages.
- 50% of completing players complete at least one minor arcana
  bridge.
- 25% of completing players complete at least one Convergent
  scenario.
- 10% of completing players complete the Procession in full.
- 100% of Procession-completers leave the saga with a Last Card
  figure on their main menu, and 80% of those tell at least one
  other person.

If those land, the saga closes.

## The shape of the whole

Across the four waves:

- **Wave 1** · the rooms exist (22 scenarios + 21 cameos · ✓)
- **Wave 2** · the rooms remember each other (ledger / companion /
  drafting / clock)
- **Wave 3** · the cards remember themselves (levels / refusal /
  mood / one-saga)
- **Wave 4** · the saga closes (dossier / minors / convergent /
  procession)

By Wave 4 the player isn't playing scenarios anymore. They're
finishing a story they've been writing the whole time. The cards
were the medium; the deck was the manuscript; the Procession is the
last reading.

That's the game.
