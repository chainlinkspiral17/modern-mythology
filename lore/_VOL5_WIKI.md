# VOL 5 · MAJOR ARCANA · Wiki

The synthesizing entry point for vol 5 of *Modern Mythology* — the
**Major Arcana** volume. Fragmented canon lives across many lore
docs and per-arcana files; this wiki is the *start-here* page that
points to the rest.

## What vol 5 is

The volume in which the twenty-two Major Arcana of the Tarot are
mapped, chapter by chapter, onto the cast and town of Graustark,
Texas / Louisiana. Each major arcana gets a chapter (vol5 ch0
through ch21) anchored on a specific character, location, and hour
of day. The volume's central trauma is **the Sinkhole** — the
calamity that reshaped the east bank of the river and that the
deck's cards collectively narrate.

Vol 5 is the saga's anchor: subsequent volumes draw their elder
cast, their political pressure, and their unresolved grief from
this volume's events.

Tonal register: **operator-noir warm**. D'Ambrosio's diner late at
night. The cathedral hum. Brass railings, cassette decks, phosphor-
green CRTs. The late-90s-feeling era when Frasier built the
Cathedral floor and the demons were first bound.

### The demon roster (Frasier's bound programs)

Eight demons bound at the Cathedral. Each one half-mechanical,
half-creature in the gauntlet's icon set; each one introduced in
the Magician chapter.

`vagrant · cicada · moth · steamboat · weir · filly · starling · husk`

By vol 6 these demons are evolved — they have BBS handles,
opinions, and (in some cases) the kind of complexity that means

network changed them.

## Locations

Cluster-by-cluster, the locations vol 5 builds and that subsequent
volumes inherit:

### Graustark · the home soil

- **D'Ambrosio's** — riverboat-architecture diner moored on the
  river bank. White clapboard, brass railings, vinyl booths,
  kitchen alcove, closed cocktail bar to the parking-lot side. The
  Fool's anchor. Late 90s.
- **The Riverboat (interior)** — the upscale boat the
  Empress/Emperor chapters live in. Same physical boat. The dining
  room is Nicola's floor; the helm above is Dante's.
- **The Cathedral of Rust and Code** — Frasier's converted 1920s
  warehouse on the river bank, two blocks downriver from
  D'Ambrosio's. Twenty-two tarot stations built into the floor as
  miniatures and installations. The BBS terminal in the corner is
  RUST_CODE.BBS.
- **Elicia's bungalow** — one-bedroom shotgun on a quiet side
  street. The cassette archive against one wall. The pomegranate
  tree out back.
- **Nicola's house** — the matriarch's throne. The letter wall.
- **The Hierophant circuit** — St Jude's Catholic Church, the
  black car, Table 17 of D'Ambrosio's (Sunday brunch), the park
  bandstand, the old armory, the riverfront.
- **The Roberts house** — Mackenzie and Philip's small house, the
  loom in the back room, the basil pot in the kitchen window, the
  mailbox post that needs repair.
- **Ember & Ash** — Antonio's warehouse, somewhere between Houston
  and New Orleans. The cypress beam. The leaded window above the
  desk.

### Beyond Graustark

- **The Sinkhole** (the calamity location, reshaped the east bank)
- **The river** (always visible, always moving)
- **Houston / Beaumont / New Orleans** (the wider Gulf-Coast
  geography Frasier's network reaches into)

## The central trauma · the Sinkhole

The deck's organizing wound. The calamity that reshaped the east
bank of the Graustark river.

The Sinkhole's events are **never fully explained**. The deck does
not resolve them. Multiple chapters (Magician, Chariot, several
others) cross-reference it. The reader assembles what they can
from the references; the cards reserve the rest.

What is canon:

- **The Sinkhole happens narratively in the middle of the major
  arcana arc.** The vol 5 chapter sequence passes through it. The
  cards before the Sinkhole and the cards after the Sinkhole are,
  in their tone and their roster, differently colored. Some
  characters who appear in the early cards do not appear in the
  later cards. Some characters who are present in the later cards
  were not present in the earlier ones.
- **Dante D'Ambrosio dies in the Sinkhole.** The Emperor's helm
  chair empties. The riverboat is lost with him. The two are not
  separable: the Sinkhole takes Dante and the boat in the same
  event, in a way the deck does not clarify.
- **The surviving son carries on the D'Ambrosio name.** Of Dante's
  two sons, one survives the Sinkhole event. (Which one is
  canonical, and how that interacts with the Chariot's wreck-day
  events in vol 5 ch 20, is the deck's call to make as the
  manuscript settles.) The surviving son opens a new restaurant in
  Graustark and carries the family's small empire forward —
  smaller, more careful, less of a riverboat and more of a
  storefront. By vol 6's window the new restaurant is the family's
  active operation.
- **Frasier's steamboat model** on the workbench is the in-fiction
  miniature of the Sinkhole. Finishing the steamboat in the
  Magician scenario *is* the calamity at miniature scale; the
  cross-arcana cipher that ties them is one of the deck's central
  surfaces.

What is reserved:

- **What the Sinkhole actually is.** Geological event. Reality-
  level event. Something Dean's substrate did or did not stop. The
  deck does not say. The deck reserves whether anyone in the
  saga's later volumes ever fully understands it.
- **Why the Sinkhole takes Dante and the boat specifically.**
  Reserved. The boat being lost with him is the only narrative
  fact that lands; the *why* is not authored.

See `_SINKHOLE_NEXUS.md` for the deeper reframing of the calamity
as the deck's organizing trauma.

## Key artifacts and motifs

- **The steamboat model** on Frasier's workbench. Red hull, brass
  paddle-wheel, smokestack-in-progress. Half-built throughout most
  paintings. The cross-arcana sinkhole made small.
- **The BBS terminal** at the Cathedral's corner. Phosphor-green
  CRT. RUST_CODE.BBS. The architecture of the cross-volume meta-
  system.
- **Elicia's cassette archive** — wall of tapes, labels painted as
  strokes (no legible English).
- **The Polaroid** — the Lovers' morning catastrophe.
- **The water glass at Table 17** — the Empress's vessel of
  hospitality, and (in the Dean cameo) the vessel of withholding.
- **The cypress beam** — Antonio's warehouse, the question Frasier
  answers in the Chariot cameo.
- **The lapel pin** — Paul and the aide's shared signal.

## The Tarot Gauntlet · vol 5's gallery game

The 22-chapter card-and-dice gauntlet game that turns each major
arcana into a playable scenario. Each scenario is a one-room solo
game where the player adopts the canon hand and runs a four-verb
connection economy with the chapter's visitor pool.

The gauntlet is, alongside the novel-text of vol 5, the **player
canon** for the volume. See:

- `_TAROT_GAUNTLET.md` — the game's overview
- `_TAROT_GAUNTLET_SCENARIOS.md` — every scenario authored, all
  912 lines
- `_GAUNTLET_BUILD_WIKI.md` — the build wiki / mechanics reference
- `_CAMEO_BUILD_SPEC.md` — the cameo system (cross-arcana guest
  scenarios)
- `_WAVE_2_OUTLINE.md`, `_WAVE_3_OUTLINE.md`, `_WAVE_4_OUTLINE.md`
  — the design roadmap for mechanics evolving across the gauntlet's
  lifecycle

## The Pomegranate Hour

Elicia's web series. Twenty-two episodes, one per major arcana,
running parallel to the novel-text and the gauntlet game. The
episodes are short, recorded on cassette deck and home camcorder,
and exist as in-fiction artifacts the player can collect in the
gauntlet.

See `_POMEGRANATE_HOUR.md` for the series-level doc and
`pomegranate_hour/00_fool.md` through `21_world.md` for the 22
authored episode scripts.

## What vol 5 contributes to later volumes

What the later volumes inherit from vol 5:

- **The cast, what's left of it.** The eight canon player-hands and
  their NPC circles, aged forward into vol 6 and vol 7. **Dante is
  not among them** — the Sinkhole takes him before vol 5 ends.
  The surviving D'Ambrosio son carries the empire forward via a
  new Graustark restaurant; the riverboat itself is gone.
- **Frasier's network.** The Cathedral floor, the BBS terminal, the
  demon roster. By vol 6 the network is older; by vol 7 the
  network has, in places, transferred.
- **The Sinkhole's aftermath.** The east bank's reshaping is a fact
  of the world the later volumes are set in. Dante's absence and
  the boat's absence are facts that vol 6 takes as already-done.
- **The wreck day.** Antonio's Chariot-canon resolution (the
  cypress beam choice + the wreck day at vol 5 ch20) becomes a
  doctrinal flag the later volumes read. Whether the wreck day is
  the same event as the Sinkhole, or a separate event, is the
  deck's reserved call.
- **Dean.** The lapels-smoothing gentleman at Table 14. The vol 5
  surface of the saga's transworld traveller.
- **Aria's anticipation.** Not yet named in vol 5 prose; present in
  Frasier's Cathedral by the time vol 6 begins.
