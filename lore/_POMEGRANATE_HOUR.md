# THE POMEGRANATE HOUR
## Elicia's web series · 22 episodes · vol5 priestess card lore

The High Priestess's card lists "Exit Through the Gift Shop" as
its subtitle — a documentary about authorship, fakery, and the
moment a viewer becomes a maker. Elicia made one. She did not
announce it. She uploaded twenty-two episodes to a streaming
page called **VEIL.tv** between vol3 and the start of vol5. The
page's domain registration lapsed in vol5; the episodes are now
hosted nowhere; ripped copies circulate in a small private list
that includes Frasier (handle "FT"), Frank (handle never
registered — Elicia mailed him a hard drive), Anya (the un-named
recurring actress), and the NexCorp Residential Solutions
compliance auditor whose handle is "RES_SOL_VERIFY" and whose
existence is the deck's clearest preview that vol6 is already
watching vol5.

The series is Lynch-tonal across the board: long takes, dream
logic, dual identities, mid-American twilight, recursive scenes,
a 60 Hz electrical hum that runs under every episode whether
the diegesis warrants it or not. Each episode runs about
twenty-two minutes. Each episode mirrors one Major Arcana. Each
episode foreshadows volume six (Planned Community / Harmony
Creek Estates) and volume seven (Land of Milk & Honey / Halsey
orchards) — the foreshadows are precise; the series is the
deck's primary advance organ for what comes next.

Filming claim, printed in the closing credits of every episode:
**"All episodes filmed within a 60-mile radius of one unnamed
Texas exurb."** The exurb is Harmony Creek.

## series-level conceits

**Cold open of every episode:** Elicia sits in her halftone B&W
chair, looks at the camera, does not speak, nods once, and the
episode begins. She is credited only as "host." She does not
appear in any episode's body. She is the threshold.

**Title card:** the word THE in white serif, then POMEGRANATE
HOUR in deep burgundy below, on black. A single pomegranate
seed falls from the top of the frame as the cards fade.

**Recurring visual elements:**
- a **Kwik Stop** sign visible through a window or in a parking
  lot in 14 of 22 episodes (the same Kwik Stop will host vol6 ch1)
- a **HARMONY CREEK ESTATES — A NEXCORP COMMUNITY** sign in
  the background of all 22 (the deck's most consistent
  product-placement; Elicia did not have permission)
- a **black pickup with Louisiana plates** passing in the
  background of 8 episodes (vol6's pickup, pre-placed)
- a **Halsey Farms** delivery van or label in 11 episodes (vol7
  pastoral arriving by truck)
- a **60 Hz electrical hum** under every episode, mixed loud
  enough that headphone viewers complain in the comments

**Recurring auditory cue:**
At 9:09 pm in the diegesis of every episode (whether the camera
sees a clock or not), a brief tonal interference. Elicia mixes
it in identically across all 22 episodes. The comment "what is
9:09" appears under fourteen of them.

**Recurring tag:**
End credits close on the line **"a pomegranate hour · veil.tv"**
in white serif. Below the line, in 8-point burgundy, the
disclaimer: **"the host does not speak, by choice."**

**Comment-thread canon:**
- **FT** comments once per episode. His comments are formal
  three-word phrases ("the trace is correct," "the gesture is
  multiplied," etc.) — Frasier reading the arcana mirror.
- **RES_SOL_VERIFY** comments once per episode. The comments
  read like compliance notes ("background signage
  unauthorized — pls remove"). Elicia never removes any.
- **lostchild** comments under three episodes (XII, XVIII, XIX).
  The handle is Natalie's. She watches the three episodes that
  describe her.
- A handle reading only **"—"** comments under VIII, IX, XVII.
  The un-named figure watching herself.

## episode 0 — THE FOOL
### "the boy who couldn't remember which diner"

**Cold open:** Elicia nods. Sits down.

**Body:** A young man wakes in a booth at a diner. His head is
on the formica. The waiter — older, soft-spoken — sets a coffee
in front of him without being asked. The young man does not
recognize the waiter. The waiter has known him for years. The
young man drinks the coffee. He looks at the menu. The menu
is the menu of another diner he has been to and cannot place.
The episode is twenty-two minutes of him trying to recall the
diner he is in by reading the menu of a diner he is not in.

**Lynch signature:** Mulholland Drive's opening disorientation;
the menu as Lost Highway's tape.

**Vol6 foreshadow:** Through the window, across the parking lot,
the Kwik Stop sign. The young man does not look. The waiter
does, once.

**Vol7 foreshadow:** In the young man's wallet, when he pays,
he discovers a pressed wildflower from "Harmony Orchards." He
does not remember putting it there. He puts it back.

**Recurring motif (Faith):** A stray dog watches from across
the road for the entire episode. The dog never crosses.

**Casting:** The waiter is canonically played by an actor who
will become John Frank in vol5. Frasier's vol5 painting of John
as cosmic being is partly a back-formed memory of this
episode's waiter.

## episode I — THE MAGICIAN
### "the trace"

**Body:** A maintenance electrician working alone at night in
an empty office building traces an electrical hum to its
source. He moves floor by floor. The hum is louder in the
elevator. The hum is loudest in the basement. The basement
contains one room with one humming server rack. The rack is
unplugged. The hum continues. The electrician puts his ear
to the rack. The rack is breathing.

**Lynch signature:** Eraserhead's industrial hum given a
floorplan.

**Vol6 foreshadow:** The building's directory in the lobby
shows "NEXCORP RESIDENTIAL SOLUTIONS — FLR 4." The
electrician's clipboard has a work order with NexCorp's logo.

**Vol7 foreshadow:** The electrician's lunchbox, sitting on
the lobby chair, has a label on its side: "property of HALSEY
FARM." We never see him eat. He never picks it up.

**Recurring motif (Frasier):** The electrician is canonically
played by an actor who will become Frasier in vol5. His
soldering iron is in his tool belt the entire episode.
Frasier's vol5 lemniscate-tracing is the gesture he learns
here, from the breathing server.

## episode II — THE HIGH PRIESTESS
### "tape 11"

**Body:** A woman alone in a halftone-monochrome room watches
a VHS tape of herself, two years younger, watching a VHS
tape. On the earlier tape, the earlier woman takes a single
note on a yellow legal pad. The later woman watches the note
get written. The later woman pauses the tape. The note is on
the desk beside her, in her own handwriting, on yellow legal.

**Lynch signature:** Lost Highway's recursion; the videotape

**Vol6 foreshadow:** A Harmony Creek Estates HOA newsletter is
folded on the desk. The headline is illegible. The masthead

**Vol7 foreshadow:** A small mason jar of honey sits in the
corner of the frame. The label reads "halsey · spring

**Meta:** This is the episode Elicia is implicitly in. She is
the woman. The cold open's nod is her acknowledging the trick.
She does not speak in this episode either; the woman never
speaks. The deck's archivist filmed herself archiving herself.

## episode III — THE EMPRESS

## episode IV — THE EMPEROR

**Vol7 foreshadow:** The steaks are labelled, on the butcher

**Casting:** The chairman is the same actor who will play

## episode V — THE HIEROPHANT

resumes.

**Casting:** The caller's voice is the voice we will hear,

Same handle, same Beaumont. The series confirms the long-
running pre-existing order.

## episode VII — THE CHARIOT

## episode XVI — THE TOWER
### "the collapse of the water tower"

**Body:** The town's old water tower collapses on a
Thursday morning. The collapse is filmed by seven separate
amateur cameras — phones, dashcams, a security feed. The
episode is the seven angles cut together, with no narration
and no music, into a twenty-two-minute composite. The
collapse takes nine seconds. The remaining time is the
seven angles before and after.

**Lynch signature:** Twin Peaks Part 8's nuclear test;
collapse as visible math.

**Vol6 foreshadow:** Angle four shows Harmony Creek Estates

van's driver gets out to look. The deck reserves whether the

ch1's Gas & Go.

**Vol7 foreshadow:** Angle six shows water from the burst
tower spraying a peach orchard. The orchard is Halsey's

**Recurring motif:** vol5's Tower chapter is partly the
composite-from-many-angles philosophy applied to one render

deck's grammar.

**Sinkhole acknowledgment:** This is the episode that comes
closest to naming the Nexus. It does not. The collapse is a
water tower, not a sinkhole; the date is wrong by years; the
location is wrong by miles. Elicia files it under "structural
collapse, dry rehearsal." She will not film the actual event.

Halfway through, you notice a gap in the wreath where one
flower would be. The gap is the shape of a person's
silhouette. By the end, the wreath has rotated almost
imperceptibly to bring the gap to the dead center of the
frame. The credits roll over the gap.

as patient indictment.

World card's circular wreath gains a vol7 provenance.

decided what her voice is for, and what it is not for. The
