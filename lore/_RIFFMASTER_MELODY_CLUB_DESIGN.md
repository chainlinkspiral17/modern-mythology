The kids'-synth toy company's own slowstick. Marc Ostrom
moonlighted on it, uncredited, on contract — and then built
Oneironautics' entire audio pipeline around the voice it teaches.
Canon compression made playable: `PDPRiffmaster.gd` already
exists in this repo, and `slowstick_synth.py` documents itself as
"the same 3-oscillator saw + saw + sub-triangle voice used inside
the games' PDP Riffmaster kids'-synth toy."

Tem's grandfather bought this cart for Tem's parent. The save
file still holds a loop recorded by a seven-year-old. That loop
is authored content, and nobody in Vol 7 comments on it.

## THE LOOK

Toy-bright and proud of it — the one stick in the catalog with a
white background:

    #f4f4f0  molded white     #e83030  fat red
    #f8c820  school-bus yellow #2870d8  primary blue
    #30a848  go green         #282828  rubber-key black

The Riffmaster itself fills the lower third: a drawn synth with
rubber keys, two chunky OSC sliders, a SUB button shaped like a
frog, and the famous smiling power light. Keys highlight as
played. The upper two-thirds is the clubhouse: four cartoon club
members on a couch (PDP's house artist, NOT Delahaye — rounder,
dumber, wonderful): DOT-DOT (drums-mouth kid), BEEBO (the frog
puppet, sub-oscillator evangelist), MARCIE (tall, serious,
absolute pitch), and TODD (Todd).

**HeroImages:** the clubhouse couch · the OPEN MIC card with a
construction-paper banner · the smiling power light, close.

## THE SOUND

Is the game. Every meeting's calls are rendered with the real
synth voice (the WAVs are generated by `slowstick_synth.py`
itself — the tool scoring its own in-fiction origin story). The
player's key input plays live through an AudioStreamGenerator
running the same 3-osc voice as `PDPRiffmaster.gd`.

## THE PLAYSTYLE

**Twelve club meetings**, each ~8 minutes, each teaching one
element of the voice through call-and-response:

1. keys (call & echo)            7. the second saw · detune
2. holding notes · envelope       8. vibrato (MARCIE's meeting)
3. rests are notes (BEEBO)        9. slides
4. the sub frog                  10. arps (DOT-DOT's meeting)
5. louder isn't better           11. writing four bars (Todd's
6. sad keys (minor)                  meeting · Todd finally
                                     speaks · it's good)
12. **OPEN MIC** — the player performs a loop of their own; it
    records to the save file, becomes the title-screen music of
    THIS CARTRIDGE forever after, and (token) becomes hummable
    elsewhere.

Call-and-response is graded by the club, generously — three
tries then BEEBO declares it "jazz." The only fail state is
quitting, and the club says goodbye kindly even then.

## THE AMBITION

- **The Rosetta stick.** A player who finishes it can HEAR the
  entire catalog differently — every Estuary drone, the Fey
  Faire waltz, the bus-stop piece — because they have played the
  instrument all of it is made of. Final line, MARCIE: "now
  every song you ever hear on this machine is partly yours."
- Canonize Ostrom's moonlight job and the pipeline's true
  origin: Oneironautics' sound is a children's toy taken
  seriously, which is the studio in one sentence.
- The seven-year-old's loop in the save file is the catalog's
  quietest heirloom — Tem's parent, age seven, four bars.
- Tokens out: `riffmaster_open_mic_recorded` (Estuary 3's cabin
  radio can tune to a station playing the player's own loop, no
  comment); `riffmaster_finished`.

the toy palette. Priority #3 — highest charm per hour in the
