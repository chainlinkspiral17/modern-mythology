# V — THE HIEROPHANT : "Sweaty Sunday Sermonettes"

POV: Maya (child). St. Jude's Acadian Church, Sunday morning,
before the 7am mass. Two acolytes kneeling — Maya and the
unnamed "Y" (revealed at Chariot VII to be Jimmy).

## Tarot lore in play

- Mirror pair: V + XVI = 21 → **Hierophant ↔ Tower**. Tradition
  ↔ revelation. The institutional axis is the tower's first
  break; the bishop's crozier carries its own future hairline
  crack (live reciprocal).
- Adjacencies: IV Emperor ↔ V Hierophant (civil order ↔
  religious order, same staff in two vestments — live cross-
  card gate); V Hierophant ↔ VI Lovers (the rite was always
  for the card that refuses to flip; AVDI ET TACE inscribed
  on the Lovers' upper border — live).
- Classical Hierophant is tradition, religious authority, the
  shared moral system. Modern Mythology renders it as the
  child's body — the dress that bites.

## The scene as a single room

The parking lot of St. Jude's, before the doors open. Maya in
the church-issued dress; Y beside her, in altar-boy whites.
The rose window above. The booth (just out of the painted
frame, in the church vestibule). The carpet beneath them with
its three Latin lines.

Idle motion: the rose window glints; the dress's pleats catch
the dawn light unevenly (a faint visual itch); the carpet
threads stay still (institutional stability rendered as
material refusal to ripple).

  — heaven and earth. Unreachable above her.
- **The triple papal tiara**. Three rings the child must pass
  through.
- **The two kneelers**. Maya and Y. Click Y to identify (forward
  link to Jimmy on the Chariot card).
- **The 8-segment rose window**. Four colors — collect-the-

- **The carpet stripes** with three Latin lines: ECCLESIA SEMPER
  REFORMANDA / ANIMA CHRISTI SANCTIFICA ME / DOMINICA MASS 7 9 11.
- **The AVDI ET TACE banner** (upper border). The chapter's
  thesis in two words.
- **The bishop's crozier** (gated by Emperor ankh, live). The
  same staff Dante holds, in different vestments.
- **Elicia's camera lens** (gated by Priestess camera, live).
  Reflected in a blue rose-window segment. She is three rows

## SAMPLE SCRIPT — "The booth call (V → VII → XII chain origin)"

Mode: the moment just BEFORE the painted scene. Father Quent
is in the vestibule booth, three rows back from the rose
window, dialing three numbers in succession. The booth-call
chain that becomes the Chariot wreck and Natalie's vigil.

```
[INTERIOR. ST. JUDE'S VESTIBULE. PHONE BOOTH. SUNDAY 06:38 AM.
 Father Quent in cassock, dialing. The booth is one of two
 phones in the parish. The other is in the rectory and the
 housekeeper is on it.]

QUENT:    [first dial]
          [waits four rings]

ANTONIO:  [picks up. groggy. evening attire. wreck site is two
           hours away.]
          Yeah.

QUENT:    Antonio. It's Quent.

ANTONIO:  Father. It's — what time is it.

QUENT:    Quarter to seven. I need you to do me a favor I'm
          not going to remember asking you for.

ANTONIO:  [pause]
          Okay.

QUENT:    Drive to Ember and Ash and tell Daigle I changed my
          mind about Thursday. Do not call. Drive. He doesn't
          take the call from this number anymore.

ANTONIO:  Thursday is —

QUENT:    Thursday is the scaffolding. I changed my mind.

ANTONIO:  Okay. I'll go after the eleven.

QUENT:    Go before the seven.

ANTONIO:  Father. The seven is in twenty minutes.

QUENT:    [pause]
          I know what time the seven is.

ANTONIO:  Yeah okay. I'll go.

QUENT:    Thank you.
          [hangs up]
          [second dial]
          [waits six rings]
          [unknown number, no answer registers on log as
           "incoming declined" at 23:47 in Natalie's chapter.]

QUENT:    [hangs up before voicemail]
          [third dial]

VOICE-3:  [unidentified, audible at edge of mic. brief.]
          — yes Father.

QUENT:    Bring the car around at six fifty. Don't take Carlie.

VOICE-3:  Yes Father.

QUENT:    [hangs up]
          [puts receiver down]
          [stands]
          [walks out of the booth toward the rose window]
          [the rite begins in twenty-two minutes]

[END. FILE NOTES: Father Quent placed three calls between
 06:35 and 06:42 on the morning of vol5_ch5. CALL 1: antonio,
 known recipient, redirected him to the wreck. CALL 2:
 unknown recipient, no answer — logged on natalie.d's phone
 as the 23:47 incoming-decline (TIME-ZONE NOTE: natalie's
 device runs PT; Quent dialed central, registered after
 cross-zone shift). CALL 3: unidentified, brief, evidence
 of a third recipient still unaccounted. The deck's
 booth-call chain originates here.]
```

The script does:
- canonizes the V → VII → XII call chain as ONE morning's three
  dials, not three separate moments
- introduces "Daigle" by name early (Devil XV's bar) and ties
  Father Quent to the bar — V → XV adjacency through the
  scaffolding decision
- makes the timing real: Father Quent sends Antonio to a wreck
  that will happen because he WAS SENT, knowing the rite is
  in twenty minutes. The institution times its own causality.
- names a third recipient ("don't take Carlie" — Carlie is
  someone Father Quent has staff he trusts not to bring) as
  forward seed for vol6
- confirms the time-zone explanation for Natalie's 23:47
  incoming-decline matching Quent's CT dial — solves the
  reader's "wait, how does CT dial register as PT 23:47" by
  making it canon
- ends with Father Quent walking toward the rose window — the
  painted scene begins where this script ends. Not derivative;
  contiguous.
