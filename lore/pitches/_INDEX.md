# Pitches & sample scripts — index

Per-card playable-scene pitch plus a sample script that enriches
the narrative without being derivative of the painted scene. 22
of 22 Major Arcana covered.

Each pitch follows the same structure: tarot lore in play · the
scene as a single room · hotspots & discoveries · cross-card
hooks currently live · expansion vectors as later cards arrive.

Sample scripts use one of three modes:
  a) the moment just BEFORE the painted scene
  b) a found document the painted scene REFERS to but doesn't show
  c) the same scene from a secondary character's POV that the
     chapter declines to render

## Through-lines the scripts establish (beyond what the painted
## cards alone show)

- **Elicia's archive corpus.** Tapes named ANYA_03 (II), HERMIT_01
  (IX), ENSEMBLE_01 (XX). The same archivist recording across
  chapters. Her tarot journal (II) writes entries for VI, XI,
  XX (refused-three threads), and XIX (Frank reads his own
  chapter's entry).

- **The withheld names.** Faith (the dog, known to Elicia, not
  written until late). Y (Maya's kneeler companion, confirmed
  as Jimmy by Chariot but unwritten by Elicia in her journal).
  Alma (Dante's 10:30 private slot, never explained). Carlie
  (the third Father Quent recipient, instruction "don't take
  Carlie"). The 23 February unidentified caller (Alberto's
  notebook blank). The half-cropped third figure in Mrs.
  Romero's photo. The deck has six conscientiously withheld
  names by the end.

- **Routing past each other.** Simon was in Bed 4 of Ward C on
  Walpurgisnacht (Death XIII script), discharging himself AMA
  at 03:55, asking about a candle. Natalie was at his
  apartment four hours earlier (Hanged Man XII script),
  watching that candle. The deck has been routing them past
  each other for two chapters; the scripts make it canon.
  Mrs. Romero was alive in Bed 5 of the same ward; Marta
  Romero is Anna's mother (Justice XI script); Anna and Erica
  were already friends; the case is a triangulation of one
  woman's trust through two readers.

- **Alberto's middleman role.** Three institutional axes:
  Dante (Emperor IV) / Father Quent (Hierophant V) / Daigle
  (Devil XV). Alberto called Daigle 19 times in 14 minutes
  the night of the wreck. Daigle didn't pick up. Knew by
  call seven. The Tower's render-farm log places Daigle's
  bar as the ONLY chapter that attempted local restart from
  the demoscene engine — for 0.04 seconds, ran independent,
  then re-attached and aborted.

- **Frank's notebook spine.** Tuesday (Temperance XIV)
  disciplined entry. Saturday (Sun XIX) full entry. Attachment
  A finally attached: the four-mote pattern IS the lemniscate
  from Magician/Priestess/Strength. Four cards, one curve,
  read in Frank's room.

- **Natalie's spine.** Hanged Man (XII) writes; Moon (XVIII)
  reads. Three voicemails on Monday (escalating-then-tightening
  pace); three sigils on Friday (claimed as her own gestures).
  The deck's longest single-character authorship arc.

- **The two un-named women.** Strength (VIII) and Star (XVII)
  both sign with a single dash. The deck's open question:
  same figure? The signatures insist they could be.

- **The Frog's appearances.** Strength VIII (in the river behind
  the figure). Judgement XX (audibly croaking at 04:12 in the
  recording). World XXI (the closing oracle reading every
  card). The oracle is present at three stations, not one.
