# VI — THE LOVERS : "The Card Stays Face-Down"

POV: none. The chapter's whole premise is the refusal to render
a single figure. The pair lattice (six implicit pairs) IS the
card's true content.

## Tarot lore in play

- Mirror pair: VI + XV = 21 → **Lovers ↔ Devil**. Choice ↔
  bondage. Same furniture in opposite registers: the chair the
  Lovers refused to sit in is the chair the Devil welded to
  the floor.
- Adjacencies: V Hierophant ↔ VI Lovers (institutional rite ↔
  private choice); VI Lovers ↔ VII Chariot (the choice the
  Lovers refused becomes the motion the Charioteer commits).
- Classical Lovers is choice, union, the moment of binding
  intention. Modern Mythology refuses to render it — the
  deck's central thesis is the gesture of refusal as itself
  a tellable story.

## The scene as a single room

There is no scene. The painted card is the ornate face-down
back of a tarot card, frame-filling. The Graustark river runs
beneath. Two banks visible — empty.

Idle motion: the river current moves very slowly; the
ornamental pattern on the card back has a faint shimmer
suggesting the card could flip; the shimmer does not resolve
into a flip.

## Hotspots & discoveries

- **The flip attempt**. Click the center of the card back —
  nothing happens. Refusal is the discovery.
- **The pair lattice** (bottom strip). Six rows. Three confirmed
  via cross-card hooks; three implicit.
- **The waiting hotspot** (upper right). Clicking does nothing
  on first visit. Returns the same nothing on visit two. The
  chapter's third hotspot is the WAIT command made spatial.
- **The listen hotspot** (upper left). Audio cue under the
  surface. Faint river sound that doesn't crest into music.
- **The Graustark river** (center). The deck's geographic
  keystone. Nobody crosses on the Lovers card.
- **The three-refused register** (upper border): VI LOVERS,
  XI JUSTICE, XX JUDGEMENT.
- **AVDI ET TACE inscription** (upper border, gated by
  Hierophant). The rite was always for THIS card — live.
- **Empty throne with two seats** (gated by Emperor steamboat —
  live). The seat that won't hold two.

## Cross-card hooks (live)

- Hierophant → Lovers: AVDI ET TACE inscribed across face-down back.
- Emperor → Lovers: throne motif with two seats, one empty.
- Fool → Lovers: pair lattice's first entry (John & Faith confirmed).
- Chariot → Lovers: pair lattice's second entry (Quentin & Antonio
  confirmed posthumously by BBS).
- Justice → Lovers: three-refused register reorders (Justice
  flipped, Lovers and Judgement still hold).
- Devil → Lovers: same furniture welded.
- World → Lovers: pair lattice closes; all six accounted for.

## Expansion vectors as later cards arrive

- Each remaining implicit pair (Frasier/Aria, Anya/Elicia,
  Nicola/Dante, Maya/Y) gets its own confirmation hotspot as
  their partner cards' cross-card content reveals.
- The river crossings the Lovers refused get rendered as
  crossings other cards make — Strength stands on the far
  bank, Devil is on the Gulf coast (a different shore
  entirely), World's wreath closes around all the banks.

## SAMPLE SCRIPT — "Elicia's journal: VI THE LOVERS entry"

Mode: found document. Elicia annotates every Major in her
tarot notes journal. Frank reads her SUN entry (XIX) on his
own card. The LOVERS entry is the deck's central refusal
given a writer.

```
                              VI · THE LOVERS                  73

The card stays face-down. I have tried to draw it three times
this year. The first time the card came up reversed, the
second time it came up on the floor, the third time it came
up in the box with the others and I did not turn it.

I am not the one to draw it. This is not a complaint about
the deck — the deck is fine. The deck is doing what it does.
The fault is at this end of the spread. I look at the card,
I know what the card means, and I do not arrange a face for
the figures on it. Six pairs in the chapter and I cannot
place any of them in their numbered positions.

This is a problem in the reader, not in the deck. Worth
noting: it is also a problem in the chapter, in the sense
that the chapter rhymes with the reader. (THIS IS WHY THE
CHAPTER RHYMES WITH THE READER. The card refuses where the
reader refuses. The deck is correct.)

I list the pairs anyway. The act of listing is the act the
card permits. Naming without facing.

   JOHN & FAITH      (the dog at the diner; she has a name
                     I will not write yet; J. has not been
                     told it.)
   QUENTIN & ANTONIO (the friends remark and the bought time;
                     I am told one is dead. I have not been
                     told which one.)
   FRASIER & ARIA    (the cathedral signal and the data
                     overlay. They have not met. They are
                     reading each other in alphabets neither
                     speaks.)
   ANYA & me         (across the tape, across the year. I
                     do not write this entry to be read by
                     anyone, including her, including me.)
   NICOLA & DANTE    (succession, not romance. The throne
                     passes; the body inherits the chair.)
   MAYA & Y          (too young; just kneelers. Y has a name
                     too. I record the name in another tape
                     I have not transcribed. The transcript
                     will be the chapter where I admit it.)

Six pairs. None face-up. The chapter says The Card Stays
Face-Down; the journal says the same thing in long form.

The journal's job is to keep saying it until the saying
becomes a different kind of refusal — until the listing is
itself the closure the card declines to render. The pairs
are accounted for. Accounting is not consummation. The card
remains face-down.

                                                ANNOTATION
The XX chapter (JUDGEMENT) is also face-down. The XI chapter
(JUSTICE) I do not yet know — the deck wants me to draw it
and I have not. If I draw it and it flips, the lattice will
have to reorganize: two refused, one flipped. If it does not
flip, three remain. Three is a stable number for refusals.
Two is a problem.

I am keeping the deck closed until I know which.

                                          ELICIA L. (April)
```

The script does:
- canonizes "Elicia knows Faith's name" as in-fiction archive
  withholding — she's not refusing to write it, she's refusing
  to write it HERE (the tape has it)
- canonizes "Y has a name" in the journal but reserved to
  another tape — the deck's deepest layered withholding
- introduces "Anya & me" as the fourth pair, finally confirming
  what every other card has implied: Elicia and Anya ARE the
  Anya-tape relationship. Anya isn't just a subject; she's the
  reciprocal partner Elicia is recording herself across
- canonizes the three-refused logic from the inside — Elicia
  IS the one tracking the count, and her math is "two refused
  is unstable, three refused is stable." The deck's relationship
  to its own refusals becomes legible AS A DECISION.
- ends "April" — the chapter month. Walpurgisnacht (XIII) is
  April 30; the Lovers' journal entry is somewhere earlier in
  the same month. The deck's interior calendar gains a month.
