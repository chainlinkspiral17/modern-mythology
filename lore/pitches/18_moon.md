POV: Natalie David. Friday night. The same kitchen counter
from XII Hanged Man, six chapters later. Natalie's second
chapter, same body, the deck cut three times now.

- Mirror pair: XVIII + III = 21 → **Moon ↔ Empress**. Illusion
  ↔ body. The dual-POV split Nicola/Aria invented at III
  collapses into Natalie at XVIII (one body holding both
  registers).
- Adjacencies: XVII Star ↔ XVIII Moon (hope ↔ illusion); XVIII
  Moon ↔ XIX Sun (night trio middle ↔ closing).

  flanking the path with dog and wolf. Modern Mythology
  replaces the dog/wolf pair with two narwhal-shaped
  reflections (Faith stops at XIII; the deck's water-fauna
  returns spectrally).

  single-character spine: writing (the notebook's unfinished
  sigils at XII) → reading (the completed sigils in the
  static field at XVIII).

## SAMPLE SCRIPT — "Three sigils, read out of the static"

Mode: b — found document. The chapter's static field carries
Natalie's three unfinished sigils, completed by being read.
The script renders the three meanings she finally extracts.
Each sigil is given a name and a one-line gloss.

```
[from natalie's notebook, p.34-35, completed friday 23:00]
[three sigils numbered as in the static field, left to right]

SIGIL ONE
   shape: a candle, single wick, the flame drawn as a
          small open hand
   reading: "What burns is what was given to me. The
          giver is not the flame. The flame is mine.
          When the wax is gone I am the candle."

   note (smaller, later hand):
   simon gave me the candle. the candle is mine. simon
   is not the flame. simon is the giver. the giver was
   never the flame. (it took me four hours at the counter
   to read this sigil from the page i had drawn. it took
   me fourteen seconds to read it from the static.)

SIGIL TWO
   shape: a phone receiver, off the hook, with three small
          rings drawn beside it
   reading: "Three calls leave me. The receiver is mine.
          The not-answered is not me. The not-answered is
          the receiver on the other end. I am where the
          calls left from."

   note (same later hand):
   the phone log on the painted card was my outbound
   record. it was not simon's silence. the chapter is
   not "simon did not call." the chapter is "i made
   three calls." that is mine. that is what i did.

SIGIL THREE
   shape: a narwhal, small, on a keyring — with a second
          narwhal, transparent, swimming behind it
   reading: "The carrier and the carried are the same
          creature. What you take with you is also
          watching you. The water you carry is the water
          you are in."

   note (same later hand):
   the apartment key has a narwhal on it. there is
   another narwhal in the static. they are the same
   narwhal. the chapter is not "i lost the dog (faith)
   and replaced her with a fish" — the chapter is
   "what i carry watches me back, and that is also a
   kind of carrying."

[bottom of page, in two-column smaller hand:]

LEFT COLUMN:                  RIGHT COLUMN:
   what i wrote                 what i read
   was a candle                 a candle is mine
   was a phone                  a phone is mine
   was a creature               a creature is mine

   conclusion: the chapter is the verb of ownership
   coming back to me. the static is the chapter where
   i find out i was always the writer of my own log.

                                                    n.d.
```

The script does:
- canonizes Natalie's reading of her own writing as the
  chapter's completion. The classical Moon's "illusion" is
  rendered as a misattribution: the chapter she thought she
  was in (Simon-silenced) was always a chapter about her
  own outbound action.
- gives the three sigils explicit meanings — the deck's
  occult symbology gets demystified as personal narrative.
  The deck refuses esoteric reading by HAVING the reader
  decode in their own working voice.
- canonizes "the candle is mine" as Natalie's first claim
  in the deck. XII was reactive; XVIII is the chapter where
  the verbs come home.
- canonizes the narwhal substitution as a reading of
  what-i-carry-watches-me-back rather than as a
  replacement-for-Faith. The deck's through-line creature
  switch (Faith stops, narwhal returns) is read as a
  relational architecture, not as a loss.
- signed "n.d." — Natalie David's first signature. The
  Hanged Man's voicemails were anonymous; the Moon's
  reading is signed. The chapter is the chapter of
  ownership.
- ends with the deck's WRITER/READER axis explicitly:
  she wrote on Monday, she reads on Friday. The deck's
  XII → XVIII spine canonized as the spine of
  authorship-of-self.
