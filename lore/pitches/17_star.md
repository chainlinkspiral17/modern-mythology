## SAMPLE SCRIPT — "What I poured. (single page, hand-numbered.)"

Mode: c — secondary POV the chapter declines to render. The
figure who poured the two vessels writes one short list
afterward. The deck's most rationed found document.

```
                            WHAT I POURED.

I poured these things into the river, on Thursday night, in
the order listed.

1.  water — half a vessel — from the well at the back of
    the property — between 21:45 and 21:48
2.  water — half a vessel — from the same well — between
    21:50 and 21:53

The vessels were emptied. I had been told the chapter
required two — one to the river, one to the land. I poured
both into the river. The land would have received the same
water by morning; the river was nearer.

This is a list of what was poured. It is not a list of what
was given. The list of what was given is different and is
not on this page.

I include the times because times are correct. Times are
the only correctness I have at the dispatch end of a
chapter. Whether anything that was poured arrived where it
was needed is not something I can determine from this side.
The river goes where the river goes. The list cannot follow.

I include this page so that someone reading the chapter
afterward — if there is an afterward, which is what the
night sky is for — does not have to imagine what was in the
vessels. There was water in the vessels. The chapter does
not require imagination at this point. The chapter requires
inventory.

                                                        —

(Postscript, smaller hand:)

The smoke at the far end of the clearing is the render farm.
The render farm is somebody else's chapter. The smoke is the
neighbor's weather. I have included it in inventory because
I can see it. I have not included what it means because what
it means is not on this page either.
                                                        —
```

The script does:
- gives the un-named figure the deck's most rationed voice.
  Two sentences, two items, exact timestamps.
- canonizes the classical Star pour as an act of literal
  inventory. The figure does not pour symbolically; she pours
  water. The deck refuses the metaphorical reading.
- canonizes the eight stars + one center as the chapter's
  AFTERWARD-LIGHT: "if there is an afterward, which is what
  the night sky is for." Hope as functional infrastructure.
- canonizes the Tower's smoke as the neighbor's weather. The
  Star chapter does not own the Tower's collapse but cannot
  help noticing it. Modern Mythology's quietest cross-card
  acknowledgement: chapters share air.
- ends with the postscript's own postscript: she has
  included the smoke in inventory because she can see it.
  She has not included what it means because what it means
  is not on this page either. The deck's two recurring
  refusals — to name and to interpret — rendered as a
  reader's discipline.
- signed only with a dash, same as the Strength figure. The
  deck has two un-named women at VIII and XVII. They sign the
  same way. Whether they are the same figure is the deck's
  open question; the signature insists they could be.
