# XIV — TEMPERANCE : "The Moderate Temperature of Tuesday"

POV: Frank. The municipal apartment, the observation chamber.
Tuesday — after the worst Monday, before the rest of the week.

## Tarot lore in play

- Mirror pair: XIV + VII = 21 → **Temperance ↔ Chariot**.
  Moderation ↔ drive. The Chariot crashed; Temperance is the
  chapter Frank reads in moderate light after.
- Adjacencies: XIII Death ↔ XIV Temperance (transit →
  moderation); XIV Temperance ↔ XV Devil (moderate temperature
  → uncomfortable inside).
- Classical Temperance is the alchemist with two cups, the
  blend at slightly different heights. Modern Mythology
  renders the cups untouched — the blend hasn't happened yet
  because the temperature has not yet been moderate enough.

## The scene as a single room

Frank's observation chamber. The two cups (cold black coffee
and warm chamomile, neither lifted). Wall clock with subdial.
Municipal floorplan pinned (three rooms, one used). A photo
frame on the desk (the Chariot wreck before the fall, gated
to Chariot). A single dust mote in the pale-emerald light —
the Sun card seeded early.

Idle motion: the coffee surface holds its meniscus exactly;
the chamomile steams very faintly (the cup is warm);
Frank does not move.

## SAMPLE SCRIPT — "Frank's notebook, Tuesday, full page"

Mode: b — found document. Frank's working observation journal,
the single Tuesday entry. The chapter's subtitle is "The
Moderate Temperature of Tuesday"; the entry is the temperature
report. Most of the page is blank.

```
                                              TUESDAY
                                              [page 47 of 220]

CUPS:    1.   black, cold, undrunk
         2.   chamomile, warm, undrunk

CLOCK:   subdial reads "AFTER THE WORST"
         second hand confirmed running
         minute hand confirmed running
         hour hand confirmed running

ROOMS:   1 of 3 in use (this one)

WEATHER:
         62°F / overcast / wind ESE 4
         barometer falling slowly

LIGHT:
         indirect, ENE window, pale emerald
         dust mote count, west wall area:
            millions estimated
            4 (four) appear to be describing a pattern
            see attachment A (not yet attached)

FRAME ON DESK:
         photograph received 06:12 mon, prior chapter
         do not look at it
         do not move it
         leave it on the desk

PHONE:
         did not ring
         did not place a call
         this is correct for tuesday


OBSERVATION (full entry, 7 lines):

i do not write more on tuesday than this. i write this much
on tuesday because writing more on tuesday would be a kind
of weather. the only weather tuesday gets to have is the
weather report. the rest is for not tuesday. i am writing
this sentence to find out how long it is, not because it is
the sentence i meant. the sentence i meant is the one before
this one. that one was correct. this one is just confirming.
end of entry.
                                                       — fp
```

The script does:
- canonizes Frank's writing discipline: Tuesday is a weather-
  report-only day. He observes; he does not interpret. The
  chapter's subtitle is also Frank's working rule.
- canonizes the four-mote pattern as already visible Tuesday
  morning. Sun (XIX) is when he writes the pattern up; Temperance
  is when he sees it but disciplines himself not to write it.
- introduces "attachment A (not yet attached)" — a placeholder
  for the pattern. Vol6 reads "attachment A" three or four
  chapters in.
- canonizes the Chariot photo as received MONDAY at 06:12 (a
  morning where the painted Chariot scene is the wreck). Frank
  receives the wreck site as a photograph before he opens his
  notebook for the next day. The deck's working speed.
- canonizes "did not ring / did not place a call / this is
  correct for Tuesday." Frank's chapter is the deck's only
  phone-silent chapter. Compared to the Hierophant's three
  dials, the Chariot's SMS thread, the Wheel's 19-call burst,
  and the Hanged Man's three voicemails, Temperance is the
  total negative space.
- ends "end of entry. — fp" — Frank's initials. The deck's
  most withholding character given a working signature for the
  first time.
