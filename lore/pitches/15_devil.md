# XV — THE DEVIL : "Gumbo Limbo"

POV: implicit (un-named bar denizen). Daigle's bar — Gulf
coast, jaundiced amber, Wednesday after Tuesday. The chapter
is the room that doesn't let go.

## Tarot lore in play

- Mirror pair: XV + VI = 21 → **Devil ↔ Lovers**. Bondage ↔
  union (refused). Same furniture: Lovers' refused chair is
  Devil's welded chair.
- Adjacencies: XIV Temperance ↔ XV Devil (moderate ↔
  uncomfortable); XV Devil ↔ XVI Tower (welded furniture
  needing only one bulb-cooked spark to fall).
- Classical Devil = bondage, addiction, the chain that's
  loose enough to lift over the head. Modern Mythology welds
  the chains; bondage rendered as the room's architecture.

## The scene as a single room

Daigle's bar. Counter the length of the frame, three
barstools, the middle one taller. One amber bulb, two stops
too warm. Gumbo Limbo fish carved into the bar edge. Status
panel reads UNCOMFORTABLE INSIDE.

Idle motion: the bulb does not flicker (the chapter's bondage
is the bulb working perfectly at the wrong color temperature);
the middle barstool is occupied (presence implied; figure
unrendered); a single drop from the bar tap every twelve
seconds.

## SAMPLE SCRIPT — "Daigle's Thursday morning note to himself"

Mode: b — found document. The morning after the Chariot's
wreck (which was Monday) and the morning after the Devil's
chapter (Wednesday). Daigle did not pick up Alberto's 19
calls on Monday evening. Antonio died on Monday morning. The
Devil chapter is Wednesday. The note is Thursday morning,
the day the Tower falls, before it does. Daigle is sitting on
his kitchen table trying to write down what happened.

```
                                             THURSDAY 06:11

i am writing this because i have to write something. if i
sit here and do not write something i will pick up the phone
and i will call alberto and he will not pick up. (he won't.
he won't. don't try. if you put down this pen to try, sit
back down and pick the pen back up.) i am writing this
because writing this is the only thing in this room that
isn't picking up the phone.

here is what is true on thursday morning. antonio is dead.
antonio has been dead since monday. it has been four mornings.
each morning has been a kind of morning that is the morning
after the morning before. monday morning was the morning of.
tuesday morning was the morning after the morning of.
wednesday morning was a different kind because by wednesday
morning the bar opens at four pm and i had work to do. now
it's thursday and the bar is closed today (i closed it. nobody
told me to. i closed it.) and i don't have work to do and i
have to write something.

here is what is true about monday. alberto called nineteen
times. i did not pick up. i did not pick up because i thought
i knew what he was calling about. he was calling because he
had heard about quentin's call to the rectory or quentin's
call to me or quentin's call to antonio, or some chain that
goes through the parish that goes through me eventually, and
alberto was calling to tell me which call to expect or which
call he had made or which call he had heard about. i knew the
shape of it. i thought i knew the shape of it.

what alberto was actually calling about was that the
scaffolding wasn't going to hold and antonio was on his way
to me to look at it. that's nineteen calls. nineteen times he
tried to tell me. i had the receiver in my hand twice. i put
it back twice. i did not pick up because i thought i knew the
shape and i did not want the shape repeated to me. i did not
want the work of having heard the shape twice. that is what
not picking up was about. it was not about ignoring alberto.
it was about not having to be the one who knew.

i knew. i know now and i knew then. i knew on call seven and
i didn't pick up. i knew on call thirteen and i didn't pick
up. by call nineteen there wasn't anything to know anymore
because antonio was already at the scaffolding. so the calls
after seven were alberto telling himself he was still trying.

i do not have a way to fix this. i am writing this so that
when i open the bar again — and i will open it again, i will
have to, that's the chapter — i will know that i am the
person who knew on call seven and didn't pick up. the bar
will be open and i will be behind it and the middle barstool
will be where the middle barstool is and i will have written
this. that is the only difference i can make. the bar will be
the bar and i will be the man who knew and i will have written
it down. that is the chapter i am in.

                                                       — D.
```

The script does:
- gives Daigle a working interior voice. The chapter shows
  the bar; the script shows the proprietor on his closed-bar
  morning, with the bulb off.
- canonizes that Daigle KNEW on call seven. He did not pick up
  not because he was indifferent but because he didn't want to
  be the one who knew. The deck's bondage rendered as the
  weight of a knowing already arrived.
- canonizes the bar as a chapter Daigle has to keep being in.
  "i will open it again. i will have to." UNCOMFORTABLE INSIDE
  in the status panel is a name for the position he can't leave.
- ends on Thursday morning. Tower (XVI) is Thursday — the
  render queue crashes that day, before sunset. Daigle's note
  is hours before the Tower falls. The Tower's lightning had
  to travel from somewhere; the Devil's amber bulb is one
  candidate, the Devil's amber bulb on its proprietor's bad
  morning is a more specific one.
- signed "— D." — Daigle's name uncertain. (Some readers will
  read it as Dante. The deck permits the read; the chapter
  refuses to confirm.)
