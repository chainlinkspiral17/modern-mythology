# VOL 6 · PLANNED COMMUNITY · Wiki

The synthesizing entry point for vol 6 of *Modern Mythology* — the
**Planned Community** volume. The vol 5 cast is here in middle age
or older; new young leads carry the volume's primary POV;
NexCorp Residential Solutions and the substrate run beneath
everything. The gallery game **COMMUNITY PLANNED** sits in the
seam of the book's summer.

## What vol 6 is

The volume set in **Harmony Creek Estates** — a planned community
on the Gulf Coast, somewhere between Beaumont and the rural land
the Daigle family came from. Cosmic Comics in the strip mall.
NexCorp Gas & Go across the intersection from the Kwik Stop. The
zine-and-mixtape scene of the wider suburban-Gulf-Coast region.

The volume's structural shape is a single summer split into two
parts:

- **Summer's Start** (part one) — the opening half, ending on a
  structural seam where the book's gallery unlock appears.
- **End of Summer Begins** (part two) — the closing half, picking
  up after the seam.

The middle is held, in the book, as a structural pause. The
gallery game **COMMUNITY PLANNED** fills that pause; readers can
play it at the seam, save it for later, play concurrently with
part two, or skip it entirely (see `_COMMUNITY_PLANNED_LORE.md`).

Tonal register: **clipped suburban paranoia**. Sincere, slightly
funny, slightly unsettling. Air-conditioned year-round by corporate
decree. The texture of zines, mixtapes, comix, drugstore Polaroids,
ceramic frogs, glossy NexCorp newsletters. Lynch-meets-zine-scene.

## Locations

### Harmony Creek Estates · the engineered community

- **Phase I** — the original 1991 lots. Connie Daigle at Lot 7.
  Mrs. Pimentel at Lot 14. The most settled phase.
- **Phase II** — under occupancy in the volume's window. Carl
  Reno's welcome breakfasts. New buyers from Graustark and
  Beaumont arriving monthly.
- **Phase III** — dirt and surveying flags. Norman Lott's modular
  trailer. NexCorp Voice's vague phrase about "honoring the
  historic significance of the ground beneath the development by
  incorporating selected period elements into the master plan" —
  the substrate language, half-coded, half-corporate.
- **The model home at Lot 47** — Carla Vega lives nearby. The
  basil pot in the model home's kitchen window is *fake*
  (Mackenzie's slow-witness intel from COMMUNITY PLANNED W5).
  Carla's real basil is three doors down.
- **The HOA welcome table and office.**
- **The community pool.**
- **892 Ashberry Drive** — the empty house Maya has been watching
  for a year. Owned by a holding company. Maintained but
  unoccupied. Maya's *zine #19* documents it.

## The artifact registers

Per `planned_community/_README.md`, five categories of
accumulation across the cast. Each character has them in different
proportions:

- **ZINES** — photocopied, stapled, sold by hand at Cosmic Comics
  or through the manila envelope at the Kwik Stop. Cosmic Comics'
  paper stock is the regional signature.
- **COMIX** — underground-comix register. Pseudonymous attribution
  common. Maya's *GENERAL STORE*. Diego's *PHASE III* (the most
  prescient). Rick's *THE BEAUMONT BARGAINER* (the most carefully
  unfinished).
- **MIXTAPES** — Maxell XLII cassettes, 90 minutes. Hand-cut case
  inserts. The Gallatin Band's unreleased fourth-record track
  appears on three separate mixes — Diego's last, Maya's "for
  dad," Sam's "for the 3 PM shift" — establishing the bassist as
  a quiet network.
- **DETRITUS** — the *suburban entertainment accumulation*. Junk
  drawers, closet boxes, the wire basket at the Kwik Stop
  register. Ticket stubs, drumsticks, Polaroids, ceramic frogs,
  lottery tickets, loyalty cards, butcher-paper maps, manila
  envelopes.
- **CHAPTER-SPECIFIC OBJECTS** — Rick's phone ledger. Skip's 1999
  mixtape never given. Maya's grandfather's funeral-parlor inkwell.
  Diego's eleven NexCorp badge clips. Sam's license-plate
  notebook.

## The Glitch Report

The 1988 collective's zine, documented in
`planned_community/glitch_report.md`. The collective's surviving
members live on the Oregon coast by vol 7 (see Mrs. Gable, Otis
Vandermeer, Sister Kell, Doc Reyes — all named in the COMMUNITY
PLANNED canon and in `milk_and_honey/_README.md`'s reserved list).

The Glitch Report documents *the substrate's first audible
appearance*. By vol 6 the report has been quietly circulating via
Cosmic Comics' Henderson Donation boxes. Maya's *zine #11* (Vol 1,
No. 4) is an extension of the original collective's work.

The substrate is also documented in vol 7 (`milk_and_honey/`); it
is the cross-volume artifact, stable for 37 years, becoming
unstable in 2025 — *concurrent with COMMUNITY PLANNED's summer*.

## COMMUNITY PLANNED · vol 6's gallery game

The gallery game that fills the seam between *Summer's Start* and
*End of Summer Begins*. Unlocked when the reader finishes part
one; available permanently thereafter. Three reading paths all
valid (read-then-play, play-at-seam, read-and-play-concurrently,
or skip-entirely).

Three docs in this directory:

- **`_COMMUNITY_PLANNED_LORE.md`** — narrative scope, characters,
  Frasier's middle-aged characterization, Dean as transworld
  traveller (off-screen), Aria Dean as the anticipated coming, the
  named cast invented for the self-contained summer episode.
- **`_COMMUNITY_PLANNED_SPEC.md`** — mechanics. The three-region
  strategic map (Graustark / HCE / Small Wood OR), the BBS layer,
  the demon and human agent economies, the conversion mechanic,
  the interlude shelf, soft-fail throughout.
- **`_COMMUNITY_PLANNED_WEEKLY.md`** — the 14-week scenario
  scaffold. Memorial Day to Labor Day. Each week documents
  strategic events, BBS focal point, mechanic introduced, named
  cast moves.

The game is **a player-canon experience** for vol 6 — equally
canonical to the novel, in the multiverse-of-experiences framing
the saga uses across all ten volumes.

## Themes

- **Normalcy becoming something else.** The central horror move.
  Ordinary contractors, ordinary HOA boards, ordinary neighbors
  drifting half a degree toward Dean's interest without knowing
  they have.
- **The medium and the medium.** The ceramic frog as a 40-year
  distribution. The 47 Hz hum under every receiver's audio. The
  fact that things in this volume are *carried* — by mail, by
  cassette, by photocopy, by hand — rather than transmitted.
- **The young recognizing what the elders don't quite name.** Maya
  recognizing the wax paper. Sam recognizing the back-cooler
  customer's reflection. Diego recognizing the eleven badge clips.
- **The migration.** People leaving Graustark for HCE; some
  continuing to Oregon. The vol 6 cast is the *staying* layer; the
  COMMUNITY PLANNED-delivered seedlings are the *going* layer.
- **The substrate's first visible cracks.** The 1988-stable
  phenomenon misbehaving as the volume opens. The destabilization
  reaches its consequences in vol 7.
