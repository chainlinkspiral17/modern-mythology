## ACT — THE PERIMETER  (00:24 — 21:42)

EXT. STATE HIGHWAY 23 — EARLY EVENING — HANDHELD FROM PASSENGER SEAT.

The camera is held at lap level. The bottom third of the frame is dashboard — gray vinyl, the round dial of an analog tachometer, the corner of a cassette deck with no tape in it. Above the dashboard, through the windshield: a two-lane country road, golden hour, the right-hand shoulder thick with summer grass.

Ahead, at a distance of approximately two hundred yards, is a black PICKUP TRUCK. The truck is a long-bed Ford, model uncertain (late nineties), with Louisiana plates. The plates are legible at certain magnifications; the production has not redacted them. The back window of the truck is tinted dark enough that we cannot see the driver's silhouette.

Voice-over does not begin. The audio is the road audio, the car's engine, the wind around the camera's microphone, and the 60 Hz hum riding under all of it.

We follow.

For ninety seconds, we just drive.

The pickup slows, slightly, and turns right onto a county road marked **CR 408**.

We turn right.

CR 408 is paved for the first half mile, then transitions to chip-seal. The shoulder is gravel. We pass a small white sign reading **HARMONY CREEK ESTATES — A NEXCORP COMMUNITY · 4 MI.** with a directional arrow pointing forward. The pickup does not slow. We follow.

We drive for one minute fifty seconds.

The pickup turns left onto an unmarked road. The road is unpaved. The camera's audio picks up the texture change — tires on dirt, gravel kicking against the wheel wells.

We turn left.

The unmarked road runs along the western perimeter of a wire-fenced property. The fence is high — eight feet — and bears no signage. Beyond the fence: open pasture, low scrub, a windbreak of poplar at the far edge. The pickup drives the perimeter at approximately twenty miles an hour. We follow at thirty yards.

The pickup does not stop.

CUT — 0.6-second black bar — 02:48.

The pickup is now at a different point on the perimeter. The fence is the same fence. We are still following. The sun has dropped one degree. The audio is the same.

We drive for two minutes.

The pickup arrives at a closed wooden gate set into the wire fence. The gate is marked, in faded white paint on weathered wood: **HALSEY · PRIVATE.**

The pickup slows. The pickup does not stop. The pickup turns right and continues along the perimeter.

We follow.

CUT — 0.6-second black bar — 05:22.

A new perimeter. A new fence — wire mesh now, lower, four feet. The property beyond is platted in rectangular lots. Survey stakes with bright orange tape are visible at lot corners. A large white sign at the far end of the visible fence reads **FUTURE PHASE III — HARMONY CREEK ESTATES — OCCUPANCY 2027.**

The pickup drives slowly along this fence.

It does not stop.

We follow.

CUT — 0.6-second black bar — 07:48.

The pickup is back at HARMONY CREEK ESTATES sign at the entrance to CR 408. We have made one full loop of an apparent route. The pickup pauses at the sign — for the first time, a complete stop — for nine seconds.

We pause behind it, at distance.

The pickup begins again. It turns left onto CR 408. We follow.

CUT — 0.6-second black bar — 08:21.

We are following the pickup down CR 408 again. The second iteration of the perimeter route.

The audio, the road, the angle, the speed — everything is the same as the first iteration. The light has dropped marginally. The shadows from the poplar windbreak are now slightly longer.

We drive the same route. We turn at the same turns. We pass the same HALSEY gate. The pickup performs the same nine-second pause at the HARMONY CREEK ESTATES entrance sign.

We pause behind it.

We follow.

CUT — 0.6-second black bar — 14:01.

The third iteration.

The light has dropped further. The poplar windbreak's shadow now reaches across the unmarked road.

The pickup passes the HALSEY gate. It does not pause. It does not slow.

On this pass — frame 7:14 of the third iteration's HALSEY-gate moment — the pickup driver's right hand becomes briefly visible. It rests on the top of the steering wheel. The hand is large. The cuff at the wrist is dark. The wrist bears a faded band of skin a shade lighter than the rest of the hand — a watch, recently removed.

The hand is in frame for approximately two and a half seconds.

The episode does not zoom. The episode does not slow. The episode does not freeze. The episode does not draw attention.

We follow.

CUT — 0.6-second black bar — 17:32.

The fourth iteration is beginning. The pickup turns onto CR 408.

This time, when we approach the HARMONY CREEK ESTATES entrance sign, we do not turn with the pickup.

The camera holds straight on the road ahead.

The pickup turns left. The pickup proceeds along its route.

We — the camera — continue straight on CR 408. Past the entrance. Past the sign. We hear the pickup's engine recede behind us, on its perimeter route, going where the pickup goes.

The camera does not turn. The camera does not look back.

We drive for two minutes thirty seconds. The road is straight, mostly empty, golden hour deepening into blue. A single oncoming car passes at 19:54 — a white sedan, driver's face visible only as a pale oval, no front plate. The sedan does not slow.

At 20:48, the road forks. The right fork is unmarked. The left fork bears a small green county sign reading **GLEN PARK — 14 MI.**

The camera takes the right fork.

We drive for fifty seconds along the unmarked fork.

The road begins to curve.

At 21:38, just before the curve closes the line of sight behind us, the camera tilts — for the first and only time in the episode — toward the side mirror.

In the side mirror, two miles back, the black pickup is visible. It has emerged from CR 408 and turned onto the unmarked fork. It is, again, behind us. The headlights are on now. The headlights match the sedan's from earlier.

The camera tilts back to the road.

We drive into the curve.

CUT — 0.6-second black bar — 21:42.

## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- I do not drive. I have not driven since I was nineteen. the operator-of-record for episode VII is therefore me-in-the-passenger-seat; the driver of our car is uncredited and remains uncredited.
- the pickup is the pickup. I did not coordinate it. I waited, on three separate evenings, at the entrance to CR 408 with the camera ready. on the third evening, the pickup arrived and I followed it. the driver became aware of us during the second iteration of the perimeter — visible in the side-mirror reflection of his own mirror, on frame 4:38 of iteration two. he chose to continue the route rather than confront. he chose to permit the documentation.
- the watchless wrist on the third iteration is the only image of his body in the episode. I do not know who he is. RES_SOL_VERIFY in the comments will note the lack of redaction. I have not redacted. the wrist is admissible.
- the decision to not turn at iteration four was made by my driver, not me. I did not direct it. I would have followed the fourth iteration to its end. my driver chose to break off. she said, later, that she was tired of going in circles. the episode honors her decision by ending at the curve.
- the side-mirror tilt at 21:38 is the episode's only operator-decision. I made it because I had to know whether he was still there. he was.
- the white sedan with no front plate at 19:54 is the sedan that appears in episode 0 (THE FOOL). same sedan. same driver. RES_SOL_VERIFY has noted the recurrence in two separate threads and has not been answered.
- the curve closes on a road I have driven exactly twice in my life. the first time, I was a passenger and so was someone I have not seen since. the second time, was filming this episode. there is no third time scheduled. the curve is the closure I am willing to film.
