## ACT I — THE ROOM  (00:24 — 06:00)

INT. LAW OFFICE — DEPOSITION ROOM B — DAY — WIDE.

A small interior room. No windows. One long table. Six high-backed chairs. A side table against the wall holding a pitcher of water, a stack of inverted glasses, and a small audio console connected to a microphone on a goose-neck stand at the head of the long table. The audio console has six channel sliders, a tape deck, and a small VU meter. The VU meter is dark.

The room's overhead lighting is fluorescent, dimmed to half by a dial on the wall.

A woman — late twenties, ERICA CAMPBELL, although the deck does not yet permit naming her in the credits — enters. She wears a charcoal pencil skirt and a white blouse. She is the firm's court reporter; the firm has not yet hired the second associate; she does the job alone on Sundays when the records require Sunday work.

She carries her stenotype machine in a hard case. She sets it on the long table.

She does not turn on the overhead.

She crosses to the side table. She lifts the pitcher. She fills one glass. She drinks half. She sets the glass beside her station.

She opens the stenotype case. She removes the machine. She sets it up. She unfurls the paper tape from its small spool. She tests three keys. The keys click.

She crosses to the audio console. She inserts a fresh blank cassette tape. She presses RECORD and PLAY together. The VU meter rises.

She returns to her station. She seats herself at the stenotype.

She places her hands on the keys.

She speaks into the room, for the record, as is her practice when filing alone.

ERICA
*(quiet, professional)*
This is Erica Campbell, court reporter, CAMPBELL & ASSOC., March third. The time is approximately 1:48 PM. I am preparing the transcript for the deposition of —

She pauses. She looks down at her notes. There are no notes on the table. She has not pulled the case file.

She speaks the next line carefully.

ERICA
*(slower)*
— the matter has not yet been opened. I am — I am here because the audio console activated this morning at 9:09 AM and the indicator was on when I came in for the unrelated Vega filing. The audio has been recording since 9:09. I am here to transcribe what is on the tape.

She presses STOP on the audio console. She rewinds the cassette to the beginning. She presses PLAY.

The audio console's small monitor speaker begins to play.

A voice speaks. The voice is firm, professional, slightly tired. The voice is also unmistakably ERICA's own voice, slightly older.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA, slightly older)*
Today's date is February nineteenth. The time is 10:14 AM. We are here in the matter of *In re: Estate of —* and the deposition of the witness, who has been sworn. Counsel, please state your appearances.

A different voice. A man, dry, careful.

MALE VOICE
For the estate, James Honaker, Honaker & Sons.

A third voice. A woman, calm.

FEMALE VOICE
For NexCorp Residential Solutions, Liane Garza.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA, slightly older)*
Thank you. The witness will state her full name for the record.

A pause.

WITNESS  *(an older woman, audibly weighing her words)*
My full name is Marta Carmen Romero.

ERICA — present-day, at the stenotype — stops typing.

She has been transcribing. Her hands are still on the keys. The paper tape has emerged from the machine with the testimony of the first three lines.

She removes her hands from the keys.

She looks at the audio console.

The tape continues to play.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA, slightly older)*
Mrs. Romero, do you understand that you are testifying under oath today.

MARTA
Yes.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA, slightly older)*
Mrs. Romero, please state your relationship to the decedent, Mr. Dante D'Ambrosio.

A long pause.

MARTA
I worked in his household for twenty-one years.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA, slightly older)*
And in what capacity.

MARTA
*(steady)*
Housekeeper and child-care, beginning 1985. Housekeeper and primary-care assistant after Mrs. D'Ambrosio left the household in 1991. Solo head-of-household staff from 1996 forward.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA)*
At any time during your employment were you paid less than what you were entitled to under federal or state minimum-wage law.

MARTA
Yes.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA)*
Throughout your employment.

MARTA
For most of my employment. I was paid the correct amount, on the record, for two periods. From 1985 through 1987, and again from 2003 through 2005.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA)*
Do you know why those two periods are different.

A long pause.

MARTA
1985 through 1987 was when the family's books were audited by an outside firm following an inheritance settlement. The family wanted no irregularity that would be visible to that audit. The audit ended. The pay returned to the previous level.

A second pause.

MARTA
2003 through 2005 was after the Sinkhole.

ERICA — present-day, at the stenotype — closes her eyes.

She speaks, very quietly, to herself.

ERICA
*(soft)*
Oh.

The tape continues.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA, slightly older)*
Mrs. Romero, can you describe what you mean by "after the Sinkhole."

MARTA
*(quiet)*
After the event in 1979 in which Mrs. D'Ambrosio's sister and several other employees of the family — and others not employed by the family — were lost.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA)*
Could you explain why the pay returned to lawful levels for those two years following.

MARTA
*(very quiet)*
The family had been advised by their attorney at the time that the records office, which had previously suppressed the irregularity, might be reviewed.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA)*
The records office.

MARTA
*(after a beat)*
The D'Ambrosio Household Archive. The records office that the family kept at the time.

A small pause. The voice from the console — the slightly-older ERICA — does not respond immediately. The tape continues to roll, recording the small silence.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA, slightly older, careful)*
Mrs. Romero, do you know the name of the records-office employee whose review was anticipated.

A long pause.

MARTA
*(very quiet)*
I knew her. She was a young woman. She left the office before any review took place.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA)*
Did she communicate to you any reason for her leaving.

MARTA
She did not. She left without notice. The family did not pursue her departure.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA)*
Do you know where she went.

A pause.

MARTA
*(after a beat)*
I know that she eventually began a different kind of work, which I do not believe is relevant to this deposition.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA, slightly older, gentle)*
Mrs. Romero, you may decide what is and is not relevant.

A small laugh from MARTA. A real laugh, dry.

MARTA
Thank you, dear. That's good of you.

PRESENT-DAY ERICA, at the stenotype, opens her eyes. She is half-smiling at the laugh.

The tape continues to play, but the dialogue grows quieter — gentler — and the audio console's VU meter dips lower than its previous range.

ERICA — present-day — looks at the VU meter for a long moment.

She presses STOP.

She rewinds the tape — only by a half-inch.

She presses PLAY.

The half-inch contains seven seconds of audio.

VOICE FROM THE CONSOLE  *(ERICA)*
You may decide what is and is not relevant.

MARTA
*(laughing softly)*
Thank you, dear.

ERICA presses STOP.

She does not press PLAY again.

She sits at the stenotype for a long beat.

## ACT II — THE FILE THAT IS NOT YET A FILE  (06:00 — 12:30)

INT. DEPOSITION ROOM B — CONTINUOUS.

ERICA rises. She crosses to the door. She opens it.

INT. LAW OFFICE — RECEPTION AREA.

A small reception. A desk for a receptionist who has not yet been hired. A waiting bench. A coffee urn. Two abstract paintings on the wall above the bench. The wall behind reception — the wall the deck does not film — has a row of locked filing cabinets. The cabinets are all dark wood. None of them have labels.

ERICA does not look at the cabinets. She crosses to the receptionist's desk. She removes a small ring of keys from the top drawer. She returns to the deposition room.

INT. DEPOSITION ROOM B.

She crosses to a low cabinet under the side table — the side table with the water pitcher, the inverted glasses, the audio console. The cabinet is also locked. She tries three keys. The fourth key fits. She opens the cabinet.

The cabinet is empty.

ERICA stares into the empty cabinet for a long beat.

ERICA
*(small, dry, to herself)*
Of course.

She removes a small white envelope from her pocket. She has been carrying it the entire episode. The envelope is unsealed. She removes from it a single sheet of paper. She unfolds it. She places it in the empty cabinet. She closes the cabinet. She turns the key. She returns to her station.

The cabinet has been seeded.

ERICA at the stenotype. She takes a fresh roll of paper from her case. She replaces the spool. She rewinds the existing tape on the audio console. She begins a new transcription session.

ERICA
*(into the microphone, calm)*
This is Erica Campbell, court reporter, CAMPBELL & ASSOC. The date is March third. The time is 2:32 PM. I am preparing a memorandum to file, not a transcript. The memorandum reads as follows.

She begins to type. The stenotype clicks.

ERICA
The audio console in deposition room B activated at 9:09 AM today without intervention. The audio console contained the audio of a deposition that has not yet been scheduled in any matter currently before this firm. The deposition was of Marta Carmen Romero. The opposing counsel was Liane Garza for NexCorp Residential Solutions. The matter was unstated but appears to involve the estate of Mr. Dante D'Ambrosio. The audio appears to be of me, the undersigned, at a later date, in a different chair. The audio identifies my voice. The audio is consistent with my voice. The audio addresses Mrs. Romero with a tone of professional care that I have, to date, only practiced in private. The audio confirms what I had not yet been told but had begun to suspect: that the Sinkhole event of 1979 produced an internal household record that the family suppressed for two distinct intervals corresponding to anticipated audits.

She pauses. She listens to her own typing.

ERICA
*(continuing, quieter)*
The audio cannot be authenticated to my satisfaction. It is also, however, not falsifiable to my satisfaction. The audio's existence is the chapter's information; the audio's content is the chapter's information; the audio's having been recorded before any matter was scheduled is the chapter's information. I am, in filing this memorandum, acknowledging receipt of the chapter.

She stops typing. She breathes.

ERICA
*(slowly, finishing)*
The cabinet beneath the side table has been seeded with a single sheet of paper. The paper contains the name of the young woman the household-archive employee was, the year she left the office, and the address at which she may presently be reached. The paper is in my hand. The cabinet is locked. The key has been returned. The seed is in. The chapter will find what it finds.

She presses STOP on the audio console.

She removes the recording cassette.

She labels the cassette in pen: **CAMPBELL — MEMO — MARCH 3 — DR B.**

She places the cassette in her pocket.

## ACT III — THE WAX  (12:30 — 17:00)

INT. DEPOSITION ROOM B — LATE AFTERNOON.

ERICA at the stenotype.

She has not transcribed for thirty-six minutes.

She has been sitting at the deposition table, hands flat on the wood, listening.

The room has been silent except for the 60 Hz hum and, very faintly, a sound from outside the room — a small mechanical click from one of the locked cabinets on the wall behind reception. A click, then silence. Then another click.

ERICA rises.

She crosses to the door of the deposition room. She opens it. She steps into reception.

INT. RECEPTION.

The clicking has stopped.

The locked cabinets on the wall behind reception are unchanged.

ERICA approaches the cabinet at the far left.

She removes the key ring from her pocket. She tries the keys. None of them work.

She tries the next cabinet. None of them work.

She tries the next.

The fifth cabinet — third from the right — accepts the seventh key. The cabinet opens.

The cabinet contains:

- a single manila folder, unlabeled
- a small jar of dark wax, melted at some point in the past and re-solidified, with a wick still embedded in it
- a small silver seal-press, the seal-face down so we cannot see its imprint
- a folded brown paper envelope, no postmark, no address

ERICA looks at the manila folder. She does not open it.

She picks up the jar of dark wax. She holds it briefly. The wax is honey-colored — darker than store-bought honey, with the particular reddish tint of HALSEY FARMS WILDFLOWER. The wax has been previously used to seal an envelope. The wax has been melted off the envelope and re-solidified into the jar, as one would do if one were preserving the wax of a particular seal for re-use.

She returns the wax to the cabinet. She picks up the seal-press. She turns it over. The seal-face shows a small device — a stylized P interlocked with a stylized H.

She does not know the device. She has not seen it before.

She returns the seal-press to the cabinet. She closes the cabinet. She locks the cabinet. She returns the key ring to the receptionist's drawer.

She returns to deposition room B.

INT. DEPOSITION ROOM B.

She sits at the stenotype.

She places her hands on the keys.

She does not type.

She speaks into the microphone for the last time in the episode.

ERICA
*(quiet, careful)*
Off the record. This is Erica. I am not transcribing this. I am addressing whoever finds this tape, which I will leave on the audio console rather than carrying it with me.

A long beat.

ERICA
The seal in the cabinet I will not name is the seal of a society I do not know. The society's seal has been re-used. The wax has been re-melted. Whoever last used it intended to use it again. I am, at present, not sure whether the cabinet is being maintained by someone associated with the firm or by someone with access to the firm. The lock on the cabinet was a lock I had not yet inventoried. The fact that one of my keys opened it is the information.

She breathes.

ERICA
I am filing this off-the-record cassette beside the on-the-record memo cassette. The two cassettes will be in the same desk drawer. If you are listening to this, you may make of the two of them what you will. I have decided to leave them both. I do not yet know the case I have just heard the deposition of. I do not yet know whether I will, on the future date, conduct the deposition or have conducted it. I do not yet know whether the audio is the future or the imagined future or the rehearsal of the future or the ghost of a future the deck has decided will be honored.

She pauses again.

ERICA
*(softly)*
I have decided to live as if the audio is correct. I have decided to live as if I am the court reporter in the matter of *In re: D'Ambrosio* and the witness Marta Carmen Romero. I have decided to do the small work of preparation that this commitment requires. I have seeded the cabinet beneath the side table. I have catalogued the seal. I have listened to my own voice in the future. I have decided to be the woman who treats Mrs. Romero with the tone I heard myself use.

She stops talking.

She presses STOP on the audio console.

She removes the cassette.

She labels it: **CAMPBELL — OFF RECORD — MARCH 3 — DR B.**

She places both cassettes in the desk drawer at her station.

She closes the drawer.

## ACT IV — THE STRIP MALL  (17:00 — 24:36)

EXT. HARMONY CREEK ESTATES STRIP MALL — LATE AFTERNOON.

ERICA exits the firm. She locks the door behind her. She steps onto the sidewalk under the awning.

The strip mall is a long single-story block with five storefronts. From left:

- **SALLY BEAUTY**
- **COSMIC COMICS**
- **CAMPBELL & ASSOC. · ATTORNEYS** (the firm she has just left)
- **GREAT CLIPS**
- **HARMONY CREEK NUTRITION** (a small storefront with a hand-lettered sign)

ERICA looks up the row. Then she looks down. Then she begins to walk toward her car at the far end of the parking lot.

She passes COSMIC COMICS.

Inside COSMIC COMICS, through the front window, an OLDER MAN stands at the counter looking at the door — at her — as if he has been expecting her for a long time. He is in his sixties. He has on a graphic T-shirt and a denim jacket. He has a phone receiver pressed to his ear. He is not speaking into it.

When she looks at him, he does not move.

She does not stop walking. She raises her hand in a small wave.

He raises his — slowly — and waves back.

She walks on.

She reaches her car. She gets in. She does not start the engine.

She sits in the driver's seat for a long beat.

She removes the two cassettes from her pocket. She had taken them after labeling them and after filing them in the drawer. The drawer is empty. The drawer has the cassettes. She has, somehow, both: the duplicate is in her pocket.

She looks at the duplicates.

She holds them in her lap.

The 60 Hz hum, audible through the car's faint exterior leakage, continues.

A black pickup truck with Louisiana plates pulls slowly through the lot at 23:48. It does not park. It drives down the row of storefronts. It pauses, briefly, in front of CAMPBELL & ASSOC. It does not stop. It continues to the end of the lot. It turns right onto Linden Boulevard.

ERICA watches the pickup pass.

She does not start her engine.

She closes her eyes.

ERICA
*(very quiet, to no one)*
Yes.

She opens her eyes.

She turns the key. The engine starts. She backs out of her space. She drives slowly out of the lot, in the opposite direction the pickup went.

The camera holds on the empty parking space.

The OLDER MAN in COSMIC COMICS, through the window, sets the receiver down on its cradle. He returns to his stool behind the counter. He picks up a book.

The camera continues to hold for fourteen seconds.

Cut.

## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- the firm is real. CAMPBELL & ASSOC. opened in the Harmony Creek strip mall in the year of this episode's filming. ERICA was, at the time of filming, the firm's sole employee — she had not yet hired a receptionist, a paralegal, or a second associate. she opened the office on her own with money from her late father's estate. her father had practiced in Beaumont. her father's name is not in the firm's title.
- ERICA had not, at the time of filming, taken on the case the audio describes. the case had not been opened. the witness had not been deposed. the opposing counsel — LIANE GARZA for NexCorp — had not, to ERICA's knowledge, been retained. NexCorp had not yet been named as a party in any matter ERICA had touched.
- the audio in the console at 9:09 AM was the audio. I did not place the audio. ERICA did not place the audio. the audio is on the cassette. the cassette was sealed and dated by manufacture three years prior. the cassette had not been opened. the cassette was opened that morning by the audio console itself, which I have inspected and which has no automatic loading mechanism. the cassette is, by physical inspection, what it claims to be — an unused TDK D-90 dated to 2020 by manufacturer's batch code. its contents are the deposition that, to my best knowledge, will be conducted in February of next year. I cannot confirm a deposition that has not yet occurred. I can confirm that the audio is consistent with ERICA's voice and MARTA's voice as both presently exist.
- MARTA, on hearing the audio after I delivered her a small cassette player and a copy of the tape, said only: *"that is what I will tell her. I am pleased that I have already told her. perhaps she has already heard."* she has been ill since the date of this episode. she expects, she has said, to be in the ward by the late winter. she is glad to have already given her testimony.
- the wall behind reception is a wall I have not filmed. the wall behind reception holds, in eleven locked cabinets, the documentary materials of approximately four dozen open files that ERICA's firm has accepted. one of the four dozen files is the Vega file. ERICA was processing the Vega file on the morning of the audio's first activation. the Vega file is reserved. it has, since the date of this episode's airing, been transferred to the firm's secure off-site storage. I do not know its contents. ERICA does not know its contents. ERICA inherited the file in the original cabinet when she took over the firm's lease; the file was already there. she has not opened it. she has decided not to open it until the cabinet itself tells her she should.
- the seal in the unnumbered cabinet — P interlocked with H — has not been identified by any heraldic authority I have been able to consult. RES_SOL_VERIFY in the comments has not addressed the seal. I have come to believe the seal belongs to a private society whose footprint includes Beaumont, Westport, Lake Charles, and Harmony Creek, and whose primary surviving documentation method is the re-use of wax. the wax in the jar is HALSEY FARMS wildflower honey-wax that has been previously used to seal at least three documents that ERICA's predecessor had filed before vacating the firm. I do not know what the documents were. I do not know who the predecessor was.
- the OLDER MAN in COSMIC COMICS is RICK. he is real. he runs the comic shop at the Harmony Creek strip mall. he has been there for twenty-four years. he received calls on his store's landline from the "gentleman from Beaumont" beginning in his second year of operation; the calls have continued at intervals of between two and seven weeks ever since. RICK has said publicly, in his own comment under episode V (THE HIEROPHANT), that he answers the calls because he is "supposed to" and that he does not know what he is supposed to do after the answering. he has, since this episode aired, replied to a private email I sent him acknowledging the encounter with ERICA. his reply read in full: *"she is going to be a good lawyer. tell her the seal is mine to use until i hand it on. — r."* he has not handed it on, at the time of this writing.
- the duplicate cassettes in ERICA's pocket at the end of the episode were not duplicates I supplied. she filed the cassettes in the drawer. she did not take them. she then, somehow, also had them in her pocket. I have asked her about this. she has told me: *"the cassettes that are in the drawer are in the drawer. the cassettes in my pocket are different cassettes. I have not opened them."* I have not asked further. she still has not opened them. she has, by her account, kept them in the bottom drawer of her bedroom dresser since the episode's filming.
- the chapter at XI is the chapter of weighing. tradition shows scales held by a blindfolded figure. this episode renders the weighing as a court reporter alone in a small interior room, transcribing a deposition that has not yet happened, listening to her own voice from the future, and choosing — quietly — to make her present self into the voice the future already has. that is the chapter's justice. it is also the deck's reading of how good lawyers are made. ERICA, watching this episode at private screening eight months later, said: *"I want to be the woman on the tape. I think I am. I think she is also me."* she signed an affidavit attesting to her presence in the scenes for the firm's records. she has not, to date, been deposed in any matter involving the D'Ambrosio estate. the chapter is patient.
