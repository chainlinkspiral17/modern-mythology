## ACT I — THE LIGHT  (00:24 — 07:00)

INT. FRANK'S OFFICE — SATURDAY MORNING — 7:48 AM.

A small white-walled office. A single east-facing window. The window has no curtains. The window is washed. The morning light comes in directly.

The room contains:

- a wooden desk facing the window
- a single straight-backed wooden chair behind the desk
- a small low table at the desk's right elbow holding a brass desk lamp, off, and a small ceramic teacup, unwashed from a previous morning, set aside
- on the desk: an open notebook, a pencil, a small thermos, an ordinary push-button telephone connected to a landline jack at the wall
- on the windowsill: a single small mason jar of Halsey Apiaries wildflower honey, partly used
- on the wall above the desk: a round analog clock with a subdial. the subdial reads, in Frank's hand, **SATURDAY.**

FRANK is in the chair. He is in a fresh button-down, soft blue. His hair has been combed. He is wearing his glasses. He is awake. He has been up for an hour.

The east-facing window is full of morning. The light comes in at a low angle, raking across the desk. The light hits the small mason jar of honey on the windowsill. The jar refracts the light, briefly, into a small array of small refracted points on the wall above the desk.

FRANK has been waiting for this.

In the slant of the light, four dust motes are visible.

The motes are at approximately the desk's height. The motes are approximately two feet apart. The motes are not moving at first — they are nearly stationary in the slant. Then, gradually, they begin to drift. They drift in a slow rotation, each on its own orbit, around an axis that is not visible.

FRANK watches.

He has not moved.

He has been watching for ninety seconds before he reaches, slowly, for the pencil.

He picks it up.

He opens the notebook to a fresh page.

He writes the date.

He writes, beneath the date, four small marks — four dots arranged in a square.

He sets the pencil down.

He continues to watch.

The motes continue to orbit.

## ACT II — THE COUNT  (07:00 — 12:00)

The clock advances. The light's angle changes by small degrees. The slant moves across the desk, gradually, toward the chair.

FRANK watches the motes for the full first act of his Saturday morning.

At 8:12, he reaches for the pencil again. He makes one small notation beside the four dots.

The notation, visible briefly over his shoulder, reads:

> *four. same as last week. same as the week before.*

He sets the pencil down.

A small breeze, somewhere outside, moves through the window seal — barely. The motes drift, slightly, in response. They do not scatter. They return to their orbit. The orbit is robust.

At 8:24, FRANK lifts his right hand from his lap. He extends his right index finger toward the nearest mote. He does not touch it. His fingertip stops approximately an inch from the mote's position. He holds the finger there for nine seconds.

The mote does not respond. It continues its drift.

FRANK lowers his hand.

He returns it to his lap.

At 8:38, the light's slant has moved to the edge of the desk and is beginning to climb the side of the chair. The motes have begun to drift downward as the slant lowers them.

FRANK reaches for the pencil again. He makes a second notation beside the four dots.

> *they are not affected by the breath. I tested.*

He sets the pencil down.

At 8:48, the light's slant is climbing across his shoulder. The motes are now near his chest. He has not moved.

A wren outside the window calls four times in succession. The notes are bright. FRANK listens.

At 8:54, the four motes — for the first time in any of his Saturday observations — perform a coordinated movement. They contract their orbit, slightly, drawing closer to their common axis. Then they expand. Then they contract. The motion is rhythmic, slow, and lasts approximately ninety seconds.

FRANK watches.

He does not reach for the pencil.

He does not reach for the phone.

He watches.

At 8:57, the motes return to their original orbit.

FRANK lifts his right hand. He places it, palm-down, on the desk. He breathes.

The clock advances to 9:00.

---

## ACT III — THE CALL  (12:00 — 17:30)

At 9:08, FRANK reaches for the telephone.

He picks up the receiver.

He dials a number from memory. Seven digits.

He waits.

A click. The other end picks up.

ELICIA'S VOICE  *(through the receiver, slightly tinny)*
This is Elicia.

FRANK
*(quiet, warm)*
Frank.

ELICIA
*(through the receiver, warm)*
Frank.

A pause.

FRANK
The motes were four this morning.

ELICIA
*(after a beat)*
Same arrangement?

FRANK
Same arrangement. Same orbit. They contracted at 8:54 and held for ninety seconds. They returned.

A long beat.

ELICIA
*(very quiet)*
That is the first time they have contracted.

FRANK
*(after a beat)*
Yes.

ELICIA
*(carefully)*
Thank you for calling.

I had to call.

ELICIA
I know.

Frank.

Yes.

The medium last night, in my apartment, told me that I should expect a phone call from you this morning at 9:09. it told me before the kettle. it said: *"frank will call at 9:09. the motes contracted today. the chapter has moved by one beat. you will know what to do."*

FRANK
*(after a long beat, quiet)*
Did it tell you what to do.

ELICIA

Okay.

*(softly, into the receiver)*
Frank.

Yes.

The chapter has moved by one beat. you are one of four. the other three will call within the year. have a good Saturday.

Thank you. you too.

ELICIA
*(softly)*
Goodbye, Frank.

FRANK
Goodbye, Elicia.

He sets the receiver in the cradle.

The call has lasted forty-two seconds.

He picks up the pencil. He makes a third notation beside the four dots in his notebook.

> *9:09 to elicia. she had been told. 9:09:42 close. the medium said the chapter has moved by one beat. one of four. three others will call.*

He sets the pencil down.

He looks at the motes.

The motes have continued their orbit through the call. They are now, with the light's slant having climbed his chest, at the level of his face. The motes are approximately two feet from his eyes.

He looks at them.

They do not contract again. They drift, calmly, in their slow rotation.

He breathes.

The wren outside calls again. Four times. Same pitch.

## ACT IV — THE OTHER THREE  (17:30 — 21:00)

The light's slant has now climbed past FRANK's face and is moving onto the wall behind the chair. The motes are following the slant upward. By 9:13 the motes are above his head.

FRANK rises, slowly.

He picks up the small ceramic teacup from the low table. He carries it to the small kitchenette adjacent to the office. He rinses it. He returns to the desk.

He pours warm chamomile from his thermos into the rinsed cup. He sets the cup on the desk.

He does not drink.

He returns his hands to his lap.

He watches the motes climb the wall.

At 9:18, the motes have reached the wall above the door. They drift, calmly, near the ceiling.

At 9:21, the slant of light moves past them. The motes settle. They are now in ordinary indirect light. They are no longer visible.

FRANK watches the place where they were.

The phone rings.

FRANK looks at the phone.

He picks up.

FRANK
*(quiet, warm)*
This is Frank.

A pause. The other end speaks. We hear, faintly, a different voice this time — a woman's, older, calm.

FRANK
*(after a beat)*
Casserole.

Goodbye, frank.

FRANK
Goodbye, casserole.

He sets the receiver in the cradle.

He looks at the notebook. He picks up the pencil. He makes a fourth notation.

> *9:21 call from c. evergreen. she counts four also. she has counted four for two weeks. the medium told her to call. we are one of four. two more will identify before year out.*

He caps the pencil. He closes the notebook.

He looks at the wall above the door, where the motes had been.

He picks up the chamomile.

He drinks. Three slow swallows.

He sets the cup down.

He looks at the camera for the first and only time in the episode.

He nods, once.

He rises. He crosses out of the office, toward the back of the suite.

The camera holds on the empty desk.

The notebook, closed. The pencil, capped. The teacup, half-finished. The thermos, set aside. The phone, in its cradle.

The clock advances to 9:24.

The light's slant continues to climb the wall.

The Saturday continues.

## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- FRANK has been counting the dust motes in the slant of the east-facing window of his office on Saturday mornings for approximately twenty-six years. he has counted, in that time, populations ranging from one to nine. he has, for approximately the past eleven years, counted four with steady consistency. he has, in his own private accounting, taken the four-count as a small signal. he has not, until this episode aired, told anyone other than me about the count.
- the four motes are not, by FRANK's own honest reading, anything more than four dust motes. dust motes drift in slants of light because air currents and Brownian motion exist. FRANK is not claiming otherwise. FRANK is also not pretending that the consistency of the count over eleven years is meaningless to him. the count is the chapter. the count is also literally a count of dust. both readings hold.
- the contraction at 8:54 has not, by FRANK's account, happened before. I asked him, after the call, whether he was sure. he said: *"I have been watching for twenty-six years on Saturdays at this hour at this window with these four motes for eleven of those years. they have not contracted. they contracted today."* I believe him.
- the call to me. the call happened. it was the same Saturday morning call we have had, intermittently, for some years — a small Saturday-morning exchange between two people who have decided to be quiet correspondents. it was, this Saturday, the call. the medium had, the previous night in my apartment in Mauriceville, told me to expect it. I have, since the call, double-checked my own notebook from the medium's broadcast that Friday. the broadcast had said precisely what I told FRANK on the call. I had not paraphrased. I had transcribed correctly. I had then closed the notebook and gone to bed and not consciously held the information until the phone rang on Saturday morning at 9:09.
- the call from CASSEROLE. she had been told by the medium, in her own apartment above the Evergreen Room, to call FRANK at 9:21. she did not, before this Saturday, know FRANK's number. the medium gave her the number. she dialed. the call was the call.
- the chapter has moved by one beat. the medium has told me, in three separate broadcasts since this Saturday's filming, what this means. I have transcribed the broadcasts. I have not, at present, decided how much of them to publish. the broadcasts are also, by their own claim, "the chapter's longer paragraph." the longer paragraph is not the chapter at XIX. the longer paragraph is the chapter that comes after. the broadcasts have said: *"the four are the four. they will gather. the chapter will be ready when they have gathered. you will know when the chapter is ready because the motes will contract again, in unison, in all four locations, at the same hour."* the four locations are FRANK's office, the EVERGREEN ROOM in New Orleans, an unnamed third location, and an unnamed fourth. I have not been told the unnamed two yet. I expect to be told.
- the chapter at XIX in the deck is the chapter of warmth. tradition shows a child on a horse beneath a smiling sun. this episode renders the chapter as a man at his desk on a Saturday morning watching four dust motes in a slant of light, calling a friend, receiving a call, drinking chamomile, and being one of four. warmth, in FRANK's reading and in the chapter's, is the discipline of paying close attention to small things until the small things acknowledge that you have been paying attention. the four motes acknowledged today. the chapter is held.
