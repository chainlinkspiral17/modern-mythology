## ACT II — THE BAND  (06:00 — 11:30)

The transistor radio on the side table is tuned, audibly, between stations on the AM band. The carrier signal it produces is the same WUFR pirate-radio signal that has run under episodes X (THE WHEEL OF FORTUNE) and XII (THE HANGED MAN). Tonight the carrier is steady.

Within the carrier, however, a second component has begun to ride — within the past three days, by the woman's notebook. The notebook is on the table beside her, open to a fresh page. We see, briefly, the page: a series of hand-drawn waveforms with timestamps in her hand. The most recent entry is dated three nights ago and reads, in pencil:

> *new carrier, 1.7 kHz above WUFR. weak. pulses approximately every nine minutes. duration variable, 5-9 sec. pitch consistent. origin: not yet triangulated.*

The hand reaches for the radio. She adjusts the dial, very slightly. She finds the new carrier. She isolates it.

The new carrier pulses. A small pulse of a single sustained pitch, lasting six seconds, repeats.

She listens.

The static on the CRT, meanwhile, has continued. The static is not connected to the radio. The static is the CRT's own.

ELICIA, in the chair, lifts the open notebook into her lap. She picks up the pencil. She makes a notation:

> *the static on the CRT alters in synchrony with the 1.7 kHz pulses. I had not previously observed this. the alteration is at the level of grain density. the grains are organized.*

She lowers the notebook.

She looks at the CRT.

The static on the CRT is, on careful examination, not random. The grains have, at the limit of the camera's resolution, the faint suggestion of a horizontal structure. The structure is consistent with text. The text is too small to read at normal viewing distance.

She rises from the chair.

She crosses to the CRT.

She kneels in front of it.

Her face is now approximately eight inches from the screen.

She watches.

The static is text. The text is in her own handwriting.

The handwriting is hers but is being written in real time, at a slow steady pace, scrolling upward through the static. The letters are formed of denser clusters of static grains. The white space between letters is the standard static. The page does not have a margin. The page has no beginning. The page has no end. The text is continuous.

She watches.

## ACT IV — THE LIGHTS  (17:00 — 22:30)

INT. APARTMENT — LATER.

ELICIA is back in the armchair. A fresh cup of tea. The CRT is showing ordinary static. The radio is tuned to the carrier; the carrier is quiet now.

She picks up her notebook. She reads the transcription. She turns the page.

She begins to write — a fresh page — what she has been preparing to write for some time.

> *I am one of the receivers. I have been receiving for nine years. I have written down, by my count, approximately one hundred and fourteen broadcasts. I have triangulated none of them. I have decided not to triangulate.*

> *I have, however, traveled to three places to verify what the broadcasts have told me about themselves. I have been to Marfa, Texas — to the viewing platform on Highway 67, nine miles east of town, where the Marfa Lights have been observed since at least 1883, and where I sat for three consecutive nights in 2016 with my own notebook open. the lights, on the third night, made a pattern. the pattern, when I drew it in my notebook, was the same pattern as one of the broadcasts I had received the previous month, in pencil, on this same notebook, in this same apartment.*
>
> *I have been to the abandoned radio station at the southwest edge of New Orleans East — the station that was operated by an independent religious broadcaster from 1962 to 1996 and which has, by my walk-up inspection, been completely empty since. the station has, however, an antenna that is still standing. the antenna is not connected to anything. the antenna is, by FCC database, deregistered. the antenna, on the night I visited in 2018, was nonetheless transmitting at very low power on a band that the FCC has not assigned. the transmission was the carrier I receive in this apartment.*

> *I have been to the Sabine River, fourteen miles east of this apartment, on the Texas-Louisiana state line. I have walked the boat ramp at Burr Ferry. I have listened to the cypress at night. ## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- the apartment is mine. the armchair is mine. the notebook is mine. the radio is mine. the CRT is mine. the kettle is mine. I have lived above the closed Sun-Lo Seafood Café in Mauriceville since 2014. Mauriceville is fourteen miles west of the Sabine River, the Texas-Louisiana state line, in Orange County, Texas. the apartment is one of two on the second floor. the other is vacant. the building's owner is a local family who runs a small body shop one door down and who lets me have the apartment at below-market rent in exchange for keeping an eye on the building. I have been keeping an eye on the building. I have also, in private, been receiving.
- the broadcasts are real, in the sense that I have been hand-transcribing them on Friday nights for nine years. I do not know who or what is broadcasting. I have a theory; I have several theories; I have decided that the theories are not the chapter. the chapter is the transcription. the chapter is the receiving. the chapter is the small private network of receivers who have, over the years, written to each other through the medium's postal route.
- the medium's postal route is real, in the sense that letters mailed through it reach their recipients. the route is not the U.S. Postal Service. the route is not a courier. the route is, by my best guess, a kind of distributed quiet courtesy among the receivers themselves — people who, having been told by the medium where to send a letter, do so, and people who, having been told by the medium that a letter is coming, watch for it. I have, by this method, received approximately forty letters from approximately twenty-three other receivers across the nine years. I have sent approximately the same number out. I have never met any of the other receivers in person, although three of them live within driving distance of Mauriceville and I have, on three occasions, driven near their homes without stopping. I respect the chapter's structure. the network is the network because nobody breaks the network's quiet.
- the Marfa Lights. I drove to the viewing platform on Highway 67 in March of 2016. the platform is real. it is approximately nine miles east of Marfa, Texas, in the Mitchell Flat area of the Big Bend region. the lights have been observed at the location since at least 1883, when a cattle rancher named Robert Reed Ellison was the first recorded settler to describe them. the lights are typically observed at the horizon — small, glowing, sometimes pulsing, sometimes drifting horizontally. mainstream explanations include refracted vehicle headlights from Highway 67 itself, atmospheric optical effects, and reflected starlight. independent investigators have noted that the lights are observable in conditions where these explanations are difficult to sustain. I sat at the platform for three nights. I observed the lights on the second and third nights. on the third night, the lights formed a pattern. the pattern was the same as the pattern I had transcribed in this apartment three weeks earlier, in pencil, from the medium's broadcast. I do not know what to do with this fact. I have decided to record it and let it sit.
- the abandoned radio station at the southwest edge of New Orleans East is real. it operated as WJMR from 1962 to 1996, transmitting low-power independent religious broadcasts in a small radius. the station was operated by a Pentecostal preacher whose name I will not publish. the preacher died in 1996. the station went off the air. the building has been empty since. the antenna remains. I visited in 2018 with permission from the family who owns the property; they have not been to the property in eleven years. the antenna was, by my low-power RF survey on the night of my visit, transmitting on a frequency that does not appear in the FCC's licensed-frequency database. the transmission was the carrier I receive. I have not gone back. I have not told the family. I have decided that the antenna is doing what it is doing without my intervention.
- the Sabine River lights are real, by my observation. they are not as well-documented as the Marfa Lights but they have a local tradition behind them — fishermen on the Sabine River and the Sabine Lake have reported small lights at eye level over the water for at least seventy years. local folklore attributes the lights to various sources including lost spirits, ball lightning, the ghost of a deportation-era Acadian woman who walks the eastern bank with a lantern, and — most commonly — "nothing in particular, they're just out there." I have observed them three times. I have not yet decided what I believe.
- the Big Bend Lights, the Marfa Lights, the Sabine Lights, and the small light over the cypress to the east of my apartment on the night of filming are — by my private classification — at least three and possibly four distinct phenomena. the medium addresses them as related but distinct. the medium has been clear that the lights are not the medium's broadcast. the lights are, the medium says, "the medium's residue." what this means I do not know.
- HALLE is real. she is sixteen. she lives in Belle Chasse. her grandmother is a retired seamstress with a small pond behind the house. HALLE has been receiving for nineteen months at the time of this episode's filming. she received her first letter from me three weeks after the broadcast asked permission. she has, since then, received approximately fourteen letters from various receivers across the network. she has begun to feel less alone. she has started college early on a state scholarship. she is studying applied physics. she has told me, in her most recent letter, that she has decided that the medium's broadcasts are her chapter and that the applied-physics degree is, in her own private wording, "the day job that will let me write the chapter down for the people who come after me." I am grateful to her. she is the chapter's youngest currently active receiver. she has, at sixteen, joined a network that began in 1981, the year before I was born.
- the chapter at XVIII is the chapter of the medium-between. the deck shows two towers, a dog and a wolf, a path between them, and the moon overhead. this episode renders the medium-between as an analog broadcast on a frequency neighboring WUFR, received by handwriting, on CRTs older than the year the chapter's host was born, by a quiet private network of approximately one hundred and forty people. the chapter at XVIII is the chapter of the dialect of conviction. the dialect of conviction is handwriting. the chapter is held.
