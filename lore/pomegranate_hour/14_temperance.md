## ACT — THE TABLE  (00:24 — 20:00)

INT. FRANK'S KITCHEN — TUESDAY — 5:00 PM.

A small kitchen-and-living space — galley kitchen along one wall, a small kitchen table set in the bay-window alcove. The space is the front of FRANK's professional suite, which doubles as his apartment. The back of the suite is his office.

The kitchen table is square, oak, scuffed at the corners. Two chairs.

On the kitchen table:

- one ceramic mug, white, no logo, on the side closest to the camera. Inside the mug: cold black coffee, brewed at 6 AM, sitting since.
- one tall glass mug, clear, with a faint amber tint to the liquid inside. Chamomile and a small spoonful of honey. The honey is from Halsey Apiaries. The chamomile was steeped twenty minutes earlier. The chamomile is warm.
- a small folded paper napkin between the two mugs, perfectly centered.
- a single pencil, lying across the napkin, parallel to the table's edge.
- a small notebook, open, blank, beside the napkin.

On the wall above the table: a round analog clock. 5:00 PM. The clock has a small subdial in its lower face. The subdial reads, in Frank's hand on a small paper insert: **AFTER THE WORST.**

In the bay window, behind the table: the early evening light from the south. The light is filtered through the magnolia in the lot's front yard. The light is honey-amber.

On the counter in the galley kitchen, four feet from the table: a small portable television, set to Channel 14. The TV is on. The TV is muted. The TV shows a man in a black shirt and a white collar at a wooden desk taking calls. The man is FATHER COSGRAVE (or the substitute who played him in S01E05). This is a rerun. Channel 14 closed in 2009. This rerun is playing on a small local cable system that has continued to broadcast Channel 14's archive on a low-power signal that the Harmony Creek Estates' professional district happens to receive.

The portable TV is muted because FRANK has chosen not to hear it. The TV is on because FRANK has chosen, for years, to have it on.

FRANK sits in the chair facing the camera.

FRANK is sixty-three. He is wearing a soft grey shirt, sleeves rolled to mid-forearm. His hair is grey. His glasses are tortoiseshell, settled on the bridge of his nose. His hands are folded on the table in front of him, between the two mugs.

He is not moving.

He has been not moving for some time.

The cold coffee continues to be cold. The chamomile continues to be warm. The pencil continues to lie across the napkin.

The clock advances to 5:01.

FRANK does not move.

The TV silently shows FATHER COSGRAVE listening on the phone, nodding gently, saying something his lips form but the muted TV does not transmit.

The 60 Hz hum continues.

The light in the bay window deepens, very slightly, toward gold.

FRANK does not move.

The clock advances to 5:04.

A small sound: a wind chime, somewhere on the porch, sounds once. The note is a low C. The chime is from a small set FRANK keeps; it sounds only in moderate wind. The wind, outside, has just become moderate.

FRANK looks at the bay window.

He returns his eyes to the table.

He does not drink.

The clock advances to 5:08.

A small movement: FRANK lifts his right hand from where it has been folded. He places his right hand, palm-down, lightly, on the table beside the cold coffee mug. He does not lift the mug. He does not move the mug. He places his hand beside the mug, the way one would place a hand on the shoulder of a person sitting down next to them.

His hand stays.

He does the same with his left hand beside the warm chamomile.

His hands are now on either side of the two mugs.

He is, the camera registers, sitting between the two mugs with his hands beside them, the way one would sit with two friends.

The clock advances to 5:09.

The 9:09 cable interference does not occur at 5:09 — it occurs, in this episode, with the wind chime. The chime sounds again. The note is the same low C. The interference rides the chime for nine seconds. The chime continues. The interference subsides. The chime stops.

FRANK has not moved his hands.

The clock advances to 5:14.

A small movement: FRANK's left hand lifts. He picks up the chamomile glass. He drinks. He sets the glass back down. He does not pick up the coffee.

He returns his left hand to the table beside the chamomile.

He breathes.

The clock advances to 5:21.

FRANK speaks. Once. To the table. Or to the mugs. Or to no one. His voice is quiet and warm.

FRANK
*(quiet)*
Right.

He does not elaborate. He does not move. He has said the word and the word is the chapter's word for him.

The clock advances to 5:28.

A car passes on the street outside. The car has a country radio station on, very quiet, audible only as a small Cajun fiddle's lift before it fades west. FRANK does not turn his head.

The clock advances to 5:36.

A small movement: FRANK lifts his right hand. He picks up the cold coffee mug. He holds the mug at the level of his sternum for nine seconds. He does not drink. He sets the mug back down.

He returns his right hand to the table beside the cold coffee.

He breathes.

The chamomile is now slightly cooler. The cold coffee is still cold. The clock advances to 5:44.

FRANK lifts both hands. He folds them, again, on the table between the two mugs. He returns to his original position.

He sits.

The bay-window light continues to deepen toward rose.

The portable TV on the counter continues to play silent Channel 14. FATHER COSGRAVE is now setting the receiver down. He is hanging up. He hangs up once. He picks the receiver up again. He hangs it up. He picks it up again. He hangs it up. Three times. The chapter's signature.

FRANK does not look at the TV.

The clock advances to 5:58.

The light in the bay window is now rose. The magnolia's silhouette outside has begun to register against the lit window across the side yard — the neighbor's kitchen, also lit, also at the end of the day.

The wind chime sounds twice in a row. The chapter is, FRANK reads with the small subdial-glance he is now making to the wall clock above the table, transitioning from afternoon to evening.

The clock reads 6:00.

FRANK rises.

The chair scrapes, slightly.

He picks up the chamomile glass. He drinks the rest. He sets the empty glass in the sink behind him.

He picks up the cold coffee mug. He carries it to the sink. He pours the cold coffee down the drain, slowly, without dramatic gesture. He rinses the mug. He sets it upside-down on the drying rack.

He returns to the table.

He picks up the pencil. He opens the notebook to a fresh page. He writes a single line.

We see the line over his shoulder. The line, in his hand, reads:

> *Tuesday. After the worst. Moderate. — F.*

He caps the pencil. He closes the notebook. He places the pencil across the napkin, parallel to the table's edge, in its original position.

He looks at the camera for the first and only time in the episode. He looks at it the way one looks at a guest one has decided is welcome. He nods, once.

He crosses out of the kitchen, toward the back of the suite. The TV, on the counter, continues to play silent Channel 14.

The camera holds on the empty kitchen.

The empty mug, washed, drying.

The empty chamomile glass, in the sink.

The notebook, closed, on the table.

The pencil, across the napkin.

The light in the bay window, fading to dusk.

The wind chime, sounding once more, two beats, low C and a fifth above.

The clock advances to 6:09.

The 9:09 cable interference rises one more time — for nine seconds — riding the chime. The chime continues. The interference subsides.

The clock advances.

The room sits.

## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- FRANK is FRANK. he is sixty-three at the time of filming. he is a private consultant whose practice is small and whose clients are quiet and whose hours are his own. he is the painted POV of vol5's Temperance chapter and of vol5's Sun chapter. he is the chapter's keystone for the moderate-temperature register.
- the kitchen is FRANK's actual kitchen. the table is his. the chairs are his. the wall clock with the AFTER THE WORST subdial is one of his three modified clocks — he keeps a different subdial-state for each of the three rooms of his suite. the kitchen's clock reads AFTER THE WORST on Tuesdays only. on other days it reads other things. on Sundays it is unmarked. I have not asked him about all of them. I have asked him only about Tuesdays.
- the camera was on a tripod at a fixed position approximately seven feet from the table. there was no operator in the room. FRANK was alone. he was, by his own report afterward, slightly aware of the camera for the first three or four minutes and then forgot it entirely. the chapter's stillness is his Tuesday stillness. the chapter is what his Tuesdays are.
- the two cups. FRANK has explained the two cups to me, in private, only once. he keeps the cold black coffee from the morning because the morning coffee is, in his accounting, "the coffee that was hot when I had to start." he keeps the warm chamomile because the afternoon chamomile is, in his accounting, "the chamomile that is warm because I have decided to be warm." the two cups together are, in his accounting, "what I started with and what I have decided." he does not always drink either. he often drinks the chamomile and not the coffee. he is honoring the morning by keeping the morning's cup at the table without consuming it. the morning was the worst. the chamomile is the moderate.
- the portable TV on the counter playing silent Channel 14 reruns is one of the chapter's surface acknowledgments. the rerun is being broadcast on a small low-power signal that, by my private inquiry, originates from a private local-broadcast operator in Westport, Louisiana — a former engineer at Channel 14 who has, since the station's closure in 2009, continued to broadcast the station's archive on a low-power signal he operates from his garage. the engineer is not licensed. the FCC has, by my inquiry, twice been told and twice declined to act. the engineer is, by my reading, one of the medium's receivers (S01E18) and has been receiving for at least eleven years. he is currently re-running the 1996 season of FATHER COSGRAVE's archive. FRANK has been watching Channel 14 reruns since FRANK was thirteen years old.
- FRANK's single word at 5:21 — *right* — is his characteristic word. it is also the same word the woman in the vigil (S01E12 THE HANGED MAN) speaks once during her two-hour shuffle. the chapter does not require the words to mean the same thing. they may. they may not. the word is the chapter's word.
- the writing in his notebook at the close — *Tuesday. After the worst. Moderate. — F.* — is the chapter's only document of itself. FRANK keeps notebooks. he keeps approximately one notebook per quarter. he records, at the end of each Tuesday and each Saturday (the latter is the Sun chapter), one line about the day. he has been doing this for forty years. he has, at present, approximately one hundred and sixty notebooks. they are stored in a closet in the back of his suite. I have not asked to read them. he has not offered. the chapter respects this.
- FRANK's gesture at 5:08 — placing his hands beside the two mugs the way one would place hands on the shoulders of two people sitting down — is the chapter's quietest acknowledgment that the two cups, on his table, are company. FRANK does not anthropomorphize the cups in any other register. he does not name them. he does not address them. he simply sits beside them and, occasionally, places his hands beside theirs. the chapter is the chapter of moderate company. the company does not require speech.
- I have, since this episode aired, received from FRANK a single small package in the mail. the package contained a small mason jar of cold black coffee, sealed, and a small ceramic carafe of warm chamomile, in a thermal sleeve. the package included a small folded note in his hand. the note read: *"in case you'd like to try. — F."* I tried. I sat at my kitchen table in Mauriceville with the cold coffee and the chamomile. I sat for forty minutes. I did not drink the coffee. I drank the chamomile. I wrote one line in my notebook. the line read: *Tuesday. After the worst. Moderate. — E.* I am, by this small letter exchange, one of FRANK's correspondents. there are, by his own count, approximately twenty-six of us. we do not, ordinarily, meet. we send each other small packages on Tuesdays. the chapter is the chapter.
- the chapter at XIV in the deck is the chapter of moderation. tradition shows an alchemist pouring water between two vessels. this episode renders the chapter as a man sitting between two mugs on a Tuesday afternoon, not drinking either, breathing, writing one line, going on. moderation is, in FRANK's reading and in the chapter's, the discipline of being a person who can sit between two states without collapsing toward either. FRANK has been performing this discipline for forty years. the chapter is held.
