EXT. NATCHITOCHES PARISH — AUXILIARY WATER TOWER NO. 4 — THURSDAY MORNING — 09:08 AM.

A wide establishing composite — assembled from the seven cameras — places the water tower. The tower is a standard rural Louisiana auxiliary: a tall narrow steel cylinder on four splayed legs, painted municipal silver, with the parish name painted on its tank in faded letters. The tower sits in an open field at the edge of the campus of THE LOUISIANA SCHOOL FOR MATH, SCIENCE, AND THE ARTS — a residential magnet high school in Natchitoches.

The morning is clear. The field around the tower is mowed. The campus is a quarter mile to the east.

The seven cameras roll. Each is identified, in subtle lower-third type, by source.

### CAMERA 1 — DASHCAM, LOUISIANA STATE TROOPER VEHICLE 47

The trooper has parked on the shoulder of Highway 1 for a coffee. The dashcam is angled north-northwest, over the cruiser's hood, with the water tower in the upper third of the frame.

09:08:34. The tower stands. The morning is clear.

09:09:00. A brief, faint visual artifact appears around the tank — a slight optical shimmer, the way heat shimmers off pavement in summer but more concentrated, more contained.

The shimmer holds for approximately seven seconds.

09:09:07. The tower's tank fractures along an upper seam. The fracture propagates downward. The tower's four legs splay outward. The tank settles, then collapses, on the field below. The collapse takes 2.4 seconds.

09:09:11. Cloud of dust and steel. The trooper, off-camera, says clearly: *"the hell."* He exits the cruiser. Dashcam continues to roll.
