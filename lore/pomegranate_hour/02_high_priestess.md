## ACT I — THE TWO ROOMS  (00:24 — 04:30)

INT. AN ARCHIVE ROOM — DAY — WIDE.

The same halftone-monochrome treatment as the host's room, but darker. The walls are lined floor to ceiling with VHS tapes on plain wooden shelves. A small CRT television and a VCR sit on a wooden desk. A yellow legal pad lies on the desk beside the VCR, blank, perfectly aligned with the desk's edge. A single sharpened number-2 pencil rests across the pad's top, parallel to the binding.

In a wooden chair in front of the desk: ELICIA. She is dressed identically to the cold-open. Her hands are folded in her lap. She is not looking at the camera now.

She is looking at the CRT.

The CRT shows static for three seconds, then resolves into a recording. The recording is in the same halftone-monochrome aesthetic.

ON THE CRT:

Another archive room. The shelves are the same. The desk is the same. The CRT on that desk is showing static. A woman sits in a wooden chair facing that desk. The woman is ELICIA. She is two years younger; her hair is shorter; her hands are folded the same way.

The earlier ELICIA reaches forward and presses a button on the VCR. The static on the CRT-within-the-CRT resolves into something we cannot see (the angle does not show it). She watches it for nineteen seconds. She reaches for the yellow legal pad. She picks up the pencil. She writes one line at the top of the pad.

The earlier ELICIA puts the pencil down. The pencil rests across the pad, parallel to the binding.

She turns her head — slightly — toward the camera. Not toward it. Past it. She holds the look for two seconds.

Then she presses STOP on the VCR.

Cut back to: present-day archive room.

The present ELICIA looks at the desk beside her.

The yellow legal pad on the desk is no longer blank.

There is one line written at the top, in her handwriting.

She does not pick the pad up. She reads it without moving. We are not shown what it says. The camera does not move in.

She places her hand flat on the surface of the desk, beside the pad. She holds it there. She does not write.

The CRT continues to play. The static returns. She does not look at the CRT.

We hold on her face. Four seconds.

The camera holds on this tape for nine seconds.

Cut back to the desk.

The present ELICIA is now holding the yellow legal pad in her lap. She is reading it. The pad's writing-side is angled away from the camera; we see her reading.

She lowers the pad. She places it back on the desk.

She reaches across to a drawer beneath the desk. She opens the drawer. The drawer contains a single VHS tape. She removes it. The label, visible briefly to the camera: **TAPE 11.**

She closes the drawer.

She slides TAPE 11 into the VCR. She presses PLAY.

## ACT III — TAPE 11  (09:00 — 13:00)

ON THE CRT:

Static for three seconds.

Then: a room that is not the archive room. A small apartment kitchen — analog. A round wooden table. A single overhead pendant light. A landline phone on the wall. A woman is sitting at the table with her back to us. The woman is not ELICIA. The hair color is wrong. The shoulders are narrower.

The woman is shuffling a deck of tarot cards.

She cuts the deck once. She cuts it twice. She cuts it three times.

She lays three cards face up on the table.

The cards are: THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

The woman gathers the cards. She shuffles. She cuts three times. She lays three cards.

THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

She gathers. She shuffles. She cuts. She lays.

THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

She does this for two minutes of screen time. The shot does not cut. The angle does not change. The cards do not change.

On the fifth iteration, the camera microphone picks up a sound that is not in the room. It is the 9:09 cable interference. It rises, briefly, behind the woman's hands. The hands do not pause. The hum subsides.

On the seventh iteration, the woman speaks. Once. We hear it through the tape's audio, slightly degraded:

WOMAN
*(quiet, to herself)*
Of course.

She gathers the cards. She does not lay them again.

Cut: static.

Cut: the woman's hand reaches forward and presses STOP on the VCR she has been recording herself on. The hand has a small scar across the back of the knuckle of the index finger.

Cut: static.

Cut: black.

The recording ends.

ON THE CRT:
Static. Three seconds. Then a blue VCR-display message — **STOP** — fades up.

## ACT IV — THE NOTE  (13:00 — 14:00)

Back in the present archive room.

The present ELICIA — same age, present-day — sits in her chair. She has been watching the CRT.

She lowers her eyes to the yellow legal pad on the desk.

She picks it up.

She reads the line she wrote two years earlier.

She rotates the pad and shows it to the camera.

In her handwriting, at the top of the pad, the line reads:

> ***the cards don't change. you have already been her. you will be her again. archive this.***

She lowers the pad.

She places it back on the desk. The pencil resting on top.

She turns her head — slightly — toward the camera. Not toward it. Past it. She holds the look for two seconds.

She does not nod.

She reaches forward and presses STOP on the VCR.

## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- the woman on TAPE 11 is not me. her hand bears a small scar I do not have. she shuffled tarot in her own apartment with her own deck on a night I was not there. the tape arrived in my archive in a brown envelope in late winter. there was no return address. the postage was correct.
- the cousin at the wedding (VI) handed me a different tape. ANYA_03. the deck has been correctly told that ANYA_03 came to me by hand. TAPE 11 came to me by mail.
- the line on the legal pad was written two years before I made this episode. I do not remember writing it. I have decided not to claim I did or didn't. the legal pad has been on this desk in this position for two years.
- the future-self / past-self conceit is the only honest way I know to film this card. the High Priestess card is read forward and backward simultaneously; the episode renders that as a closed loop in which the writer of the note is the reader of the note is the woman on the tape is the next woman who shuffles. Natalie, of course. Natalie does not know this yet.
- the dressing change: I deliberately wear the same clothes in the cold open, the body of the episode, and the closing chair. the show's host does not speak; the show's host is therefore also the show's only consistent recurring character. she is the chapter's syntax.
