# EARTHMAN CHRONICLES — story treatment

Two versions in parallel: **THE MANUSCRIPT** (Hubbard's 1948
unpublished novel · earnest pulp · self-aggrandizing) and
**THE GAME** (Astro-Cortex 1985 adaptation · arch, humorous,
morally braver · Rocha's QA team's revisions all over it).

The manuscript exists in-fiction as a physical document in
Rocha's Trailer bookcase.  Andy Link can find it there · read
it as a scrapbook item · compare it to what he's just played.
The game and the manuscript BOTH ship as playable text; the
game is the interactive form, the manuscript is a readable
document with hand-written marginalia by Rocha and colleagues.

Companion docs:
- `_EARTHMAN_CHRONICLES_DESIGN.md` · master design
- `_EARTHMAN_MANUSCRIPT.md` · the actual scrapbook manuscript
  document, formatted as a photocopied typescript with
  handwritten annotations

## The manuscript · L. Rafayette Hubbard, 1948

**Title**: *THE PROPHET OF THE RED WORLD*
**Length**: 78,000 words, twelve chapters, single manuscript box
**Status**: never published · Hubbard shelved it in 1949 to
develop what became CORTEXOLOGY · found in a 1985 estate sale
by an Astro-Cortex acquisitions editor who was drunk

**Tone**: pulp planetary romance in the John Carter tradition.
Purple prose.  Self-aggrandizing first-person narration.  Every
mystical claim rendered as literal fact.  Women are prizes.
Villains are ugly.  The narrator is always right and takes
his time telling you.

**Perspective**: Jack Whiteside speaks in first-person past-
tense · addressed to a "you" who is Rafaton (Hubbard's self-
insert · scribe · mentor · confidant).

**Central conceit**: Jack Whiteside is a Caltech chemist who
in January 1946 performed a specific propellant experiment
combined with what he calls "an old text I had been studying".
The experiment opened a gate to a red desert world called PARSA.
Jack finds himself in a lost civilization, becomes its Prophet
via his innate wisdom and specific ritual skill, wins the
princess, and ascends.

**The Hubbard-analog character** in-manuscript is RAFATON, a
scholar-monk of Parsa's Kelait people who serves as Jack's
adviser · a wise elder who ALWAYS gives Jack the correct
counsel.  Rafaton is the manuscript's second lead.  He teaches
Jack how to perform each Working.  He translates the Parsan
tongue.  He is beloved by all Parsan peoples.  Manuscript
Rafaton is Hubbard writing himself as HE would have been
remembered.

## The game · Astro-Cortex, March 1985

**Title**: THE EARTHMAN CHRONICLES (marketing changed it ·
Hubbard's estate wanted the modifier "EARTHMAN" for series-
branding reasons; the estate was apparently unaware that
this manuscript had been unpublished, and Astro-Cortex did
not clarify)

**Length**: six chapters, 30-40 hours to complete on a first
playthrough, all six endings across ~3 playthroughs

**Tone**: earnest at surface, arch beneath.  Every
Hubbardish self-aggrandizement in the manuscript is preserved
in the DIALOGUE Rafaton speaks, but the party members' reactions
and the specific animations undermine it.  The prose that
survives from the manuscript is presented as text-box narration
that GRADUALLY BREAKS DOWN over the six chapters until, by
Chapter 5, it stops making sense and Rocha's Corrections
overwrite it in specific scenes.

**Perspective**: third-person controllable · the player IS Jack
but sees him from behind as a sprite · dialogue in text boxes ·
narration in specific italic text boxes that are visibly
retyped in a different font from Chapter 3 onward.

## STORY BEATS · manuscript chapters vs. game chapters

The manuscript has TWELVE chapters.  The game has SIX.
Astro-Cortex compressed 2:1.  What was cut and what was added
matters as much as what stayed.

### Manuscript Chapter I · "The Laboratory"
Jack Whiteside prepares a specific ritual in his Pasadena lab
on the seventeenth of January nineteen-forty-six.  Rafaton is
present as scribe.  Jack lights three candles.  Jack reads
aloud from a specific "old text."  Something moves in the
corner.  Rafaton says "Do you feel it, Jack?" and Jack answers
"I feel the very fabric parting."  The ritual completes.  The
lab is empty.  A yellow butterfly, out of place in January,
lands on Jack's notebook.  Chapter ends.

**GAME CHAPTER 1 · "Pasadena · 1946"** (compresses manuscript
Ch. I but adds significantly)
Fully playable · Jack navigates his laboratory as a first-person
grid dungeon (~8 cells).  Boot questionnaire established Jack's
class-focus (chemistry vs. mysticism).  Player must:
- Gather ritual components from the lab (a specific propellant
  formulation · a specific old book · three candles · a bowl)
- Chalk the correct sigil on the floor (a small ARC-BASED
  minigame · fail and re-chalk)
- Choose which version of the working to attempt · three options,
  each biasing which Parsan tribe Jack first meets in Ch. 2
- Handle Rafaton's advice, which is aggressive · Rafaton wants
  Jack to omit specific components · a player can refuse
- Perform the ritual (a small rhythm minigame · Rafaton keeps
  time)
- Choose whether to invite Rafaton across the threshold or
  send him home first · **this is the game's first fork** ·
  sending Rafaton home is the pacifist route's beginning; taking
  Rafaton with you means he becomes an antagonist party-adjacent
  NPC in Ch. 3+

Rocha's addition (visible in ROM at 220 Hz once found): a
specific document on the lab bench titled MEMORANDUM · RE: OTO
FEES · reveals Jack's specific real financial situation in
1946 as a specific footnote-in-text.  Not in the manuscript.

### Manuscript Chapter II · "The Passage"
A one-page chapter.  "I fell.  I fell farther than any man has
fallen.  I fell through what Rafaton later called the
Corridors of the Between.  I have not written of what I saw
there because it is not for the printed page.  I emerged."

**GAME CHAPTER 2 START · "The Approach"** (manuscript Ch. II is
handled in 15 seconds of screen time · black screen, sound
design)
Fine as-is.  Rocha and team decided the ellipsis was in
character for Hubbard and let it stand.

### Manuscript Chapter III · "The Sand"
Jack wakes on Parsa.  Rust-red sand.  Two moons overhead, one
visibly dying.  He walks.  He meets a Delvanni scout · a
four-armed warrior named HEL VELLI.  Jack demonstrates his
"native wisdom" and Hel Velli is impressed.  Hel Velli takes
Jack to his tribe.  Chapter ends with Jack about to enter the
tribal circle.

**GAME CHAPTER 2 · "The Approach"** (manuscript Ch. II-III
compressed)
Playable overworld.  Jack traverses Parsan wastelands.  Meets
Hel Velli, who does NOT read Jack as instantly-wise (in the
game, Hel Velli reads Jack as "small · four-limbed · not from
here").  Hel Velli's respect must be EARNED via specific
choices.  Combat introduced.  The tribal circle Jack enters
is at the end of the chapter.

Rocha's changes: Hel Velli speaks Delvanni ceremonial phrases
that Jack does NOT understand.  Subtitles show what Jack thinks
Hel Velli said (which is often wrong).  Comic underread.

### Manuscript Chapter IV · "The Circle"
Jack fights a Delvanni champion named MURG in a tribal duel.
Jack wins because Rafaton has told him (in a specific dream
sequence) how to defeat a four-armed opponent.  Manuscript Ch.
IV is 12,000 words of Hubbard's actual combat prose.  Awful.

**GAME CHAPTER 2 · duel scene** (embedded)
Playable combat.  Jack vs. MURG.  Player picks weapons and
strategy.  Rafaton's "dream advice" IS available (via a small
dialogue option), but a player who WINS without it earns Hel
Velli's true respect.  A player who USES Rafaton's advice wins
easily but Hel Velli's disposition drops.

Rocha's addition: a fourth option next to the three weapon
choices, labeled "BRIEF CASE".  Selecting it opens a specific
comedy scene · Jack pulls out his Caltech lab briefcase · Murg
laughs · they have a specific brief conversation about their
respective jobs · they duel afterward but with less hostility.
This is one of many small Rocha-team humor beats.

### Manuscript Chapter V · "The Court of the Kyrindi"
Jack meets the tall-blue Kyrindi at their capital, TALIKAN.  He
is presented to KING VESSEL of the Kyrindi.  Vessel's daughter
SARA NAI meets Jack and instantly falls in love with him.
Vessel offers Jack a marriage-alliance.  Sara is a prize.
Hubbard writes Sara as "a woman such as any man dreams of."
Which is a lot to say about someone in 1948.

**GAME CHAPTER 3 · "Talikan"**
Playable Kyrindi capital.  Sara Nai has her OWN arc: she is
a princess but she is ALSO a scholar of ancient Kelait texts,
and she has been having recurring dreams about a human man for
years, and the game frames this NOT as fate but as SARA
NAI DELIBERATELY LEARNING TO INTERPRET her dreams.  Jack's
arrival is her PROJECT.  She's been waiting for him academically.

Sara Nai is playable as a party member from Chapter 3.  She has
opinions.  She DISAGREES with Jack about specific Kyrindi
customs.  She has a preferred version of the ancient rituals.
She is a person, not a prize.

Rocha's additions:
- Sara Nai and Jack's first meeting is a CORRECTION scene ·
  the manuscript's version is displayed on-screen, and then
  a specific line of Sara's dialogue OVERRIDES it: "You did
  not fall in love with me on first sight.  You looked at me
  like I was a chart."
- King Vessel does not offer Sara as prize.  Sara chooses.
- The manuscript's line "such as any man dreams of" is
  displayed briefly, then struck-through in red pixel-marker,
  and replaced with "a scholar of ancient rituals who has been
  looking for a specific technical collaborator."  This is the
  first explicit Correction the player sees.

### Manuscript Chapter VI · "The First Working"
Jack performs the Star Ruby ritual (Working I) with Rafaton's
guidance.  Manuscript describes the ritual in loving detail.
Jack gains the ability to "banish evil influences."

**GAME CHAPTER 3 · Working I scene**
Playable ritual.  The nine Workings are the game's Progression
Ladder.  Working I costs specific components + a fasting cost
(Jack must skip one meal, which is a text-box moment).  Sara
Nai has an opinion on how it should be done ("The Kelait
version of this rite is more accurate.")  Jack can accept
her input or Rafaton's.  Different outcomes.

Rocha's changes: Rafaton's specific instructions include an
odd platitude · "A prophet is he who knows his own worth."
Sara Nai in the same scene mutters "Or he who charges by the
hour."  Party disposition shifts.

### Manuscript Chapter VII · "The Hunt"
Jack rides with Hel Velli's tribe against another Delvanni
tribe.  Hubbard's Delvanni are savages who need civilizing.
Jack teaches them specific techniques (all of which are
plausibly 1948 U.S. Army small-unit tactics).  Wins.  Trophies.

**GAME CHAPTER 3 · Delvanni conflict**
Playable tactical combat.  BUT: the tactical goal is not
victory over another tribe · it's DEFENSE OF A KELAIT
SETTLEMENT (which is now the actual reason for the raid).
The manuscript's "tribes at war" is revealed to be a
DELIBERATE FEUD staged by a specific Rafaton-analog Parsan
who benefits from Delvanni conflict.  Jack can INTERRUPT the
war rather than win it.

Rocha's changes: Hubbard's "small-unit tactics" descriptions
still exist in the manuscript-quote text boxes, but a party
member's aside overlays them: "Jack, that's the manual you
were issued at Caltech ROTC."  Small giggle from Sara.

### Manuscript Chapter VIII · "The Mountain"
Jack ascends a specific mountain to consult the KELAIT SAGES.
The Kelait are the small quiet children-shaped ancient people
of Parsa.  They give Jack cryptic wisdom.  Manuscript Kelait
speak in aphorisms that sound profound.

**GAME CHAPTER 4 · "The Mines of Delvan"**
Playable dungeon crawl in the Delvanni mining operation.  This
is where the Kelait secret is revealed: they are NOT sages,
they are ENSLAVED, and their "wisdom" is a specific set of
riddles they use to communicate escape plans in front of
their Delvanni overseers.  Jack learns to help them.

Rocha's changes: Kelait aphorisms are decoded in-game (via a
specific optional puzzle) and revealed to be RECIPES.  "The
answer is in the salt" turns out to mean · in the specific
Kelait chef's kitchen · "add salt to that pot."  Deflates
Hubbard's mysticism to comedic effect.  BUT: the ACTUAL Kelait
wisdom, once trust is earned, is genuine and moving.

### Manuscript Chapter IX · "The Oracle"
The SCARLET WOMAN appears to Jack.  In manuscript form she is
"clothed in a light not of Parsa."  She gives Jack a specific
prophecy about his role.  She is Sara Nai's OPPOSITE and Jack
must choose between them (Hubbard was working out something
personal).  Manuscript Ch. IX is the shortest chapter · one
scene · the choice is forced.

**GAME CHAPTER 5 START · The Scarlet Woman**
Playable meeting.  The Scarlet Woman is available for
recruitment only if the player has completed specific
prerequisites (see design doc).  She is DELIBERATELY
AMBIGUOUS about whether she is Babalon or a Delvanni
priestess named GHYRA playing at the role.  The player is
never told which.

Rocha's changes: the "clothed in a light not of Parsa" text
is preserved verbatim as a manuscript-quote text box, but a
Sara Nai dialogue option immediately after reads: "It's called
photoluminescent moss, Jack.  She's been prepping this room."

### Manuscript Chapter X · "The Betrayal"
Jack is betrayed by a specific figure in Parsa who was
serving evil forces.  Manuscript names him NALAT.  Nalat has
been undermining Jack throughout.  Jack duels Nalat and wins.

**GAME CHAPTER 5 · The Academy**
Playable.  The BETRAYER is now revealed to be a PARSA-COPY of
Rafaton (a Kelait scholar named NALAT · same character in the
manuscript · but here his role is expanded).  Nalat is
serving the manuscript's REAL antagonist: the ORDER OF THE
PROPHET, a Parsan cult that has been secretly guiding Jack's
Workings for a specific goal.

Rocha's team's biggest Correction: the ORDER OF THE PROPHET's
symbol · a specific eight-pointed star · is IDENTICAL to a
symbol from a specific real-world organization that Hubbard's
own life brushed against.  This is not stated on-screen; it's
in the compendium.

Nalat's specific dialogue in the game version includes: "You
have completed six Workings on my instruction, Jack.  Do you
not wonder why?  Well.  I get a specific bonus for each."
That last line is not in the manuscript.  It's Rocha's.

### Manuscript Chapter XI · "The Great Work"
Jack performs Working VII, THE GREAT WORK.  Sara Nai is
briefly transformed into her "true form."  She IS Babalon in
the manuscript.  Hubbard makes this explicit.  Working VII is
climactic.  Sara Nai returns to Kyrindi form.  They marry.

**GAME CHAPTER 5 · Working VII scene**
Playable.  Sara Nai DOES briefly transform · but the game
frames this AMBIGUOUSLY.  Working VII is presented as either:
- Sara Nai HAS BEEN Babalon this whole time (the manuscript's
  reading), OR
- Sara Nai has ALLOWED herself to be seen as Babalon for
  Jack's benefit (the game's alternative reading), OR
- Jack is projecting his desire onto Sara Nai and the vision
  is his own (the Corrections' reading)

The player picks their interpretation via a specific dialogue
tree.  All three are canonical.  A player who chose the third
reading unlocks a specific line from Sara later that recontextu-
alizes the entire relationship.

### Manuscript Chapter XII · "The Babalon Working"
Jack performs the terminal working.  Babalon manifests.  Jack
is elevated to Prophet of the Red World.  He rules Parsa
alongside Sara.  Rafaton (in this final chapter) appears one
last time to congratulate Jack.  Rafaton's exact final line:
"You have done what I could not, Jack.  You are my SUCCESSOR."

**GAME CHAPTER 6 · The Finale**
Playable · one of six endings triggers depending on the
player's cumulative choices.  See design doc.

Rocha's addition to the endings:
- Any ending that includes the manuscript-canonical "Rafaton
  congratulates Jack" moment is REVERSED at 220 Hz · Rafaton's
  actual dialogue plays inaudibly underneath the on-screen
  text: "You have done what I could not, Jack.  Now let's talk
  about my percentage."  Rocha's team quietly authored this
  audio layer.  A player who noticed the 220-Hz signal can
  extract the actual Rafaton audio via the compendium.

## Character bibles

### JACK WHITESIDE
- Age 32 in Chapter 1 (matching the real Parsons in 1946)
- Height 5'10", brown hair, blue eyes, always slightly tired
- Caltech Ph.D., propellant chemistry
- Amateur Thelemite, has been for eight years
- His wife Helen died in 1943 (this is in the MANUSCRIPT · a
  specific line that Astro-Cortex left in as-is, though Rocha's
  marginalia questions whether Hubbard invented this to
  give Jack a "reason" to be free-agent for Sara)
- Speaks with a specific slight stammer when talking about
  his emotions (Astro-Cortex added this · it's a lovely detail)
- His father was a preacher.  His mother was a suffragette.
  He does not talk about either.

### RAFATON
- MANUSCRIPT · a wise Kelait scholar-monk, Jack's mentor and
  scribe, ageless, always right
- GAME · a specific Kelait scholar named NALAT who WAS
  Jack's mentor and is revealed in Chapter 5 to be serving
  the Order of the Prophet.  Astro-Cortex renamed him Rafaton
  in some scenes and Nalat in others · a deliberate confusion
  that mirrors Hubbard's own duplication in life
- Voice actor direction: "avuncular, always slightly too
  pleased with his own aphorisms"
- His specific character animation includes a tell: he
  always adjusts his cuff before delivering a big line.
  Rocha's team added this.  Nobody has told anyone what it means.

### HEL VELLI
- Delvanni warrior, four arms, seven feet tall, calm
- Speaks Delvanni ceremonial phrases (see language notes below)
- Has been a warrior for 22 years.  Has killed 34 enemies in
  combat.  Remembers each name.
- His mother is alive and lives in a specific coastal
  Delvanni settlement.  Hel Velli writes to her weekly.
  Jack learns this in Chapter 4 and the moment lands.
- Preferred weapons in combat: greatsword (top hands),
  spear (bottom hands).  Can dual-wield all four.
- Loyal to Jack after specific proof of respect (not
  manuscript-instant, must be earned)

### SARA NAI
- Kyrindi princess, 24 years old, 6'4" (Kyrindi are tall)
- Silver-blue skin, silver hair, eyes that reflect green in
  torchlight
- Scholar of Kelait ancient rituals · has read every extant
  Kelait manuscript in Kyrindi archives · fluent in three
  Parsan languages plus formal court speech
- Been having dreams about Jack for four years before he
  arrives · thinks she summoned him deliberately · KNOWS she
  summoned him deliberately by end of Chapter 3
- Loves Jack but is under no delusion about him · loves him
  for his SPECIFIC qualities, not his manuscript-mythos
- Refuses Working IX because she has read the Kelait
  interpretation of it and she knows what it does
- Preferred weapon: her voice.  Kyrindi vocal magic is her
  specialty.  Can also fight with a specific ceremonial dagger.
- Sara's arc across the game: she stops being anyone's
  princess and becomes a fellow scholar / partner.

### THE SCARLET WOMAN / GHYRA
- Ambiguous identity · either Babalon-in-truth or a specific
  Delvanni priestess named Ghyra playing the role
- Manuscript treats her as literally Babalon
- Game treats her as ambiguous, with three canonical
  interpretations available (see Ch. 5 above)
- Manifests specifically in scarlet fabric · flowing garments,
  a specific pattern on the hem that a specific keepsake
  recognizes
- Never gives a straight answer.  Not once.  Even when it
  would help.  This is a character trait, not a mechanic.
- If recruited, her party disposition never rises above
  NEUTRAL.  She isn't playing at friendship.

### ROCHA (as NPC)
- Claims to be a CARTOGRAPHER'S ASSISTANT working for a
  specific Kyrindi mapmaker
- Actually a 1985 QA tester at Astro-Cortex Software (age
  22 in the fiction)
- Recruitable in Chapter 4 by asking her a specific
  question: "Are you SURE you're just a cartographer's
  assistant?"  She smiles.
- Her party dialogue includes: "Have you tried talking to it?"
  and "Have you saved recently?" and "This is an obvious
  puzzle, Jack" and other fourth-wall breaks
- In combat she uses DEBUG TOOLKIT skills: reveal enemy
  stats, skip enemy turn, save the fight and reload
- Her specific final scene, if recruited and if the player
  finds all Corrections: she takes off her cartographer's
  hat, looks at Jack, and says "Yeah.  I lied about the
  cartographer thing."  Then a specific handwritten note
  scrolls across the bottom of the screen: "for jack · a.r. ·
  march 1985"

### RAFATON'S BOSS (only appears in the manuscript's Chapter X,
### expanded in the game's Chapter 5 as an actual encounter)
- Manuscript calls him THE PROPHET.  Just that.
- Game calls him RONSON.  He is a Hubbard-analog specifically ·
  a 6'0" Parsa human-adjacent man in a specific outfit that
  no other Parsan wears.  He has been on Parsa for 14 years
  before Jack arrived.  He got there via a specific earlier
  ritual.
- His character animation includes: he stands slightly turned
  from camera, always.  This is deliberate · he is always
  presenting to an audience that isn't there.
- Delivers a specific climactic soliloquy in Chapter 5 · fully
  playable, the player MUST sit through it · which one
  specific party dialogue option reduces to nine words: "you
  are describing a franchise operation, Ronson."

## The Kelait wisdom · Rocha's biggest single addition

The Kelait people appear in both manuscript and game.
Manuscript renders them as mystical children-shaped sages
speaking in profound aphorisms.  Game renders them as an
ENSLAVED people whose aphorisms are secretly RECIPES and
ESCAPE PLANS.

But EARNED trust with the Kelait unlocks their actual wisdom ·
and this Rocha team's version of Kelait wisdom is one of the
most beautifully written elements in the whole game.  Sample
Kelait teachings (game version):

- "A person who arrives with a chair is a person who plans to
  stay a while."
- "The moon that is dying still has weight."
- "If you don't like the tribe, you can be the tribe."
- "Rocks remember, but not what you would think.  Rocks
  remember weather.  Not people."
- "The salt in the pot is not the same salt as in the sea.
  The sea does not know about your pot."
- "There is a room in every house that is for grief.  There
  is a room in every house that is for laughter.  Sometimes
  they are the same room."
- "You will die.  I will die.  Sara Nai will die.  Rafaton
  will die.  Hel Velli will die.  Rocha will die.  The two
  moons will die.  The last light will be a specific color
  none of us have ever seen.  Live now."

The last one is delivered to Jack by a specific Kelait elder
named MOTHER KANEL in Chapter 4.  It's the game's single most
quoted line among players.  Rocha's team wrote it and Astro-
Cortex tried to cut it as "too depressing."  They lost.

## Endings · full script fragments

### Ending 1 · THE WARLORD OF PARSA
Subtitle: "SOMEONE HAS TO SIT ON THAT THRONE"

Final cutscene · Jack seated on the Kyrindi throne, Sara Nai
at his right, Hel Velli standing behind, the Delvanni tribes
gathered in the great hall.  A specific narration plays:
"And thus did Jack Whiteside rule Parsa with wisdom and
mercy, for such a time as any Prophet may."  Then a specific
final image: Sara Nai's crown is slightly too small · she
adjusts it while nobody is looking.  Fade.

### Ending 2 · RETURN TO EARTH
Subtitle: "PARSONS DIES ANYWAY"

Jack wakes in his lab, 1946, holding a book.  Rafaton is
absent.  Sara Northrup is on the porch.  A specific montage
plays: 1946 · 1947 · 1948 · 1949 · 1950 · 1951 · 1952.  In
the 1952 frame, a specific coroner's report is briefly visible.
Then a specific image · the Parsonage in Pasadena, June 18,
1952.  Then black.

### Ending 3 · BABALON COMES
Subtitle: "AS FORETOLD"

Jack completes Working IX.  Sara Nai transforms and remains
transformed.  She embraces him.  The universe shifts.  A
specific cutscene shows Los Angeles in 1946, from above, with
a new star in the sky.  A specific voice · gender ambiguous ·
says "It is begun."  Then a specific closed door.

Rocha's Correction on this ending, at 220 Hz, plays a specific
audio: "This is the ending Hubbard wanted.  We left it in.
You should know that he wanted it."

### Ending 4 · REFUSED THE WORK
Subtitle: "THE MOST BEAUTIFUL LIFE JACK EVER LIVED"

Jack refuses Working IX in Chapter 6.  He stays on Parsa.
Sara Nai stays with him.  They live in Talikan.  Hel Velli
visits often.  A specific montage plays their years.  Jack
dies of age at 70.  Sara mourns him in Kyrindi ceremonial
speech that is subtitled but not translated · the player must
learn it themselves via specific Chapter 3 dialogue if they
want to understand it.  A specific Kelait elder speaks the
mourning song's last verse.  Fade to specific still image ·
Jack and Sara at 62, sitting on a porch in Talikan, quiet.

This is the Rocha-team's preferred ending.  The subtitle is
not ironic.

### Ending 5 · THE CORRECTION

### Ending 6 · HUBBARD TAKES THE CREDIT
Subtitle: "SEE ABOVE"

Jack completes Working IX and never spoke to Rocha.  A specific
final cutscene shows Rafaton delivering a specific soliloquy
claiming Jack as his true heir.  The credits roll under a
specific Hubbardish self-praise monologue.  Then a specific
SCREEN CRACK effect · the ROM itself, damaged, playing an
error message reading "PLEASE INSERT CORRECTED CARTRIDGE."
The player is being told they missed something.

## Notes for the manuscript scrapbook document

- A dedication page (Hubbard's is "to a specific woman" · unnamed)
- Table of contents (12 chapters listed)
- 3-4 sample chapter openings with Hubbard's actual (fictional)
  prose · one paragraph each · to establish the tone
- Rocha's handwritten marginalia in a specific color on
  specific passages · her editorial notes calling out
  Hubbard's self-aggrandizement, his sexism, his specific
  factual errors
- A specific inserted page in Rocha's hand titled "CORRECTIONS
  WE MADE" · an itemized list of what Astro-Cortex changed
