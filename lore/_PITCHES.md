# Card-as-scene pitches: 0/Fool + I/Magician

Working from what's already painted on the cards, what each character
is doing in their canonical chapter, and the classical tarot lore.
Each pitch is a single playable scene the player can enter from the
card, with set pieces and discovery hooks. Shallow on the surface,
deeper as more cards arrive and gated content wakes up.

---

## 0 — THE FOOL : "Between Acts"

POV: John Frank. D'Ambrosio's diner, Graustark TX/LA, 3:47 AM.

### Tarot lore in play

- The Fool is the seeker who hasn't yet committed — paused in the
  archetypal instant between the safe place behind him and the cliff
  edge ahead.
- Traditional pairings:
  - **Fool ↔ World** (0 + XXI = the closing of a cycle).
  - **Fool ↔ Magician** (the will the seeker will later assume).
  - **Fool ↔ High Priestess** (conscious surface ↔ unconscious
    archive; she is the one who remembers his leap).
- REVERSED reading: paralysis disguised as openness, the 24-hour
  diner of the soul where you never actually leave. The Fool's
  "between acts" pause is canonically the chapter's whole register.

### The scene as a single room

The diner. Two booths, the counter, the kitchen pass-through, the
door to the parking lot. John's circuit is `counter ↔ booth_a ↔
booth_b ↔ kitchen ↔ door` — five soft hotspots.

Idle motion: the fluorescents tick. The clock above the door is at
3:47 AM and stays there no matter how long the player lingers — only
the painted shadow moves, very slowly. The dog is asleep against the
booth-side toekick.

### Hotspots & discoveries

- **The counter (wipe).** Wiping a clean stripe across the formica
  reveals laminate underneath. Repeat to reveal an entire
  classified-ad page (the same ad column already painted on the card)
  — but only after enough wipes for the player to realize the table
  has been carrying a hidden layer the whole time. Easter-egg cap:
  the existing 64-wipe alt-prologue payoff.

- **The bindle on the coat hook.** Take into inventory. Anachronism is
  the point: a 1990s diner worker should not have a knotted handker-
  chief on a stick. Cipher reveal flags this as "mythic time as much
  as clock time." Carry it to other cards — see Magician below.

- **The booth Frasier will sit in.** Empty. The vinyl seat is slightly
  warmer than the rest of the bench when you click it (faint heat haze
  in the rendering). Cipher: "the choice he's about to make is
  already made" — links to the VN-DESIGN-NOTES easter egg about the
  first choice being illusory.

- **The door's threshold.** A bell hangs on a coil. Clicking it from
  inside does nothing yet. After reading the Priestess card's chapter,
  the bell answers with two distinct tones — one for the door opening,
  one for the door closing — that aren't the same chord. (The
  Priestess heard him leave; she has not yet heard him arrive
  anywhere.)

- **The dog.** Petting it the first time wakes it just enough to look
  at you. Petting it after carrying the bindle: it sits up and wags
  once. The dog has a name; the name appears as a tag glint after the
  bindle's been taken (the bindle's cloth is the dog's old blanket).
  Working name placeholder: TBD per the json's "fool_dog_companion"
  cipher.

- **The cliff that isn't there.** The Precipice Door — `II — THE
  PRECIPICE DOOR.` — is painted as a labeled artifact on the card.
  Closer-zoom on it dissolves the diner mural and shows what's
  through it: a single jump-cut frame of the booth Frasier hasn't
  arrived in yet, lit at a different hour. Returning from the closer
  view restores the diner. This is the Fool's leap *as a feature of
  the room*, not an event.

### Cross-card hooks (already partially wired)

- **bindle → Magician's paint can** ("COUNTY ROAD" partial label
  completes against a map John has been carrying). Currently live.
- **fool_demoscene_mod → Emperor's throne watermark** ("rendered by
  DEMOSCENE_VIBE.EXE" appears as Dante's render credit). Currently
  live.
- **fool_inner_card → Priestess's archive Polaroid** (Elicia has
  already filed away the recursive Fool image). Currently live.
- **The dog → all subsequent dogs in the deck** (every chapter that
  introduces a new dog gets a tag-glint hint that this is the SAME
  dog moving forward in mythic time). Not yet wired.

### Expansion vectors as later cards arrive

- Strength (VIII): the dog reappears at her side; clicking it on the
  Fool card lights up a "she'll be looking for him" hint.
- Tower (XVI): the diner is on fire in a Polaroid pinned to the bell
  coil. Visible only after Tower's chapter is read.
- Moon (XVIII): a sigil drawn in counter grease appears under one of
  the place settings. Visible only after Moon's chapter unlocks
  Natalie's sigil work.

## I — THE MAGICIAN : "Cathedral of Rust and Code"

POV: Frasier Temple. The warehouse-temple, dawn-blue cathedral light
falling through corrugated steel.

### Tarot lore in play

- The Magician is the conscious will: the figure who declares "as
  above, so below" by holding all four suit-tools and routing power
  between heaven and earth. The classical posture is one arm up, one
  arm down — exactly Frasier's soldering pose.
- Traditional pairings:
  - **Magician ↔ High Priestess** (I + II = active/receptive on the
    same glyph; he draws the infinity, she archives it).
  - **Magician ↔ Emperor** (I + IV = will → realized empire; Frasier
    models D'Ambrosio's empire *before* meeting Dante).
  - **Magician ↔ Tower** (I + XVI = the tower he builds gets struck
    by the lightning he summoned).

### The scene as a single room

The workshop. Four soft zones: the workbench (center), the model
city (foreground floor), the CRT bank (back-right), the rust girders
& demon periphery (upper-left/upper-right). Camera idle motion: the
demoscene particles drift very slowly across the back wall, paused on
each click and resuming when the cursor leaves.

The infinity sigil traces itself in code-stream particles above the
workbench — a slow, looped animation drawn from the
`mag_infinity_sigil` cipher. It's there from frame one, but the
player only consciously notices it after their second visit.

### Hotspots & discoveries

- **The soldering iron.** Take into inventory. Carrying it changes
  which hotspots on OTHER cards flip to USE cursor — see Fool above
  (the demoscene module boots into render mode when the iron is
  applied to its label).

- **The model city.** Closer-zoom into the diorama. Frasier has wired
  up D'Ambrosio's empire in miniature; the steamboat, the warehouse,
  the riverfront cinema all sit in place before the prose canonically
  introduces them. Clicking the steamboat (live) wakes a corresponding
  reflection on the Fool card and a carved motif on the Emperor's
  throne (live). Three other model elements are placeholders for
  future cards' synergies.

- **The CRT bank (lower-right).** Three monitors. The leftmost
  scrolls process logs. The middle shows a hand-drawn waveform that
  *matches* the audio Aria-Empress is generating in Nicola's HUD
  overlay (live cross-card). The right is locked black until Tower
  reads — at which point it boots into a feed of the burning diner.

- **The warehouse demons (left/right periphery).** Two clickable
  shadowed silhouettes. Examining each marks `lore:demon_left_*` /
  `lore:demon_right_*`. After clicking BOTH, a third silhouette wakes
  in the rafters and Frasier's "wakey wakey, eggs and bakey" line
  fires (the card's painted quote becomes diegetic dialogue).

- **The paint can (newly added hotspot).** "COUNTY ROAD" partial
  label. Examining shows the cipher; arming the bindle and clicking
  again completes the label against a hand-drawn map John has been
  carrying. The two halves of the route reference finally meet (live).

- **The infinity sigil itself.** Clicking it sets it spinning faster
  for a beat, then resumes its slow drift. After the player has clicked
  it three times across separate visits, it leaves a faint persistent
  glyph in the upper-right of the Priestess card next to OCCULT
  SYMBOLOGY of the ASCENDANT (live cross-card gate already wired).

### Cross-card hooks (live)

- **mag_steamboat → Fool's diner reflection** and **Emperor's
  throne motif**.
- **mag_crt_readout → Empress's HUD waveform**.
- **mag_infinity_sigil → Priestess's book spine glyph**.

### Expansion vectors as later cards arrive

- Lovers (VI): two of the model-city buildings are revealed as a
  motel and a sanctuary; one of the demons holds a key.
- Tower (XVI): the back-right CRT boots. The workshop visibly cracks
  in a thin diagonal seam across the back wall that only renders if
  Tower has been read.
- World (XXI): the model city's missing center piece — until now an
  empty platform — gets a tiny figure placed on it. The figure is
  John, no longer wiping a counter.

## How the pair plays together (vertical slice)

Read Fool's chapter → click around the diner → take the bindle.
Read Magician's chapter → click around the workshop → take the iron.

Two separate scenes, each shallow, fully explorable in a couple of
minutes. The player notices on their second pass that:

1. The counter on Fool and the workbench on Magician have the same
   slight diagonal scuff.
2. The classified-ad column on Fool labels a module by name; the
   render watermark on Emperor (once read) credits the same module.
3. The steamboat shows up in three places, gradually.
4. Clicking the iron on the Fool's demoscene label boots a "lower
   third" effect across BOTH cards — a unified visual layer the
   player has now turned on.
5. Clicking the bindle on Magician's paint can completes a route
   that becomes legible on Empress when she reads it later.

Each individual discovery is small. The map of discoveries IS the
deck.
