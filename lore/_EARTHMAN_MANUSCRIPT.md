# THE PROPHET OF THE RED WORLD

*Rocha's copy · marked in blue pen · coffee-stained · found in
her trailer bookcase.  This is what Astro-Cortex adapted into
The Earthman Chronicles in 1985.  It exists in-game as a
scrapbook document (multi-page keepsake) the player can find,
read, and compare against the actual game.*

*In-game render: photocopied typescript pages, monospace font,
Rocha's marginalia in a specific blue handwritten font.
Editor's-mark items in red.  Coffee stain overlay in the
upper-right of each page.*

## [PAGE 8 · EXCERPT · CHAPTER I "THE LABORATORY"]

```
    I set the third candle at the compass-point of West
and stepped back to survey what I had wrought.  The
laboratory · MY laboratory · smelled of tetraethyl lead
and old paper.  Rafaton stood at the North point, robed
in the specific manner he had taught me, notebook and
pen in hand.  He was still · always still · in that way
he had which was neither breath nor absence of breath but
rather a suspension between the two, characteristic of
those who have crossed into the higher spheres of the Work.

    "Do you feel it, Jack?" he asked.

    "I feel the very fabric parting," I answered, and my
voice was steadier than a lesser man's would have been at
such a moment.
```

**[Rocha's marginalia, blue pen]:** *"a lesser man's" hubbard's
first act of self-praise, page 3.  he does this in EVERY chapter.
we tracked it.  17 explicit self-praise lines across the
manuscript.*

**[further down]:** *we kept this scene in the game but changed
Rafaton's line.  our Rafaton says "Do you feel it, Jack?" and
then a specific dialog option lets the player answer honestly
("No, I don't feel a thing yet") or in Hubbard's canonical way
("I feel the very fabric parting").  the honest answer earns
Rafaton's disappointment.  the canonical answer sets Jack up
for a specific manipulation in chapter 5.  both are canonical.
players who choose honestly get MORE content, not less.*

## [PAGE 47 · EXCERPT · CHAPTER III "THE SAND"]

```
    The Delvanni scout was seven feet tall, four-armed,
and armed for war.  I stood before him with none of the
tools I had commanded in my laboratory · only my mind,
my breath, and the knowledge Rafaton had drilled into me
through those long Pasadena nights.  He raised his upper
weapons in what I recognized instantly as the Delvanni
salute of tribal-inquiry.  I answered him in the same
gesture, though my two arms could not perfectly match his
four.  This was enough.  My innate understanding of the
tribal ways of primitive peoples had served me well before
and served me now.
```

**[Rocha's marginalia, larger, angry]:** *"innate understanding
of the tribal ways of primitive peoples" ← this line is why we
took his name off the box.  we could not remove hubbard's name
entirely, marketing wouldn't allow it.  but he lost the "adapted
by" credit and got demoted to "based on a lost manuscript"
credit.  small victory.  worth taking.*

**[further down]:** *in the game, Hel Velli's first meeting with
Jack goes DIFFERENTLY.  we wrote it so Hel Velli reads Jack as
"small · four-limbed · not from here" and takes Jack in with a
specific kindness that is not condescension.  Hel Velli is a
person.  not a symbol.  see also: the Kelait, whom hubbard writes
as walking wisdom-dispensers.  we made them enslaved.  the
change has weight.*

## [PAGE 89 · EXCERPT · CHAPTER V "THE COURT OF THE KYRINDI"]

```
    Sara Nai was such a woman as any man dreams of.  I
say this simply and I say it with the authority of one
who has known such women.  Her skin was the blue-silver of
Parsan moonlight before the second moon rose.  Her eyes,
turning to me from her father's throne, held me with a
recognition I could not have earned in my one day among
her people.  She knew me.  I knew her.  King Vessel of the
Kyrindi observed this recognition and did what a wise
king would do: he offered his daughter to the offworlder
who had bested Murg of the Delvanni.  I accepted.  Sara Nai
accepted.  Rafaton, as was his habit, said nothing but
approved with a small nod.
```

**[Rocha's marginalia, very forceful]:** *NOPE.*

**[continuing]:** *sara nai's arc in the game is entirely ours.
we spent three weeks on this one arc alone.  the manuscript's
"such a woman as any man dreams of" is a specific line we
DISPLAY on screen in the meeting scene, and then a sara nai
dialog option OVERRIDES it: "You did not fall in love with me
on first sight.  You looked at me like I was a chart."*

**[continuing]:** *sara nai is a SCHOLAR.  she has been reading
about earth-based ritual practice for four years.  she summoned
jack deliberately as an ACADEMIC COLLABORATOR.  she loves him
because he actually knows some things she was hoping he would
know.  he loves her because she is smart.  the manuscript's
"princess-as-prize" is a specific ideology and we rejected it.*

**[at the bottom of the page]:** *sensitivity note · when we
have the sara-jack meeting overwrite the manuscript's line
on-screen, it's the FIRST time in the game the player sees a
CORRECTION applied to hubbard's text.  it's supposed to feel
like the world is fighting back.  it does.  playtesters
consistently pointed to this moment as their turning point.*

## [PAGE 158 · EXCERPT · CHAPTER VIII "THE MOUNTAIN"]

```
    The Kelait spoke in aphorisms that a lesser philosopher
might have called cryptic.  I understood them at once, for
their wisdom was of the older variety which our Terran
schools have largely abandoned in favor of specialization
and materialist inquiry.  "The salt in the pot is not the
same salt as in the sea," said Mother Kanel, and I nodded,
for I saw at once what she meant · that the essence of a
thing is transformed by its context, and that the wisdom
which serves in one venue does not always serve in another.
```

**[Rocha's marginalia, blue pen, small handwriting]:** *the
kelait joke.  we made this work.  in the game, "the salt in
the pot is not the same salt as in the sea" is · in fact · a
specific KELAIT COOKING INSTRUCTION.  Mother Kanel's kitchen
has a specific pot.  the salt jar is next to it.  jack can
figure this out by talking to the kelait cook.  the aphorism
is a recipe.*

**[continuing]:** *BUT.  once trust is earned with the kelait,
their ACTUAL wisdom emerges, and it is not aphorisms.  it is
specific.  Mother Kanel's real wisdom includes the line "you
will die.  I will die.  Sara Nai will die.  Rafaton will die.
Hel Velli will die.  Rocha will die.  the two moons will die.
the last light will be a specific color none of us have ever
seen.  live now."  that line is not in the manuscript.  it is
ours.  Astro-Cortex tried to cut it.  we did not let them.*

## [PAGE 213 · EXCERPT · CHAPTER XI "THE GREAT WORK"]

```
    Sara Nai stood before me as she truly was.  The blue-

robe, and what stood before me was Woman Herself · the
face of Babalon, whose name I had spoken in my Pasadena
laboratory those many months ago.  She had been Babalon
all along.  I understood this now as I understood the

outside of time for a duration I cannot describe.
```

**[Rocha's marginalia]:** *"Woman Herself" · capitalized.

readings, player's choice.*

**[continuing]:** *the third reading (jack projecting his
desire) unlocks additional sara dialogue later that reframes
their whole relationship.  the player who picks the third

**[at the very bottom, tiny handwriting]:** *my colleague j.f.

## [PAGE 244 · EXCERPT · CHAPTER XII "THE BABALON WORKING"]

```
    Rafaton stood at my right hand as I completed the
terminal working.  He said, and I remember his words
precisely: "You have done what I could not, Jack.  You
are my SUCCESSOR."  His voice broke.  He was moved.
Behind him, Parsa fell into shadow and rose again, and
in that shadow-rising I understood that the world had
changed.  I had changed it.  I had done what Rafaton had
guided me toward.  I turned to Sara Nai · to Babalon ·
and said what needed saying.  We stepped forward together
onto the throne that was ours.
```

**[Rocha's marginalia, in a specific different pen · larger,
darker · almost defacing the page]:**

*we did not accept this ending.  we did not accept it and we
put it in the game as ONE of six possible endings and it is the
worst one and the game tells the player it is the worst one
via specific mechanics.*

*we buried a specific audio layer under rafaton's "you have
done what I could not, jack" line at 220 hz.  what actually
plays under it is: "you have done what I could not, jack.
now let's talk about my percentage."  I recorded that line
myself.  I have never been more satisfied with a piece of
work.*

*if you find this document in the game, you have already
played some of it.  you know what we did.  the game is
better than the manuscript.  we made it better.  we did
it for parsons.  we did it because parsons could not do
it himself.  · a.r., march 1985*

## [ATTACHED · Rocha's inserted pages · titled "CORRECTIONS WE
## MADE"]

```
   CORRECTIONS WE MADE
   a specific document
   for the record
   a.r. · qa · astro-cortex · march 1985

  1. HEL VELLI
     manuscript: primitive tribal warrior who Jack civilizes
     game: fully-realized person with a mother he writes to
     weekly, 22 years of combat memory, and specific opinions
     about specific things.  Jack must earn his respect.

  2. SARA NAI
     manuscript: princess-as-prize offered by her father to
     the offworlder-hero
     game: kyrindi scholar of kelait rituals who summoned Jack
     deliberately as an academic collaborator.  loves Jack
     for who he actually is.  disagrees with him openly.
     refuses working IX because she has read the kelait
     interpretation and she knows what it does.

  3. THE KELAIT
     manuscript: mystical children-shaped sages
     game: enslaved workers in delvanni mines whose aphorisms
     are secretly recipes and escape plans.  earned trust
     unlocks their actual wisdom, which is real and moving.

  4. RAFATON
     manuscript: wise mentor, always right
     game: revealed in chapter 5 to be nalat, working for the
     order of the prophet, taking a specific cut of each of
     jack's workings.  hubbard's self-insert becomes the
     game's actual antagonist.

  5. THE SCARLET WOMAN
     manuscript: literally babalon, no ambiguity
     game: three canonical interpretations available (literal
     babalon / priestess ghyra playing role / jack's projection).
     player picks their reading.  all three are canonical.

  6. THE ORDER OF THE PROPHET
     manuscript: does not exist
     game: parsan cult secretly guiding jack's workings for
     their own specific purpose.  their symbol (which we chose
     carefully) is an eight-pointed star.

  7. WORKING IX
     manuscript: canonical ending, ritual completes,
     protagonist becomes prophet
     game: one of six endings.  the WORST one.  the game
     tells you it is the worst via specific mechanics.

  8. THE CORRECTION ENDING
     manuscript: does not exist
     game: the TRUE ending.  breaks the fourth wall.  the
     dedication for jack.

  9. ROCHA (as NPC)
     manuscript: does not exist
     game: recruitable in chapter 4 by asking a specific
     question.  her presence in the game is the game
     telling on itself.  she will not shut up about it.

  10. THE 220-HZ LAYER
      manuscript: does not exist
      game: audio under specific hubbardish scenes plays what
      hubbard actually meant.  players who notice can extract
      the actual audio via the compendium.

  11. THE KELAIT MOURNING SONG
      manuscript: mentioned once, half a paragraph
      game: fully-composed piece of music, played at specific
      emotional moments.  shares its final bar with a specific
      song from another oneironautics game (see wilson-ashe
      music-box tune in pirate summer · cross-reference).

  12. THE ENDINGS
      manuscript: 1 ending (Working IX completes)
      game: 6 endings.  most players get REFUSED THE WORK on
      first play, which is our favorite ending, and the one
      we consider the truest to parsons's actual life.

  · a.r.
  qa · astro-cortex software
  march 1985
```
