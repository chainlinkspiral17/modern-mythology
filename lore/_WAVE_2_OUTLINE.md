# Wave 2 · Mechanics & Gameplay Evolution

Wave 1 took the Tarot Gauntlet from a Fool's-diner prototype to a
white-box of 24 canonical scenarios + 21 cameos across eight major
arcana. Wave 1's contract: every chapter has a playable room, a
canon-aligned hand, and three difficulty tiers. Wave 2 turns the
white-box into a game.

The premise of Wave 2 is that the player now has *enough rooms* to
make meta-decisions, *enough cards* to make per-run identity
decisions, and *enough cameos* to make cross-arcana decisions. The
mechanics need to honor those decisions.

## Pillars

### 1. The Run Ledger (cross-scenario memory)

A persistent ledger that records lore_tokens, leap-tokens, and
visitor-connection-tokens earned across every run. Several scenarios
already write tokens; Wave 2 makes the ledger consequential.

- **Carry tokens forward.** Tokens earned in any scenario become
  available as `requires_token` conditions for visitors / cards /
  thresholds in later scenarios. The Frasier-gives-the-beam binary
  in the Chariot cameo, for example, changes which visitor walks
  into the ch20 emperor scenario.
- **The ledger is visible.** A small wax-seal page in the gallery
  shows accumulated tokens grouped by chapter. Mouse-over reveals
  the moment the token was earned.
- **Doctrine.** A reserved sub-token. Doctrine accrued by Paul's
  cameos compounds Nicola's doubt on the next Empress run started.
  This is the first explicit cross-arcana penalty.
- **Implementation.** Extend `SaveSystem` to persist a `ledger`
  Dictionary keyed by token id, with arrays of (scenario_id,
  timestamp, source_visitor_id) tuples. Token-test predicates added
  to visitor / threshold / card requires-clauses.

### 2. Hand Drafting & Persistent Decks

Right now hands are static JSON. Wave 2 introduces *drafting*:
between runs, the player can spend insight earned in winning runs
to add one card to a hand (from a curated pool), or remove one.

- **Picker addition.** A pre-run "Hand" panel shows the deck the
  player will bring; one slot is editable, drafted from a four-card
  reveal generated by the host's location + the player's hand.
- **Constraints.** Drafted cards must be `available_in_locations`
  for the host, or universal. Drafting a card carries a doubt
  penalty on the next run.
- **Implementation.** A `draft_pool.json` per hand listing eligible
  add-cards. A `_drafted_cards` array on each hand persisted via
  `SaveSystem`. Engine merges into `_action_cards` at run start.

### 3. The Companion Slot

A second visitor-as-companion that travels with the player across
runs in the same chapter. The companion has their own patience
clock, but offers a once-per-run helper effect.

- **Earn a companion.** Winning a hard-tier scenario binds one of
  the connected visitors as the chapter's companion until the
  chapter is reset.
- **Companion verbs.** The companion has a 5th verb (`help_with`)
  not in the standard 4. Verbalizes the relationship.
- **Implementation.** New visitor field `companion_help_action`
  describing the helper effect. Engine reads `_companion_vid` per
  arcana, presents the help action as a card in the hand.

### 4. Time-of-Day Variants

The same scenario at a different time of day swaps the visitor pool,
the ambient palette, and the difficulty tilt — without authoring
new scenarios. The picker shows the three difficulties; tapping a
difficulty long-presses into a time picker.

- **Three slots per scenario.** Morning / Afternoon / Evening, each
  with a small visitor-pool override (a fourth visitor swapped in)
  and an ambient-pool override.
- **Implementation.** Extend setup JSON with `time_variants` keyed
  by slot. Engine merges the active slot's override on load.

### 5. The Cross-Chapter Cameo Chain

A linked series of cameos that form an arc across chapters: e.g.,
*John's seven days* — John at the warehouse → John at the
Roberts → John before Montreal → John at the bandstand. Each
unlocks the next. The chain pays out a unique ending the host
chapters can't.

- **Three chains designed.** John's Seven Days, Antonio's Two
  Months Before, Frasier's Sunday Errand Loop.
- **Implementation.** Cameo setup JSONs carry a `chain` field;
  picker visualizes chain progress (3/4 nodes lit). A `chain_payoff`
  scenario unlocks at chain completion.

### 6. Sanity & Doubt as Variable Currencies

Right now sanity and doubt are dials. Wave 2 makes them *spendable
in different denominations* per arcana — the Magician spends doubt
for fuel, the Priestess spends doubt for insight, the Emperor spends
sanity for authority, etc. The same number on the dial means
different things in different rooms, surfaced via tooltip and the
hand's color palette.

- **Conversion cards.** Each hand gets one universal conversion
  card whose rate matches the arcana's economy.
- **Implementation.** Each arcana's engine block defines a
  `currency_conversion` table; conversion cards reference it
  symbolically.

### 7. The Round-the-Clock Calendar

The eight arcana sit on a 24-hour clock (Fool dawn, Magician
sundown, etc.). Wave 2 introduces a meta-calendar where the order
in which the player completes scenarios advances a real clock — and
some endings are time-of-day-locked.

- **Visualization.** A clock face on the main menu showing which
  hours have been "lit" by a completed run.
- **Implementation.** Each scenario tags an `hour_of_day` integer.
  Calendar state persisted via SaveSystem.

## Sequencing for Wave 2

A roughly four-phase build:

1. **Ledger + cross-arcana tokens.** Lowest risk, highest leverage —
   the data is already being written; we just need to honor it.
2. **Companion slot + hand drafting.** Player-facing additions that
   make the system feel like progression instead of a series of
   one-off rooms.
3. **Time-of-day variants + cross-chapter chains.** Authorial
   leverage — many new "scenarios" with little new content.
4. **Currencies + clock.** Late, polish-tier — these are the systems
   that make the eight arcana feel mechanically distinct without
   requiring new scenarios.

## Out of scope for Wave 2 (held for Wave 3)

- Minor-arcana scenarios (cups, swords, wands, pentacles).
- Tournament / score-attack mode.
- True multiplayer / passing-the-controller cameos.
- Procedural visitor generation.

## Wave 2 success criteria

- Player can complete a chapter, see something they did change the
  next chapter's room.
- Player can draft one card into their hand between runs.
- Player can bind a companion that helps in the next run.
- Player sees the chain payoff for at least one cross-chapter chain.
- Player understands sanity / doubt mean different things in
  different rooms.

If all five land, the white-box becomes a game. Wave 3 is then the
visual / audio / endgame polish + minor arcana.
