# IX — THE HERMIT : "One Lantern, No Audience"

POV: an un-named man. Like the Strength figure, no given
name. The chapter's specificity is the lantern's cone, not
the figure's biography.

## Tarot lore in play

- Mirror pair: IX + XII = 21 → **Hermit ↔ Hanged Man**.
  Chosen solitude ↔ forced suspension. Same vertical, opposite
  shadow direction.
- Adjacencies: VIII Strength ↔ IX Hermit (mastery →
  contemplation); IX Hermit ↔ X Wheel (contemplation → fate's
  turn — but Wheel is Erica, not the Hermit; the deck breaks
  POV continuity here).
- Classical Hermit is wisdom-as-light, the solitary lantern,
  the figure who has stepped out of the world. Modern
  Mythology renders the cone of light as six-foot reach — the
  space of one held thought.

## The scene as a single room

An unmapped clearing — somewhere off the Acadian river, hours
past where anyone would walk. The figure stands at center,
lantern raised, staff leaning against him.

Idle motion: the lantern flame flickers; the staff is still;
the figure's shadow falls upward (Hermit ↔ Hanged Man mirror,
live).

## Hotspots & discoveries

- **The lantern**. Six-sided, one panel cracked, narrow cone.
- **The staff**. The Hierophant's crozier without the
  institution; the Emperor's sceptre without the throne.
- **The six-pointed star**. Inside the lantern's broken
  panel. Seal of Solomon — wisdom-as-light. Source is inside
  the carrier, not above.
- **The void** (surrounding darkness).
- **The upward-falling shadow**. Mirror of Hanged Man's
  downward shadow.
- **A VHS tape labeled HERMIT_01** (gated by Priestess Anya
  tapes — live). Tucked into the lantern base. Elicia
  archives the Hermit too.
- **The staff/crozier echo** (gated by Hierophant authority —
  live). Three vestments of one rod.
- **Hanged-shadow confirmation** (gated by Hanged Man shadow —
  live reciprocal). The two cards complete each other.

## Cross-card hooks (live)

- Priestess → Hermit: HERMIT_01 tape in lantern base.
- Hierophant → Hermit: staff is third position of same wood.
- Hanged Man ↔ Hermit: mirror shadow confirmation.

## Expansion vectors as later cards arrive

- The Hermit is the deck's most withholdable character. Vol6
  could open with him as a named figure, or could leave him
  as the deck's permanent anonymous. The deck's choice will
  be its argument about whether wisdom requires identification.

## SAMPLE SCRIPT — "HERMIT_01 (full transcript)"

Mode: b — found document. Mirror of ANYA_03 from the Priestess
chapter (II). Elicia archives the Hermit too. The tape is
mostly silence. The transcript is mostly silence rendered as
timestamps.

```
[HERMIT_01 / cassette side A, full / 47:11 total]
[transcribed e.l. — undated, sometime after walking him to
 the clearing and turning around]

[00:00]  tape starts. ambient. crickets, faint. very faint
         water — the river is far enough that this is residual
         in the air, not heard. no human sound.

[01:14]  the lantern is lit. there is a brief crackle of fuel
         going to flame. it is so quiet on this tape that the
         crackle is the loudest event recorded so far.

[03:22]  he breathes in.

[03:24]  he breathes out.

[03:28]  he sits down. cloth on grass. very small.

[07:11]  he speaks. one word, very flat.

         HERMIT:   "okay."

[07:12]  he is silent.

[19:45]  he speaks. ten words, very even, no inflection.

         HERMIT:   "i did not realize i was waiting for the
                    lantern to gutter."

[19:55]  the lantern is not guttering. the lantern is fine.
         he did not realize. that is the report.

[24:00]  twenty-four minutes in. nothing.

[31:30]  rustle. very brief. possibly a small animal at the
         edge of the clearing. possibly his sleeve.

[31:30]  he does not turn his head.

[40:00]  the lantern crackles again. fuel pop.

[42:11]  he speaks. three words.

         HERMIT:   "elicia, you can stop the tape."

[42:13]  i did not stop the tape.

[42:14]  he speaks again. one word.

         HERMIT:   "fine."

[42:15]  he is silent for the rest of the tape.

[47:11]  tape ends.

[notes, e.l.: he heard me. he did not see me. i was sitting
 farther back than that. he knew the tape was running because
 he knew it would be — i had told him no tape, but the no-tape
 was the agreement i broke when i pressed record. he did not
 ask me to stop because i broke the agreement. he asked because
 he wanted to give me the chance to be the one to keep it. i
 did not keep it. the tape is HERMIT_01 because there will not
 be a HERMIT_02. that is the part i agreed to.]
```

The script does:
- mirrors ANYA_03 (mostly speech) with HERMIT_01 (mostly
  silence). The Anya tape and the Hermit tape are the deck's
  most opposite recordings; both made by the same archivist.
- canonizes ELICIA AS THE PERSON HOLDING THE RECORDER on the
  Hermit card. The Priestess's archive extends to the Hermit's
  clearing. II → IX is the deck's most direct relational link
  outside the painted scene.
- gives the Hermit two lines and one word total in 47 minutes
  — his speech is rationed like the lantern's fuel.
- canonizes the Hermit's name: never spoken. He calls Elicia
  by name; she does not call him by name. The deck's withhold
  rendered as an acoustic decision.
- canonizes the no-second-tape rule. HERMIT_01 is the only
  tape because Elicia made an agreement she broke once and
  the broken once is the entire record.
- ends with Elicia's annotation, which is the script's true
  body. The tape is the artifact; the notes are the chapter.
  The reader gets the Hermit through the archivist who broke
  her word to record him and then kept her word about not
  doing it again.
