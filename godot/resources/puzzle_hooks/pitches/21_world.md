## SAMPLE SCRIPT — "Frog Knows Best, Mostly (oracle reading)"

Mode: b — found document. The deck's only oracle delivers
its closing reading. One brief verdict per arcana, each
qualified per the chapter title. The Frog's discipline is
"mostly" — partial knowledge as the deck's only honest
form.

```
                FROG KNOWS BEST, MOSTLY
                  closing reading, sunset
                       (qualified)

I will read the deck I have been near. I will read it once,
in order, with the qualifications I am allowed. The
qualifications are mostly. The reading is mostly. The
discipline of the reading is that the mostly is not a
weakness — the mostly is the part that lets the reading
be true.

0 — THE FOOL.
   He did not move. That is the chapter. (Mostly.)
   He is moving now. The clock advanced to dawn.
   The chapter that did not move is the chapter that
   ended.

I — THE MAGICIAN.
   He built it. He did not realize the gesture would
   be borrowed. That is fine; gestures are made to be
   borrowed.

II — THE HIGH PRIESTESS.
   She recorded what was given. She kept the agreements
   she could keep. She broke one. The breaking is in
   the archive. The agreement is in the archive too.
   Both are correct. (Mostly.)

III — THE EMPRESS.
   Two figures, one body. The split is the chapter.
   The collapse of the split is the chapter that
   follows the chapter. The body is fine. (Mostly.)

IV — THE EMPEROR.
   The throne passes. He knows it. He wrote the
   rule for what not to write.

V — THE HIEROPHANT.
   The institution speaks. The child hears. The
   dress bites. The booth dials. The chapter is the
   chain. (Mostly.)

VI — THE LOVERS.
   The card stays face-down. This was correct. The
   refusal is the reading. The pairs are accounted
   for. The accounting is not the consummation.
   (Mostly.)

VII — THE CHARIOT.
   The wreck is the wreck. The wreck was warned. The
   warning was not picked up. The chapter is the
   not-picking-up. (Mostly.)

VIII — STRENGTH.
   The hand is the hand. The lion is the dog. The
   crossing is the crossing nobody made into a story.
   (Mostly.)

IX — THE HERMIT.
   The lantern is small. The tape is one. The
   agreement was kept on the second attempt. (Mostly.)

X — THE WHEEL OF FORTUNE.
   The wheel turned at the desk. The fortress had
   transparent walls. The lawyer worked the night.
   The night ended.

XI — JUSTICE.
   Refused. Flipped anyway. Split. The two readings
   are both correct. The shattered scales are the
   verdict carried forward. (Mostly.)

XII — THE HANGED MAN.
   She waited. He did not answer. The voicemails
   were hers. The chapter was hers. (Mostly.)

XIII — DEATH.
   The hand was gloved. The ward had two empty beds.
   One died. One walked out. Both empties were
   correct emptings. (Mostly.)

XIV — TEMPERANCE.
   He did not write more on Tuesday than he had to.
   The discipline was the content. (Mostly.)

XV — THE DEVIL.
   He knew by call seven. He did not pick up. The bar
   opened again. (Mostly.)

XVI — THE TOWER.
   The engine could not render itself failing.
   The log truncates itself. The file size increases.
   (Mostly.)

XVII — THE STAR.
   She poured the water into the river. She included
   the smoke in inventory. She did not interpret it.
   (Mostly.)

XVIII — THE MOON.
   She read out of the static what she wrote on the
   page. The verbs came home. The candle was hers.
   (Mostly.)

XIX — THE SUN.
   The pattern was four motes. The thank-you was
   42 seconds long. The reply was correct silence.
   (Mostly.)

XX — JUDGEMENT.
   Refused. Held. The gathering was rendered. The
   verdict was not. The gathering was enough. (Mostly.)

XXI — THE WORLD.
   I am reading this now. The wreath closes. The
   center is empty by design. The dancer was not
   coming. The corners are filled. The count is
   restored to sixty-four. The chapter ends.

   (MOSTLY.)

   I am the Frog. I know best, mostly. The mostly is
   the only part I am sure of. The mostly is the
   honest reading. The deck closes.

                                                    —🐸
```

The script does:
- closes the deck with a one-stanza reading per arcana,
  each qualified by "mostly." The Frog's discipline of
  partial knowledge is the deck's signature form.
- canonizes the reading order. The closing pass goes 0
  through XXI in order; the deck's structural sequence is
  the chapter's working spine.
- gives the Frog a voice. Two lines per card, the second
  often qualifying the first. The deck's only explicit
  oracle is also the deck's most patient reader.
- canonizes each through-line: the Fool's clock, the
  Magician's borrowed gesture, the Priestess's archive,
  the Empress's body, the Emperor's rule for what not to
  write, the Hierophant's chain, the Lovers' accounting,
  the Chariot's not-picking-up, the Strength figure's
  unstoried crossing, the Hermit's one tape, the Wheel's
  worked night, the Justice flip, the Hanged Man's hers,
  the Death glove, the Temperance discipline, the Devil's
  call seven, the Tower's recursion, the Star's water,
  the Moon's verbs coming home, the Sun's 42-second
  thank-you, the Judgement gathering, the World's closing.
  Twenty-two cards, twenty-two threads, all named.
- ends signed by an emoji: —🐸. The deck's only emoji
  signature. The Frog signs in its own form. The deck
  closes with the smallest possible signature for the
  smallest possible oracle.
- ends with the deck's whole working program: "the mostly
  is the only part I am sure of. the mostly is the honest
  reading. the deck closes." The deck's premise was
  partial knowledge from the start; the Frog says so out
  loud at the close.
