# VII — THE CHARIOT : "Ember & Ash Restaurant — Two Horses, One Wreck"

POV: Antonio (the_charioteer, D.O.A.). The wreck of his chariot.
The chapter is a forensic surface — a BBS diagnostic page in
diagram form, read after the crash.

## Tarot lore in play

- Mirror pair: VII + XIV = 21 → **Chariot ↔ Temperance**. Drive
  ↔ moderation, motion ↔ blending. The Chariot crashed because
  the duality was never controlled; Temperance is the chapter
  Frank reads in moderate light after.
- Adjacencies: VI Lovers ↔ VII Chariot (the choice the Lovers
  refused becomes the motion the Charioteer commits); VII
  Chariot ↔ VIII Strength (failed force ↔ quiet hand).
- Classical Chariot is willed direction, mastery of opposites
  via the driver's resolve. Modern Mythology renders the
  failure of that mastery — the chariot is the wreck.

## The scene as a single room

The wreck site, rendered as a BBS diagnostic page. Top: the

map of Ember & Ash), system status. Left column: food menu,

restaurant's BBS the night the SYSOP went D.O.A.

Idle motion: cursor blink at the bottom of the status panel;
the wreck centerpiece does not move (DOA is permanent); the
faint smoke at the wreck's edges rises one pixel per ten
seconds.

The script does:
- introduces the chapter's working tense — past, but barely.
  The texts are real-time; the wreck is in the next minute.
  The reader knows the SYSOP is D.O.A. before they read the
  last text; Antonio doesn't.
- canonizes the Empress dinner — Antonio sat next to Erica
  Campbell ("the lawyer i told you about") at D'Ambrosio's the
  morning of the chapter, and noticed Nicola did not eat. The
  III, X, and VII chapters share a breakfast nobody else knows
  about.
- canonizes "the chariot's flame" as the drink Daigle and
  Antonio used to call something else. The Devil card's middle-
  barstool drink has a private name between two friends — Daigle
  put it on the menu, and Antonio noticed.
- introduces "Q" as Quentin's chat handle — economy of names
- ends on the leaning scaffolding. Antonio sees the failure
  one minute before it happens. The chapter's "scaffolding
  check in progress" status line is Antonio's last text.
