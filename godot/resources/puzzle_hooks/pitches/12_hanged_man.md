# XII — THE HANGED MAN : "Gravity is Optional After Midnight"

POV: Natalie David. Simon's apartment, LA. 1:47 AM. Vigil.
Natalie's first chapter; her second is XVIII Moon, same body
six chapters later.

## Tarot lore in play

- Mirror pair: XII + IX = 21 → **Hanged Man ↔ Hermit**.
  Forced suspension ↔ chosen solitude. Same vertical,
  opposite shadow direction.
- Adjacencies: XI Justice ↔ XII Hanged Man (verdict ↔ wait);
  XII Hanged Man ↔ XIII Death (suspension → transformation).
- Classical Hanged Man = suspension, sacrifice, the
  upside-down view. Modern Mythology renders the suspension
  as Natalie waiting at someone else's kitchen counter for
  the phone to ring.

## The scene as a single room

Simon's apartment kitchen. Counter, two chairs (one used).
Natalie at the counter — not hanging, but suspended in the
same vertical the card demands. The maroon-backed deck on
the counter, cut once. A single-wick beeswax candle, half-
burned. Her notebook open to a page of unfinished sigils.
The phone log on her side.

Idle motion: the candle flame flickers; the wax line drips
once every forty seconds; the clock on the microwave reads
1:47 AM and stays there.

## SAMPLE SCRIPT — "Natalie's three voicemails to Simon"

Mode: a — the moment slightly before / DURING the painted
scene. The phone log on the chapter records three outgoing
calls to Simon (no answer). The voicemails she left are not
in the painted log. Here they are. The three are timestamped
to the log's three outgoing rows.

```
[VOICEMAIL 1 — to simon.j — Natalie David — 23:14 PT]
[duration 00:38]

Hi, it's me. I know you don't want to —
[breath]
I know. Okay. I'm at the apartment. I have the key. I am
just letting you know I am here. I am not — I won't — I am
not opening anything. I am sitting at the counter. The
candle you bought me is on the counter. Did you buy me the
candle. I think you did. It is half. I'm going to wait until
it is the other half and then I'm going to call again.
Half a candle is how long I think this is.
Okay. Bye.


[VOICEMAIL 2 — to simon.j — Natalie David — 00:12 PT]
[duration 00:51]

The candle is two-thirds. I lied about half. I lied because
half is how long I wished this was. Half a candle is two
hours and I have been here three. The candle was already
half when I sat down because YOU lit it before you left.
That is a thing you did. You left the candle lit. The cards
are in front of me. I am not — I will tell you I have not
turned any of them. I cut the deck. I did the cut you taught
me. You taught me one cut. The cut is the deck cut once
toward the dealer. I have done that and I have not turned a
card. I am waiting for you to tell me to. Call me.

[breath]

Okay. Bye.


[VOICEMAIL 3 — to simon.j — Natalie David — 00:58 PT]
[duration 00:17]

Simon.
Simon, the candle is gone. I am at the counter. Call me.
I'm staying.
Bye.
```

The script does:
- gives Natalie a working voice and a relational specificity
  — she's not just "the figure suspended," she's a person
  with a habit of self-correcting on the next sentence ("I
  lied because half is how long I wished this was")
- canonizes the candle as Simon's gift — and as already
  lit when Natalie arrived. Simon left the candle burning
  when he left. This is the chapter's deepest cruelty: he
  was preparing the room for her arrival but did not stay
  for it.
- canonizes one cut of the deck as Natalie's only learned
  technique. "You taught me one cut." The Hanged Man's
  deck-within-deck is half-Natalie, half-Simon — he taught
  her the gesture; she does it without him.
- the voicemails decrease in length (38 → 51 → 17 seconds).
  The chapter's whole pacing in three rows. By the third,
  she is naming him only. By the third, she has stopped
  apologizing.
- doesn't show what Simon does or doesn't do. The chapter
  declines to render Simon. The voicemails go into a phone
  that does not answer.
