# SUBURBAN BLIGHT
## a New Auburn / Harmony Creek garage band — Jesse Henderson, Nate Yost, Carl Boudreaux, Em Vargas

Four kids who have been a band since April. Jesse on Telecaster
(frontman, writes the riffs), Nate on bass (seventeen, traffic-light
green hair, skateboarder, plays his father's hand-me-down Fender
Precision), Carl on drums (sixteen, smaller than the rest, wiry, plays
a left-handed kit right-handed because that is how Nate's father had
the kit and that is how Carl learned), Em on backing vocals and
occasional keys (eighteen, three inches taller than anybody in the
garage, *I am from Fort Worth* energy). Friends-of-the-band who are
not in the band but who are, by the end of the summer of vol6, the
band's audience-of-record: **Ben Kowalski** (the Pit Stop, observer),
**Sam Miller** (the Kwik Stop, the protagonist), **Maya Daigle**
(Cosmic Comics, the archivist).

The band practices in Jesse's garage Tuesday and Thursday at seven,
and in Nate's garage at the same time whenever Jesse's parents are
having Nate's parents over for dinner.

## the songs

Confirmed in the canon as of August:

> **GRID FAILURE**
> *the first song the band finished. three-chord ascending Telecaster
> figure over a driving eighth-note bass and a snare that hits on the
> and of two and four. Em's backing vocal is a long sustained syllable
> — ohhhh — that sits under the chord changes like a drone. The lyrics
> are mostly about a specific kind of failure of a specific kind of
> electrical grid; Jesse will admit later that the lyrics are not
> especially good. The song is mostly riff. The riff is the chapter.*

> **SUBSTATION NINE**
> *the song Jesse has been working on since mid-July. the lead break is,
> by his own internal sense, becoming a thing he has begun to trust.
> the song's title is the band's quietest signal — Substation Nine is
> the NexCorp power substation at the corner of Cypress Point and
> Linden whose hum is the loudest in the development, and the song's
> long sustained bridge is, by Jesse's structural decision, set in a
> key designed to make the audience's molars register a beat against
> the substation's hum if they hear the song outdoors anywhere within
> two blocks of the substation. The Foxhole on Live Oak is within two
> blocks. The Saturday 8/22 show will be the song's first public
> performance.*

> **BLOWBACK**
> *Carl wrote the bones. Jesse wrote the lyrics over a single
> afternoon at the Pit Stop in mid-July. The song is two minutes
> twenty seconds, all snare-and-tom, no cymbal, a single sustained
> bass note under the whole thing, Em's vocal is the angriest the
> band has captured to date. About the small specific kind of
> dishonesty that happens when an authority tells you nothing has
> happened and you can see that something has.*

> **THE NEW ONE CARL WROTE**
> *(working title pending). Carl's first complete song. The band has
> not, by mid-August, played it for an audience. They are running it
> in practice. It is, by Em's quiet read, the best thing the band has
> done. Carl does not yet know.*

In rotation but not yet recorded, working titles: ASHBERRY (Jesse's;
about Diego's empty driveway, written in early August, the band has
not yet decided whether to put it in the set), STRIP MALL ECONOMICS
(Nate's; bass-heavy; one chord), and a cover the band has been
rehearsing of Minor Threat's *In My Eyes* that Carl wants to play and
that Em has not yet agreed to.

## the tape

A single self-released demo cassette, dubbed thirty-three times on
Em's stepfather's home dual-deck. The cassette is a Maxell XLII, hand-
labeled in Em's neat block print:

> **SUBURBAN BLIGHT · GARAGE 4 · APR–JUL**

The insert is a single folded sheet of half-letter cardstock, cut and
folded by Maya at the Cosmic Comics paper cutter as a quiet favor.
The cover art is Jesse's: a hand-drawn block-letter SUBURBAN BLIGHT
arched across the top, the rest of the cover taken up by a Geiger-
esque tentacled monster wrapped around and eating an oversized
NexCorp™ Spiral logo, captioned in small marker at the bottom: *art
& lyrics by jesse henderson, all songs ©2025 the band collectively,
do not feed the corporations.*

## the zine — SUBURBAN BLIGHT PRESS RELEASE

A small show-announcement zine Jesse runs as a single-fold half-
letter sheet, photocopied at Cosmic Comics on Sunday afternoons when
Rick has the back-room machine free. Issues are numbered. As of
mid-August Jesse has done #003 through #008, which are the issues
that correspond to the band's six bookings since April. Each issue
announces one show, lists the date and venue and lineup, includes
one Jesse-drawn illustration (typically the tentacled monster doing
something incongruous), and on the back has a small running column
called *FROM THE GARAGE* — Jesse's notes on whatever he wants to
notice that week, one or two paragraphs of unedited prose that have
become, by issue #006, the part Maya looks forward to.

### gallery / music-player integration

The cassette is the primary music-player surface for Act II. Each
track, played, surfaces:

- **liner-notes panel** (the case insert plus the long credit)
- **dedications** that cross-link to other meta-game files (e.g., the
  thanks-to Mr. Henderson Sr. links to the Henderson Donation box at
  Cosmic Comics; the thanks-to Walt Yost links to a small Walt sketch
  in Maya's *General Store*–era unpublished sketchbook page — wait,
  not canon, scratch that — links to nothing yet; reserved)
- **the photograph or flyer** for the recorded venue (the Foxhole
  flyer for tracks 6, 7, 8 as relevant)

Sequence-listening unlocks:
- **Side A, in order** → unlocks Jesse's *FROM THE GARAGE* run of
  zine columns (issues #003 through #008)
- **Side B, in order** → unlocks the three live-recording back-
  matter notes Em added with a felt-tip pen on the inside of the
  cassette insert: the dates, the apologies for sound quality, the
  small acknowledgment ("the july 28 take of substation nine is the
  one diego heard. he has not heard it back. — em.")
- **Side A + Side B in single sitting** → unlocks the band's
  Substation Nine 8/22 live recording, which is not on the tape, and
  which exists only because Maya stood at the front of the room on
  her phone for the song's entire 7:42 length; the recording lives
  in Maya's archive and only the meta-game's full-listen completion
  surfaces it. The recording is the volume's most important single
  piece of audio.

### stickers

Em prints stickers in runs of approximately a hundred at a time at
the Kinko's in New Auburn. The design is Jesse's tentacled monster
in a single color (black on bright orange paper) with the band name
in his block lettering across the top and, in small letters at the
bottom: *NEW AUBURN'S OWN.* The stickers cost a dollar at shows and
zero dollars if Em likes you. By mid-August there are stickers on
the Kwik Stop dumpster, the back of Diego's truck (which is missing
but the sticker survived the impound), the inside of Maya's locker
at Cosmic Comics, Sam's bedroom window frame, the underside of the
Pit Stop diner counter (Ben put it there), the inside of Walt
Yost's guitar case, a stop sign at Cypress Point and Linden (Jesse
put it there one night, not at Em's suggestion), and on the back
of one of Mr. Henderson Sr.'s vintage Graflex cameras — which Em
put there on a dare from Jesse and which Mr. Henderson, who has
seen the sticker, has not, in two weeks, removed.

The sticker on the Graflex is the chapter's quietest single
artifact.
