# SAM MILLER · accumulation
## Kwik Stop · Gallatin Ave · Harmony Creek Estates

Eighteen. Brown hair to her shoulders. The Kwik Stop pinks-and-blues uniform polo, untucked. Her name tag, plastic, reads **SAM** in marker because the corporate label-maker has been broken since 2019. She's been at the store since she was sixteen on a hardship work permit. She's been with Diego since she was seventeen. Her phone is on the counter face-up.

This is what she has.

A 90-minute Maxell XLII cassette, dubbed at a friend's house on a borrowed dual-deck. Side A and Side B. The case insert is hand-cut from the back of a Kwik Stop register paper roll and folded by Diego at the kitchen table. The handwriting is Diego's: a small careful block print he learned from his grandmother's grocery lists.

The insert is in black ballpoint pen and one passage of red. Track titles are exactly as written. Some are real band names, some are not — Sam has never asked.

## II. THE ZINE — *"GAS STATION OBSERVATIONS"*

Sam has been making this for fourteen months. It is photocopied at the Kinko's that closed in March (she has a small stockpile of pre-made copies in the storeroom under the cleaning supplies). She has never sold one. She has given six away. Three of them to Diego. One to Maya Daigle. One to Jen the day manager. One to a regular customer she likes named ROY who buys a single Lone Star tallboy every Friday at 5:48 PM.

The zine is half-letter size, eight pages, stapled twice along the spine. The cover is a hand-drawn pencil sketch of the Kwik Stop's exterior at golden hour, with the gas pumps in the foreground and one small figure leaning against the ice freezer outside. The figure is Diego.

The title, hand-lettered: **GAS STATION OBSERVATIONS · #4 · spring**

### contents

**Page 1 — masthead.**

> *gas station observations is a zine i make on my breaks and on slow shifts. i started it because my high school english teacher told me to "find your voice" and the only place i could think of where i could hear myself was here, between the soda fountain and the lottery scratchers. issue #4 — spring. i am not on any social media. if you found this zine, you can leave a note in the brown manila envelope taped to the back of the slushie machine.*
>
> *— sam.*

**Page 2 — "the regulars i can identify by their cars."**

A list, handwritten, of fourteen cars and what their drivers always buy. Highlights:

- *the silver toyota corolla, license plate begins with HJF — buys two diet cokes, one bag of pork rinds, never makes eye contact, pays in cash.*
- *the white ford f-150, no front license plate — buys nothing. comes in. walks the back cooler. leaves. has done this on six separate days. i write down the date each time.*
- *the gray hatchback, license plate ENDED IN 666, full plate now redacted — bought a single beef stick and a half gallon of milk, looked at me too long, never came back.*
- *the maroon buick, plate 5XR-AB7 — graciela ramos, diego's grandma. comes by on her own sometimes when diego is at practice. doesn't say much. once asked me if i was eating. i said i was.*

**Page 3 — "things people leave at the register and don't come back for."**

A drawing of a small wire basket Sam keeps under the register. The basket's contents are listed:

- *one tube of chapstick, watermelon, half-used*
- *a polaroid photograph, blurry, of a child on a swing*
- *a receipt from a different gas station in another town*
- *a hand-drawn pencil sketch of a chair*
- *a single house key on a green plastic keychain shaped like a fish*
- *a folded piece of notebook paper that, when opened, has only the phrase "i forgive you" written in cursive*
- *a small ceramic frog, broken, no body, just the head*

**Page 4 — "the back cooler customer."**

A two-page spread, prose, no illustrations.

> *there is a man who comes in once every nine days, on average. i have not yet identified the pattern beyond nine days. he is in his fifties. he wears golf shirts tucked into khaki shorts. he has a sunburn on the back of his neck in the particular shape of a man who has begun, recently, wearing collared shirts after a lifetime of not. he comes in. he goes to the back cooler. he stands in front of it for a long time — longer than anyone ever stands in front of the back cooler at the kwik stop, because the back cooler holds the beer, and beer is a dollar twenty-nine a can, and that decision does not take long. he is, i have realized over many visits, not looking at the beer. he is looking at the reflection of me in the cooler glass.*
>
> *he buys a single tallboy of something domestic. he hands me a five. he does not make eye contact. he takes his change and his beer and his receipt, and he nods, and he leaves.*
>
> *he drives a white ford sedan with no front license plate. texas requires a front license plate. he has been coming in for at least four months. he does not buy anything else. he never asks about lottery scratchers. he has never used the bathroom. he is not from harmony creek. i have run his plate by a friend who has a friend at the dps. the plate does not match the vehicle.*
>
> *i don't know what to do with this information. i am writing it down here because writing it down is the closest thing to telling someone that does not require me to actually tell someone.*

**Page 5 — "things jen the day manager has said this month."**

A bullet list:

- *"sammy if you don't go on your break i am literally going to write you up."*
- *"this scratcher is not a winner. it has never been a winner. nobody who has bought one has ever won. you can stop suggesting it."*
- *"the stockroom light is buzzing again. corporate will not send anyone for a buzzing light."*
- *"my mother called me at three in the morning. she said the cable channel said her sister was in trouble. her sister has been dead for nine years."*

**Page 6 — "weather report."**

A small block of weather notes for the past two weeks. Written like a poem.

> *monday — fog at 6 am, gone by 8.*
> *tuesday — a bird flew into the front window. it lived. it sat on the gas pump for half an hour. then it left.*
> *wednesday — clear. nothing happened.*
> *thursday — clear. nothing happened.*
> *friday — diego came by at 7. we ate one bag of takis between us.*
> *saturday — i was off.*
> *sunday — clear. an old woman came in i had never seen. she bought one bottle of water. she handed me a folded piece of paper. she walked out. the paper said "the harmony creek wind is coming. be inside by tuesday at four." i was inside by tuesday at four. nothing happened.*

**Page 7 — "a poem."**

Centered on the page, in pencil, untitled:

> *the microwave clock is nine minutes fast.
> jen says it helps the closers.
> i am the closer.
> i have not been helped.
> i have been counting backwards
> in the small subtractions
> jen makes for me.
> by the end of the shift
> i am the only person in the store
> who knows what time it is.*

**Page 8 — back cover.**

A pencil sketch of Diego, mid-laugh, head tilted back, his curly hair against the Kwik Stop's fluorescent. The drawing is good. Sam can draw. Below the drawing, in marker:

> *issue #5 — summer — coming when i feel like it.*
> *— s.*

### gallery unlocks gated by the zine

- **Open the zine** → gallery surfaces all eight pages as full-resolution scans
- **Read page 4 (the back cooler customer)** → gallery surfaces a single Polaroid of the white sedan parked outside the Kwik Stop at golden hour, no driver visible. The Polaroid was in Sam's basket of left-behind objects (page 3); she did not take the photo. She does not know who did.
- **Read page 6's Sunday entry (the old woman)** → gallery surfaces the folded note, photographed: *"the harmony creek wind is coming. be inside by tuesday at four."* In a hand Sam has not, at the time of vol6 ch1, yet identified. We will identify it.

## III. THE CORKBOARD

Above Sam's bed in her room at her father's house in Harmony Creek Estates, lot 28, phase II, a cork bulletin board four feet by three feet. Sam has been pinning to it since she was twelve. It is full.

The board is a single gallery surface — clicking it opens an interactive zoom into individual pinned items. Each item has its own small reveal.

### pinned items (selected)

- **a Polaroid of Sam and Diego at the wedding chapel in Westport, age 17 and 18, both in someone else's wedding clothes.** They are not married. They were guests. They are laughing because the photographer asked them to.
- **a ticket stub from a Gallatin Band show at THE GALLATIN ROCK CLUB, Lake Charles, January 14, $8 cover.** Sam has the stub from this show. Diego does not. Diego was the band's drummer at this show. Sam went alone to see him play. He saw her in the third row. He played the show looking at her.
- **a small drawing on a Kwik Stop receipt of a frog wearing sunglasses.** Drawn by Diego on the back of Sam's first-ever sale receipt at the Kwik Stop (a single Hostess CupCake, $1.49, 11:48 AM, August 14, 2022). Sam saved the receipt. The frog wears the sunglasses in panel one, takes them off in panel two, says **"hello, sammy"** in panel three. Sam has not, on inspection, identified panel four. Panel four is the frog's mouth open in a slight smile, no caption.
- **her high school graduation tassel (gold, white, blue) — Harmony Creek HS, class of last spring.** Sam graduated. She did not walk. Her father did not attend. Jen the day manager attended. Jen took the tassel back to Sam at the next shift.
- **a folded square of paper that, when unfolded, reads in Diego's hand: "if you ever want me to stop, just say."** Sam has never said.
- **a postcard from her mother, dated 2019, postmarked Pensacola.** The postcard's message reads: *"Sammy — I'm okay. I'm not coming back. I love you. Be brave. — Mom."* The handwriting matches the handwriting of the old woman's note in Sam's zine on page 6. Sam has not, at the time of vol6 ch1, yet realized this. The meta-game asks her to.
- **a small printed photograph of the Harmony Creek Estates Phase II model home, plan IV.** Sam printed this herself at the school library when she was a sophomore for a real-estate research assignment. She has never gone inside the model home. She has thought about it. The pin holding the photograph is, on close inspection, the lapel pin from a NexCorp Residential Solutions employee badge. Sam does not know where she got the pin.
- **a sketch on a torn napkin from D'Ambrosio's Diner of a road, a fence, a gate marked HALSEY · PRIVATE.** Sam has never been to the gate. She has never been to D'Ambrosio's Diner. The napkin was in her father's pocket. He left it on the counter one Saturday. She took it.

### gallery unlocks gated by the corkboard

- **Identify the handwriting match between the Pensacola postcard and the old woman's note in the zine** → unlocks the dormant chapter of Sam's mother's continued life. Sam's mother is alive. She is in Harmony Creek. She has been writing notes that arrive at Sam's job without identifying herself. Vol6 reserves the full reunion for ch5 or ch6.
- **Unfold the napkin from the diner and click the gate symbol** → unlocks a small vol7 forward seed: the gate is the same gate the Hermit walks to in S01E09 (THE POMEGRANATE HOUR), the same gate Halle's letter mentions (S01E18). Sam's father has been to the gate. Sam does not yet know why.
- **Click the lapel pin holding the model-home photo** → unlocks a single line of in-game lore: *"Sam's father, J. Miller, worked for NexCorp Residential Solutions as a regional sales rep from 2014 to 2017. He resigned without explanation. He has not, since, accepted any of the residual commission NexCorp has continued to mail him."*

## IV. THE LICENSE PLATE NOTEBOOK

Spiral-bound, college-ruled, 9x6 inches, blue cover. Sam keeps it in the front pocket of her backpack. She started it nineteen months ago. She has filled approximately a third of the pages.

The notebook records license plates Sam has seen at the Kwik Stop and at the Gas & Go across the intersection. Each entry includes:

- the plate
- the make/model/color of the vehicle
- the time
- whether the driver came inside
- what they bought, if anything
- any irregularities (no front plate, wrong front plate, plate not matching state, etc.)

The notebook is not a casual hobby. The notebook is a small private intelligence operation. Sam has not told anyone about it. Diego knew. Jen does not.

### selected entries

> *March 14 · 14:48 · black ford pickup · LA plate FZ-2287 · driver did not enter · parked behind car wash at Gas & Go for 11 minutes · driver visible only as silhouette · met another vehicle (white NexCorp van) for less than three minutes · van driver gave pickup driver a small envelope · van left · pickup left at 14:59 ·*
> *(this is the third time i have seen this pickup. always the same plate. always the same behavior. always between 14:00 and 16:00. always behind the car wash. always envelopes.)*

> *April 2 · 18:14 · silver toyota corolla · TX plate HJF-2901 · in/out 4 min · 2 diet cokes 1 pork rinds · cash · regular ·*

> *April 18 · 17:32 · white ford sedan · NO FRONT PLATE · in/out 9 min · golf shirt · single tallboy · cash · the back cooler customer · same as 3/12 same as 2/26 same as 2/14 same as 1/26 same as 1/12 same as 12/22 ·*
> *(every nine days. now confirmed. nine days. why nine days. who is on a nine day cycle.)*

> *April 28 · 11:52 · diego's black f-150 · TX plate DGR-1188 · driver NOT diego · driver wore a hat · could not see face · shifted into third gear by the corner of ashberry and linden · diego never shifts into third gear on our street ·*
> *(THIS IS WRONG. diego does not drive his truck like this. i was in the driveway. i was sitting on the porch swing because i could not sleep. i saw the truck leave. i did not see who drove it. i wrote the plate down before i remembered i already know the plate. i wrote the plate down anyway. i am going to call him in the morning. it is morning now. he has not answered.)*

### gallery unlocks gated by the notebook

- **Sort the notebook by plate** → meta-game presents a small connection-graph: the black F-150 (Diego's truck driven by someone else), the white sedan (the back-cooler customer), the NexCorp van (the envelope) all appear within a four-month window. The plates match no honest pattern. They match a pattern.
- **Identify the 9-day cycle on the white sedan** → unlocks the dormant chapter of the back-cooler customer's identity. He is — vol6 reserves the name — but the meta-game permits the player to add him to the connection graph as a vector named SUNBURN.
- **Recognize Diego's truck's plate as the entry where Diego himself stopped being the driver** → unlocks the diorama of Diego's last morning at the Kwik Stop, replayed from Sam's POV at the counter, with the small details Sam did not, at the time, register but the notebook has preserved.

## V. DETRITUS — *the junk drawer*

A single bedside drawer in Sam's bedroom. Sam has not cleaned it out since she was fifteen. It is the gallery's miscellaneous category.

Contents, on a careful inventory:

- a small stack of NexCorp-themed pencils, branded with the Residential Solutions logo, that her father brought home from work in 2016 and that Sam has been using ever since (she has nineteen left)
- a half-used roll of Mexican lottery tickets from a class trip to Galveston in eighth grade
- four guitar picks from various venues (one from THE GALLATIN ROCK CLUB, one from the Evergreen Room — she has never been to the Evergreen Room — one unlabeled, one with the Halsey Farms logo)
- a folded paper crane made by Maya Daigle at Cosmic Comics when Sam came in to trade her zine for one of Maya's
- a small ceramic frog approximately the size of a marble (green, unbroken, intact, unlike the broken-frog-head in her zine's left-behind basket)
- a single sealed envelope addressed to Sam in her mother's hand, never opened, postmarked from Pensacola two years after the postcard, *"do not open until you are nineteen — m."*
- a NexCorp Gas & Go customer-loyalty card with no name on it (Sam stole it from Skip Donnelly's till one Saturday afternoon at the gas station; she has not told anyone)
- a small folded note that reads, in Diego's hand: *"sammy — the address i'd want them to look at if anything happens is 1414 ashberry not 892. just so you know. — d. p.s. don't bring this up unless you have to."*
- a single drumstick — the right one — from Diego's primary kit. He left it in her car the night they went to see the Gallatin Band play at the Houston club in October. Sam never gave it back. He never asked for it. They both knew it had become hers.

### gallery unlocks gated by detritus

- **Open the sealed envelope from her mother** (gated on Sam's nineteenth birthday in the vol6 calendar, an in-game date the player can advance to via Sunday-night progression) → unlocks the chapter of her mother's continued life, her mother's local presence, her mother's reason for leaving, the postal route through which her mother has been keeping watch.
- **Hold the drumstick at the corkboard view** (rotate to align with the ticket stub from January 14) → unlocks the dormant memory diorama of that show: Diego playing, Sam in the third row, the small lift between them across the crowd. The diorama plays as a single 2:40 long take with no dialogue, just the band's *"Sammy"* (track 7 of side A on the mixtape) at full volume on the venue's sound system, and Diego, behind his kit, looking at her between snare hits.
- **Recognize the NexCorp customer-loyalty card she took from Skip's till** → unlocks the dormant connection between Skip Donnelly's accumulation file and Sam's. The card is not a card. The card has, on its back, in microprint, an internal NexCorp employee ID number that — when cross-referenced with the personnel-graph in the meta-game's L3 layer — identifies Skip as part of the NexCorp Residential Solutions operations cell that has been running through the Gas & Go for the past nine years.
- **Eat the Mexican lottery ticket from Galveston eighth grade** *(joke, do not actually eat the ticket)* — but the lottery ticket, examined under the gallery's high-resolution view, is in fact a tiny zine, folded eight times: a single thumbnail-sized poem Sam wrote at fourteen on the bus home from Galveston, never shared, never re-found until now. The poem reads, in microprint: *"the sea was salt. the sea was always salt. nobody told me. i tasted it. i kept tasting it for two weeks afterward. that, my teacher said, is the chapter. — s."*
