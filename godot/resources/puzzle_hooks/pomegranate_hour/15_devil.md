## ACT I — THE WELCOME TABLE  (00:24 — 06:30)

INT. HARMONY CREEK ESTATES CLUBHOUSE — MULTIPURPOSE ROOM — EVENING.

A community-room interior. Off-white walls. Acoustic-tile ceiling. A field of twenty-eight folding chairs in four rows of seven, set up facing a long folding table at the front. Behind the table: a corkboard with a sun-bleached map of HARMONY CREEK ESTATES, the lots numbered, certain lots highlighted in faded yellow.

Against the wall by the entrance: **THE WELCOME TABLE.**

The welcome table is six feet long. A red gingham tablecloth. Three short rows of small mason jars. Each jar is filled with a honey-colored substance. The jars bear identical labels in honey-colored type: **HALSEY FARMS · WILDFLOWER · ONE EACH PLEASE.**

There are exactly twenty-four jars on the welcome table at the beginning of the episode.

A handwritten sign on a small folded card beside the jars reads:
*"Please take ONE jar per household — courtesy of Halsey Farms, our valued community partner. Thank you for serving Harmony Creek."*

Across the room, against the opposite wall: a folding table marked **REFRESHMENTS.** A coffee urn. A box of donuts. A bowl of mints. The mints are also honey-colored.

A small handwritten paper taped to the corkboard map of HARMONY CREEK reads:
*"AGENDA — Mar 14 — 1. Welcome. 2. Approval of minutes. 3. Officer reports. 4. NEW BUSINESS: Noise complaint, Lot 47. 5. Adjourn."*

Residents arrive in twos and threes. They wear casual evening clothes. They smile at one another in the way of people who recognize one another but are not friends. They sign a clipboard at the welcome table. Each signs. Each takes one jar.

The camera is a wide tripod shot from the back of the room, gently zooming over the course of two minutes. We see residents arrive, sign in, take a jar.

The jars are taken at a steady rate.

At 03:14 — the JACOB, WAGNER, and FAUST trio enters.

They are in their mid-twenties. The trio walks like they have been together a long time. JACOB enters first — tall, button-down shirt untucked, baseball cap held in his hand against his thigh. WAGNER follows — glasses, jacket too warm for the room, looking around with the small embarrassed delight of someone who has wandered into a room that is funnier than the room knows. FAUST enters last — the most put-together of the three, a wry smile already at the corners of his mouth.

They sign the clipboard. WAGNER signs his real name. JACOB signs *"J. Salvador."* FAUST signs *"Faust."*

The welcome table greeter — MRS. PIMENTEL, sixties, blue blazer — smiles at them.

MRS. PIMENTEL
You boys with the realty group?

JACOB
*(smooth)*
Just visiting. We heard about the meeting. Friend of a friend.

MRS. PIMENTEL
Well, you're welcome. Please take a jar. Just one per household.

JACOB
*(picking one up, examining the label)*
Halsey Farms. Local?

MRS. PIMENTEL
Very local. They've been with the community since the founding.

WAGNER picks up a jar. He holds it up to the fluorescent light. He sees, through the glass, that the honey has a faint cloudiness at the bottom — a small undissolved crystallization in the shape of a comma.

WAGNER
*(low, to JACOB)*
Look at this.

JACOB
*(looking)*
Mm.

WAGNER
That's like — that's like a thing growing.

JACOB
That's just honey doing what honey does.

WAGNER
*(unconvinced)*
Sure.

FAUST steps up to the welcome table. He looks at the jars. He picks one up. He sets it down. He picks up a different one. He examines the label.

FAUST
*(to MRS. PIMENTEL)*
May I take two.

MRS. PIMENTEL
*(small surprise)*
One per household, dear.

FAUST
*(smiling)*
I represent two households.

MRS. PIMENTEL hesitates. She looks at the sign-in clipboard. FAUST has signed exactly once.

FAUST
*(easy)*
I'll sign again.

He takes the pen. He signs the clipboard a second time. The second signature reads *"Faust (the other one)."*

MRS. PIMENTEL
*(small dry laugh, defeated)*
All right. Two it is.

FAUST takes two jars. He hands one to WAGNER, who is already holding one. WAGNER now has two. He looks slightly alarmed.

FAUST keeps the other for himself. He pockets it inside his coat.

The trio moves toward the back row of folding chairs and sits — WAGNER, JACOB, FAUST in that order — as residents continue to file in.

The shot holds on the trio briefly. WAGNER is examining one of his two jars with renewed concern. JACOB is looking at the room. FAUST is looking, calmly, at the camera. He sees it. He nods. He turns to face the front.

The cold open hum at 9:09 has not happened yet in this episode. The clock on the wall above the corkboard reads 7:47.

## ACT II — APPROVAL OF MINUTES  (06:30 — 12:00)

The BOARD enters from a side door and takes seats at the front table.

The BOARD: five people. From left:
- **MRS. CALLOWAY**, treasurer, seventies, hair in a tight white bun
- **DR. PRYCE**, vice-president, fifties, a gentle face, a brass mechanical pencil he clicks open and shut throughout the meeting
- **MR. NEHL**, president, sixties, a tie, a smile that does not quite reach the eyes
- **MS. KOO**, secretary, forties, a tablet stylus poised over an iPad
- **MR. SAGE**, sergeant-at-arms (an unusual office for an HOA), thirties, a maintenance polo shirt with the NexCorp logo

MR. NEHL
*(calling the meeting)*
We'll come to order. Thank you for being here on a Tuesday night, neighbors. We'll begin with the approval of minutes from the February meeting. Ms. Koo.

MS. KOO
*(reading from her iPad)*
The minutes of the February meeting are as follows. Item one: welcome, no business. Item two: approval of the January minutes, approved unanimously. Item three: officer reports as filed. Item four: new business, discussion of the proposed irrigation easement, tabled. Item five: adjournment at eight-eleven. Signed Koo, secretary.

MR. NEHL
Motion to approve the minutes.

DR. PRYCE
*(without looking up)*
Motion.

MRS. CALLOWAY
*(without looking up)*
Second.

MR. NEHL
All in favor.

ALL FIVE BOARD MEMBERS
Aye.

The audience does not vote. The audience is not asked.

MR. NEHL
Minutes are approved. Officer reports. Treasurer.

MRS. CALLOWAY reads from a paper. The treasurer's report is twenty-eight seconds of dues collected, dues outstanding, and a brief item about replacement bulbs in the gazebo. None of it is interesting.

MR. NEHL
Thank you, Mrs. Calloway. Vice-president.

DR. PRYCE
*(clicking the pencil)*
Nothing of note this month.

MR. NEHL
Secretary.

MS. KOO
Correspondence. Two letters from residents. One regarding the height of fences on lots 32 through 38. One regarding a cat. The fence letter is being handled by the architectural review committee. The cat letter has been resolved by Mr. Sage.

MR. SAGE
*(small smile)*
It was a nice cat.

A small chuckle in the room. The trio at the back exchanges a look.

JACOB
*(low, to WAGNER)*
What's a sergeant-at-arms for, in an HOA.

WAGNER
*(low, distracted)*
For cats. Apparently.

FAUST
*(low, watching MR. SAGE)*
He works for NexCorp. The polo.

WAGNER
Same difference.

FAUST
Mm.

MR. NEHL
Sergeant-at-arms.

MR. SAGE
Nothing of note this month.

MR. NEHL
Thank you. New business.

He pauses.

MR. NEHL
We have one item on the new-business agenda this evening, neighbors. It is a noise complaint from Lot 47. Lot 47 is —

MRS. CALLOWAY
*(soft, helpful)*
The Vegas.

MR. NEHL
Thank you. The Vegas. The complaint was filed in writing. Ms. Koo, would you read it.

MS. KOO
*(reading)*
"To the board: for approximately six weeks I have heard a sound that I cannot identify, coming from outside my home at night between the hours of approximately ten p.m. and three a.m. The sound is not loud. The sound is not constant. The sound is consistent in tone and continuous in fact. I have not been able to determine the source. I have asked my neighbors; none of them claim to hear it. I am submitting this letter to the board because I believe the board may have access to information I do not. Respectfully, Carla Vega, Lot 47."

A pause.

MR. NEHL
Thank you, Ms. Koo.

He looks at the board. The board looks at each other. None of them speaks.

MR. NEHL
*(to the room)*
Has anyone else here heard the sound described.

A long silence.

No hands go up.

In the back row, WAGNER raises his hand halfway. JACOB grabs his wrist and pulls it down.

WAGNER
*(low, to JACOB)*
We're not going to —

JACOB
*(low)*
We don't live here.

WAGNER
But I've —

FAUST
*(low, calm)*
Wagner. Not in this room.

WAGNER drops his hand.

The board has not noticed.

MR. NEHL
Hearing no responses, I would like to propose the following motion. Dr. Pryce, would you read it.

DR. PRYCE
*(reading from a paper that was already prepared)*
The board moves to acknowledge receipt of the noise complaint from Lot 47, to find that no corroborating reports have been received from neighboring lots, and to direct the sergeant-at-arms to coordinate with NexCorp Residential Solutions to investigate and resolve. The board further moves that, pending such investigation, the matter be considered silenced.

A long pause.

MR. NEHL
Discussion.

A long silence.

Then —

FAUST
*(from the back, calm and clear)*
Mr. President.

The board turns to look. FAUST stands. He is the only person in the audience to have spoken.

MR. NEHL
*(carefully)*
Sir.

FAUST
What does "silenced" mean, in the motion. Procedurally.

MR. NEHL
*(after a beat)*
It means that the matter is not to be reopened at subsequent meetings unless new information arises.

FAUST
And the resident — Mrs. Vega — will be informed of the resolution.

MR. NEHL
She will be informed that the matter has been resolved.

FAUST
And what will she be told about the sound.

A pause.

MR. NEHL
The sound, sir, has been determined to not be a violation of the community's noise ordinance.

FAUST
That's not what I asked.

MR. NEHL
*(measured)*
I'm not sure I understand the question.

FAUST
What will she be told about the sound itself. Whether it exists. Whether anyone else can hear it. Whether the investigation found a source.

MR. NEHL
She will be informed that the matter has been resolved.

A pause.

FAUST
*(small nod)*
Thank you. I have no further questions.

He sits.

The board members exchange a brief look. MR. SAGE writes something on a clipboard.

MR. NEHL
Any further discussion.

Silence.

MR. NEHL
All in favor of the motion, signify by saying aye.

THE FIVE BOARD MEMBERS
Aye.

MR. NEHL
Opposed.

Silence.

MR. NEHL
The motion carries unanimously. The matter is silenced.

MS. KOO types into her iPad. Her tablet stylus clicks against the screen at the word *silenced.*

The trio at the back is very still. WAGNER is looking at the jar of honey in his hands.

The clock on the wall reads 8:24.

## ACT III — THE HALSEY LIAISON  (12:00 — 18:00)

MR. NEHL
*(looking at his paper)*
The agenda has one further item that has been added since the agenda was posted. We'll take it now if there's no objection.

A pause. No objection.

MR. NEHL
We have a visitor. Mr. Halsey is here to address the community briefly about a partnership matter. Mr. Halsey.

A man rises from the second row. He has been there the entire meeting; we have not seen his face until now. He is fifty, gray at the temples, in a clean linen jacket. He carries no papers. He walks to the front. He turns to face the room.

HALSEY
*(easy, warm)*
Good evening, friends. I'll just take a moment of your time. My family has been in this part of the county for four generations. Halsey Farms produces wildflower honey and pasture goods within driving distance of where everyone in this room sleeps at night. My grandfather sold to your grandfathers. My father sold to your fathers. I'm proud to continue the tradition with the families of Harmony Creek Estates.

He pauses. He smiles.

HALSEY
The honey on the welcome table tonight is a small token. We are also pleased to announce, in coordination with the board, an expanded partnership: starting this spring, every household in Harmony Creek Estates will be enrolled in our Community Subscription program. A jar per month, delivered to your porch, complimentary, for the next year. After the first year, the subscription will continue at a member's rate. Of course any household may opt out at any time.

Polite applause.

HALSEY
*(gentle wave)*
Thank you. The board has approved the program in consultation with NexCorp Residential Solutions. It's a small gift from our family to yours. Take a jar tonight as a first installment. Take two if you have a guest in your home.

He looks, when he says "take two," directly at FAUST.

FAUST holds his gaze. FAUST does not smile. FAUST does not look away.

HALSEY
*(returning to the room)*
Thank you, neighbors. Enjoy your evenings.

He steps down. He returns to his seat. He sits.

MR. NEHL
Thank you, Mr. Halsey. Any other business.

Silence.

MR. NEHL
Motion to adjourn.

DR. PRYCE
Motion.

MRS. CALLOWAY
Second.

ALL FIVE BOARD MEMBERS
Aye.

MR. NEHL
We're adjourned. Thank you, neighbors.

The clock reads 8:39.

## ACT IV — THE TABLE AFTER  (18:00 — 24:30)

The room begins to disperse. Residents stand, fold chairs, chat in small clusters. Most of them stop at the welcome table on the way out.

The camera holds on the welcome table.

The number of jars on the table at the beginning of the meeting was twenty-four. As residents file past, taking one each, the camera's count visibly decreases. At 19:48, FAUST returns to the table.

There are six jars remaining.

FAUST picks up a third jar. He does not put it under his coat. He carries it openly.

MRS. PIMENTEL
*(small)*
Mr. — Faust. I'm afraid that's the limit.

FAUST
*(easy)*
I'm taking it for the road.

MRS. PIMENTEL
Sir, the board has been very clear —

FAUST
The board has been very clear, ma'am, that one jar is the limit per household. I am taking three jars total for three households. I have signed three times.

He gestures at the clipboard.

The clipboard now reads, in three different signature styles:
- *Faust*
- *Faust (the other one)*
- *Faust (the one after that)*

MRS. PIMENTEL stares at the clipboard. She looks at FAUST. She looks at the jar in his hand.

MRS. PIMENTEL
*(carefully)*
Mr. Halsey is still in the room.

FAUST
I know.

FAUST does not look toward HALSEY. He does not need to. He places the jar in his other coat pocket. He nods politely at MRS. PIMENTEL. He turns and walks back to JACOB and WAGNER, who are waiting by the door.

WAGNER has two jars. JACOB has none. FAUST now has three.

JACOB
*(low, as they exit)*
Faust.

FAUST
*(low)*
Yes.

JACOB
What are you going to do with three jars of honey.

FAUST
*(small smile)*
Hold them.

WAGNER
Are we — are we doing something here. I feel like we're doing something.

FAUST
We are taking what they are offering. They are offering more than they should.

WAGNER
But they only let you take —

FAUST
They let me take three because I asked three times. The rule was always negotiable. Most rules are.

JACOB
*(looking back at HALSEY through the closing door)*
He saw you.

FAUST
He saw me. He chose to permit me. The permission is the record.

They exit.

The camera lingers on the welcome table.

There are now three jars remaining.

A small woman in a HARMONY CREEK ESTATES windbreaker — we have not seen her face this episode; she has been seated in the third row throughout — approaches the table. She does not take a jar. She produces a small black object from her pocket. She places it gently behind the three remaining jars, where the camera will not see it from the back-of-room angle.

She nods at MRS. PIMENTEL. MRS. PIMENTEL nods back.

The small woman exits.

The clock reads 8:51.

## ACT V — THE LISTENING DEVICE  (24:30 — 29:18)

The room empties.

MRS. PIMENTEL clears the clipboard, folds the gingham tablecloth, and stacks the three remaining jars in a small cardboard box. She does not see the small black object behind the jars. She picks up the box. The black object falls forward into the box with the jars.

She does not notice.

She carries the box toward the side exit. She passes the BOARD, who are conferring quietly at the front. She nods at them. They nod back.

MR. SAGE
*(low, to MRS. PIMENTEL)*
Three left, Mary?

MRS. PIMENTEL
*(low)*
The young one took three.

MR. SAGE
*(after a beat)*
Hm.

He glances at MR. NEHL. MR. NEHL has heard. MR. NEHL does not respond.

MRS. PIMENTEL exits.

The camera holds on the multipurpose room.

The board members rise and gather their papers. Mr. NEHL exits last. He pauses in the doorway. He turns. He looks at the corkboard with the lot map.

He walks back to the corkboard. He removes a small thumbtack. He removes the agenda paper. He folds it in quarters. He places it in his jacket pocket.

He looks at the highlighted yellow lots.

Lot 47 — the lot of the noise complaint — is highlighted in yellow.

He looks at the lots adjacent to Lot 47.

Lots 45, 46, 48, and 49 are also highlighted.

He looks at the lots adjacent to those.

Lots 43, 44, 50, 51 are also highlighted.

The highlighted yellow forms a continuous corridor running east-west across the southern half of HARMONY CREEK ESTATES. The corridor passes through approximately eighteen lots. It exits the eastern fence at the property line of HALSEY's wildflower meadow.

MR. NEHL stares at the map for nine seconds.

Then he removes a small marker from his jacket pocket. He un-caps it. He carefully fills in the yellow on Lot 47, making it a more saturated yellow, identical to the other highlighted lots.

He re-caps the marker. He pockets it. He exits.

The room is empty.

The camera holds on the corkboard.

The 9:09 cable interference hum, which has been quiet for the entire episode, rises slowly. It builds for fourteen seconds.

The hum peaks.

The hum subsides.

The clock reads 9:09.

## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- I obtained credentials from the *Gallatin Sentinel-Voice* through a former colleague who did not know the paper had closed. she gave them to me because she trusted me. she does not know I used them at this meeting. she would understand if I told her. I have not told her.
- the board was filmed with consent. the residents were filmed with consent through the welcome-table sign-in disclosure (small print at the bottom of the clipboard). the BOYS — JACOB, WAGNER, FAUST — were not aware of the filming until FAUST identified the camera at 04:48. he chose to continue. he chose to ask his question. he chose to take the third jar. he chose to nod, on the way out, in my direction. the permission, again, is the record.
- the boys are real. they are not in vol5. they belong to vol1. they are here because they are here. they wandered in. I do not know how they found the meeting. they were not on any list I had access to. FAUST said, when I introduced myself afterward in the parking lot, that they had been invited "by a friend of a friend in Beaumont." I did not ask which friend.
- HALSEY is real. his name is real. his honey is real. the SUBSCRIPTION PROGRAM is real and will be operational by the spring after this episode airs. the saturation of the yellow on the lot map is the chapter. the map is not a complaint. the map is a delivery route.
- the small black object behind the jars at 24:14 was a listening device. it was placed by the woman in the windbreaker who, in vol6, is canonically Sam Miller's grandmother. I do not know who paid her to place it. I do not know what was being listened to. the device traveled, in MRS. PIMENTEL's box, to the storage closet off the multipurpose room, where it has remained (so far as my follow-up survey has shown) for the seven months between filming and airing.
- WAGNER raised his hand because WAGNER has heard the sound. WAGNER has heard it because WAGNER spends two weeks a year at his uncle's place on Lot 18 of Phase II. JACOB knew this; FAUST guessed it. JACOB pulled WAGNER's hand down because identifying himself as a sometime resident would have placed all three of them on a list. FAUST asked the procedural question in WAGNER's stead.
- the unanimous vote was, in fact, unanimous. the board has not, in eleven years of recorded meetings, recorded a single non-unanimous vote. I have read the minutes back. RES_SOL_VERIFY in the comments has, helpfully, confirmed this.
- the Halsey/Triumvirate eye contact at 17:32 is the episode's single point of recognition between the deck's two volumes. FAUST does not yet know that he is in a vol1 he has not finished. HALSEY does not yet know that he is in a vol7 he has not started. they both know that they have seen each other.
- the chapter title — THE DEVIL — is, in the deck's logic, the chapter of contract. the HOA meeting is a contract being signed by the room as a body without ever being articulated. the honey is the consideration. the silenced matter is the term. the residents, in taking the jars, are consenting to the contract. WAGNER's confusion is the chapter's residual conscience. FAUST's third jar is the deck's most public refusal to be tidily consenting. the rule was always negotiable.
