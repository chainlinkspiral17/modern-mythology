### CALL 04 — 00:38 CT

INT. A KWIK STOP — STAFF AREA — NIGHT.

The Kwik Stop's small staff area behind the register. The shift manager — a woman, forty, hair tied back, wearing a corporate polo — at a small steel desk doing paperwork. A landline phone on the desk.

Through the doorway behind her, the front of the Kwik Stop is visible. The fluorescent lights buzz. A customer wanders the cooler aisle.

The phone rings.

SHIFT MANAGER
*(brisk)*
Kwik Stop, this is Renée.

She listens.

RENÉE
*(small annoyance)*
I'm sorry, who is this calling for. There's no one here by that name. You — you have the wrong number, sir. This is the back line at the —

She listens.

RENÉE
*(face going still)*
Excuse me.

She listens for fourteen seconds.

RENÉE
*(very quiet)*
Yes.

She hangs up. She does not move.

Behind her, the customer in the cooler aisle has not moved. He is standing in front of the back cooler, looking through the glass. He is not selecting a beer. He is looking at her reflection in the cooler glass.

RENÉE does not see him.

She picks up the landline phone again. She dials a four-digit extension. She speaks into the receiver, low.

RENÉE
Skip. Renée. I'm at the back line. I need you to come over here.

She listens.

RENÉE
*(steadier)*
No. Now.

She hangs up.

She sits at the desk with her hands flat on the paperwork. The customer in the cooler aisle continues to look at her reflection. He does not turn. He does not pick anything up. He is wearing a golf shirt tucked into khaki shorts. He has a sunburn on the back of his neck.

### CALL 11 — 04:48 CT

INT. AN UNKNOWN ROOM — NIGHT.

The frame is dark for a long beat. Then a single hanging bare bulb clicks on, illuminating a small windowless room. Concrete walls. A single small wooden table. A single wooden chair. A landline phone on the table.

There is nobody in the room.

The phone rings.

It rings nine times.

On the tenth ring, the receiver — by itself, the camera does not show a hand — lifts off the cradle and floats, briefly, above the table. We hold on this image for two seconds.

*(small, calm, neither boy nor girl, no apparent fear)*

## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- the eleven calls are real. I placed them. I did not record my voice; I removed myself from the audio in post. the audio retains the called party's responses and the called party's room. the called party's room was filmed under landlord agreements that did not specifically grant filming rights but did grant general property-access rights, which I have, with some squeamishness, treated as sufficient.
- the question I asked was the same question, eleven times. the question is not in the episode because the question makes the calls about me. they are not about me. they are about whether the called parties said yes.
- the called parties did not coordinate. they had no relationship to one another that I am aware of. I selected eleven numbers from a list compiled by a method I will not describe in detail; the method is forensic, the numbers were available to be found. I assert without elaboration that the eleven numbers are linked.
- the customer at the Kwik Stop in CALL 04 is the customer from THE FOOL (S01E00). same sunburn, same shorts, same sedan. RES_SOL_VERIFY in the comments has connected the two appearances. I have not redacted him. he is paying attention.
- the photograph of the HALSEY gate in the law office in CALL 06 is real. CALL 06's office is the office of an attorney in the Harmony Creek Estates strip mall who in vol5 becomes Erica Campbell. her predecessor at the firm — the actual attorney filmed here — left the firm five weeks after this episode aired. the manila folder is no longer in the filing cabinet. Erica, when she inherits the practice, will find the empty cabinet. the cabinet's lock will still work. she will not know why the cabinet is empty.
- CALL 07 is Halsey. I confirmed this. I am sitting on what I confirmed for now.
- CALL 08 is Natalie's apartment. her face is not in the frame; the shot is composed from her shoulders down. Natalie did not know I called her. when I crossed paths with her at a tarot shop in Glen Park three months after the episode aired, she said: "you got me with the cards. you got the layout exactly right." I said: *that wasn't me. that was you doing what you do.* she said: "but you knew I'd be doing it." I had no answer for her. she smiled. she walked away.
- the shortwave carrier on the transistor in CALL 08 is the pirate-radio carrier WUFR (no FCC registration, no station ID, broadcasting on shortwave from somewhere in the marsh north of Lake Charles, intermittently, since at least 1979). it broadcasts a single repeating tone. the tone is the carrier hum of an unmanned transmitter operating on an unauthorized frequency. people in southern Louisiana have been listening to it for decades. people have written letters to the FCC about it. the FCC has, on three separate occasions, sent inspectors to triangulate it. no inspector has located the transmitter. natalie listens to it because the tone matches the cable interference at 9:09. it is, I believe, the same carrier.
- CALL 09 is the retired priest in Lake Charles who substituted for my father (V — THE HIEROPHANT, the FATHER COSGRAVE substitute). he was answering this call in his own home, in his own voice, without his collar. his "yes" is his actual yes. he did not, after the call, contact me. I did not contact him. we both know.
- CALL 10 is a working father who I will not identify. he is the only called party whose yes is followed by an action that he carries out the next morning. the action is small. the action is irreversible. the action is good. I will not describe it. the chapter respects this.
- CALL 11. I do not know whose room that is. the call was placed to a phone number that, according to the phone company, has not been assigned in fourteen years. I did not arrange a camera in that room. there was no production crew in that room. there was no boom operator in that room. the footage is in my archive. I have not been able to determine, on multiple expert reviews, how it was acquired. the footage is consistent with the rest of the night's filming in its resolution, its color science, its hum signature, and its 9:09 cable-interference timestamp. there was a 9:09 interference event during CALL 11. the interference is visible in the bulb's flicker. the child's laugh did not register on the meter.
- the question I asked was the same question. the question was not the chapter. the chapter was the yes. that is the entire conceit. the wheel turns and the people say yes because they have been waiting to be asked. fortune is the consent given before consciousness catches up. that is the deck's reading. it is also mine.
