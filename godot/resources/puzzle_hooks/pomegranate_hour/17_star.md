## ACT — THE OFFERING  (00:24 — 16:36)

EXT. GRAUSTARK RIVERBANK — NIGHT — LONG LENS.

The frame is wide. A river runs left-to-right across the lower third. The opposite bank is dark, a low rise of grass and silt with a single flat stone at the water's edge, large enough for a person to stand on. Above the rise: open sky. The sky is filled with stars. The exposure has been adjusted to make the stars legible. There are eight bright stars visible in the upper portion of the frame. The brightest is dead-center overhead.

No moon.

The shot is held, motionless, for forty seconds before anything moves.

Then —

At the upper-left of the frame, in the distance, on the opposite bank's road, a small light appears. The light is a flashlight, hand-held, pointed at the ground in front of its bearer. The light moves slowly toward the river.

It takes the light eighty seconds of screen time to arrive at the flat stone at the water's edge.

THE WOMAN — *uncredited; signed* **—** *in the credits* — is forty-five. She wears a long brown coat, knee-length, and a wool cap pulled low. Her face is in shadow under the brim throughout the episode. She carries a flashlight in her left hand and two clay vessels under her right arm, balanced against her hip.

She sets the flashlight down on the stone, pointing at the ground beside her so that the light scatters and does not focus.

She sets the two vessels down beside the flashlight.

She straightens.

She looks at the sky.

She holds the look for nine seconds.

The camera does not move. The microphone catches the wind across the water and, faintly, the 60 Hz hum that is not in this place but is in this recording.

She reaches into the coat. She removes a folded piece of paper. The paper is a single sheet, folded in quarters. She unfolds it. She holds it down at the level of her thighs and tilts it to catch the flashlight's scatter.

She reads it. We do not see the text. The camera does not zoom.

She reads for eighteen seconds.

She folds the paper back along its original creases. She places it on the stone, weighted by the flashlight.

She lifts the first vessel.

The vessel is dark clay, hand-fired, about eight inches tall and six inches across at the rim. Inside the rim — though we cannot see the inscription at this distance — is the mark *+IV.*

She holds the vessel at the level of her sternum for three seconds.

She tilts it.

The water inside the vessel begins to pour. It catches the flashlight's scatter as it falls, briefly glittering, and meets the river.

The vessel empties slowly. The pour takes eleven seconds.

On the river's surface, where the water enters: eight concentric rings spread outward. They do not disperse immediately. They hold their shape for fourteen seconds and then dissolve into the current.

The camera does not zoom on the rings. The camera is fixed.

The eight rings on the river are an exact match for the eight visible stars overhead. The episode does not announce this. The shot is just held.

THE WOMAN sets the first vessel back on the stone.

She lifts the second.

The second vessel is identical in shape and material. Inside its rim — though we cannot see — is the inscription *—*.

She holds the vessel at her sternum for three seconds.

She does not look at the river when she pours.

She looks across the river at the opposite bank.

She does not look toward the camera. The camera is not on the opposite bank from where she looks; the camera is upstream of the bank she looks toward. She is looking at something else.

(Note: in production, what she was looking at was the silhouette of a render farm in the distance behind the camera's setup. In the painted scene at vol5 XVII, this becomes the still-smoking aftermath of the Tower. In this episode, the render farm is intact. The smoke does not exist yet. She is looking at a structure that will collapse in a different year.)

She tilts the second vessel.

The pour takes nine seconds.

On the river's surface: a single long ring forms where the water enters, elongates as it moves downstream, and does not dissolve. It travels with the current out of frame.

THE WOMAN sets the second vessel back on the stone.

She picks up the folded paper. She unfolds it on the stone, weighted with her hand. She picks up a small pencil-stub from her coat pocket. With her thumbnail braced against the paper, she makes a single short mark at the bottom of the page.

She drops the pencil-stub back into her pocket.

She folds the paper back. She places it inside her coat.

She lifts both vessels. She tucks them under her right arm against her hip.

She lifts the flashlight.

She turns.

She walks back along the road by which she came. The light moves slowly, growing smaller in the upper-left of the frame.

The camera holds.

It takes the flashlight one hundred fifteen seconds to disappear from the road's vanishing point.

The camera continues to hold.

The river continues.

The eight stars continue.

The sky has not moved appreciably.

We hold for forty more seconds in total silence — the 60 Hz hum is no longer audible — except for the river and the wind.

At 16:14, the camera ends the recording without a fade-out. The frame simply stops.

## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- I filmed the first offering from a blind I built on the opposite bank three days earlier. the blind is still there. I have not moved it. I have used it four times.
- I do not have the subject's consent. I do not have any kind of release. I would not have been able to obtain one without breaking the chapter. she does not perform the offering with witnesses. she does not perform the offering with friends. she does not perform the offering at the same time on the same date each year — only when she can — and so it cannot be reliably anticipated by a friend who might wish to come. my anticipation was a small forensic act and an act of intrusion. I would not undertake it lightly again. I undertook it anyway.
- the inscriptions inside the vessel rims are *+IV* and *—*. I have not filmed them on any of the four offerings. I have inferred them from the deck's other appearances — the dash she signs her name with at STRENGTH (VIII), and the four-count of an apparent recovered-persons-log she filed with D'AMBROSIO INDUSTRIAL once and stopped filing. the +IV count is corroborated by an old news clipping I keep in the archive but have not published.
- the elongated ring on the second pour is not a CGI effect. it is the river. the river receives the second pour differently. I have filmed it four times. the river receives it the same way each time. I cannot explain this; I have not researched it; I am not sure I want to.
- the still-smoking render farm she looks at, in this year, is intact. she is looking at a structure that has not yet collapsed. by year three, the structure has collapsed; she is looking at the smoking ruin. by year four, she is looking at the empty patch of ground. the gaze does not change. the gaze is fixed at the same horizon angle each year. she is looking, I have come to believe, at a place that she has been preparing herself, annually, to look at. the offering and the gaze are paired.
- airing this episode without the subject's consent has been the hardest editorial decision I have made on this series. I aired it because the offering was already public. the riverbank is open access. anyone could have come upon her there. the offering is not, in any strict sense, a private act. but it is performed by a person who has chosen anonymity in every other regard, and to release a document of her annual discipline without her permission is to take from her what she has not been willing to give. I made the decision to air at year one because I had not yet filmed years two, three, or four; I could still tell myself that the documentation might be acceptable. by year three, I knew it was not. years two, three, and four sit unreleased on the partition. they will sit there until she tells me what to do with them.
- I have, twice in the years since year one, considered crossing the river and identifying myself. I have not. I keep deciding that crossing the river would intrude on a discipline whose entire syntax is being alone on the bank. she has chosen to be alone there. the camera, on the opposite bank, behind glass in a blind, is not company. the camera is the documentation she did not ask for and may not want.
- the figure has, since year one, become — in vol5 — VIII (STRENGTH), IX (THE HERMIT), and XVII (THE STAR). she is the same woman. her hand has been on the lion's jaw and on the second vessel's rim. her chapter at IX is the chapter of unattributed lantern-light on the road she walks home along after the offering. her chapter at XVII is the chapter of the offering itself. I have watched the painted versions of these three chapters with the same discomfort I feel airing this episode. the deck respects her anonymity in its credits and refuses to render her face. I have tried to extend the same respect, in the framing of this episode, by keeping her face under the brim of her cap and her body at a distance the lens will not resolve. it is not enough. it is the minimum I can do without losing the chapter.
- the comment under this episode signed **—** appeared four months after the episode aired. I do not know whether the comment is from her. I have decided to act as if it is. the comment reads: *"i was the driver."* this is, on the surface, a reference to the comment thread under episode VII (THE CHARIOT). it is also, possibly, a more general statement — the un-named figure is the driver of her own offering, of her own withholding, of her own annual return to the river. if it is her, the comment is the only signal I have ever received that she knows about the episode. I have not replied. I have not contacted her. I have not crossed the river.
- the chapter at XVII closes the deck's spine VIII ↔ IX ↔ XVII for me. three chapters of one woman who has chosen to do her work without being named. I am, on this series, the inverse spine: the host who does not speak. the chapter's closing card — *the subject does not know* — is the deck's most honest piece of typography. it is also a confession. it is also a vow.
