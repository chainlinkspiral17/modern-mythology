## ACT — THE WREATH  (00:24 — 28:42)

EXT. HARMONY CREEK ESTATES — PLAN IV MODEL HOME — FRONT DOOR — LATE AFTERNOON.

A wide framing. The front door of the Plan IV (Victorian) model home — the same model home as S01E03 (THE EMPRESS). The door is a tall heavy wood, painted dark forest green, with a small leaded-glass inset at chest height. The door's frame bears, on the trim, the HOA's compliance plate. A small brass kick-plate at the bottom.

On the door, at chest height: **A WREATH.**

The wreath is approximately eighteen inches in diameter. It is a wreath of mixed wildflowers, fresh, the colors deep and saturated in the late-afternoon light. The wreath has not been balanced for symmetry. The wreath is the kind of wreath made by a careful hand which is not professionally trained but is, in its own register, extremely careful.

The wreath contains, in close inspection:

- Halsey wildflower meadow Plot IV — primarily a deep small purple-pink flower the deck does not name, plus a yellow daisy-like flower, plus small sprigs of green
- Vietnamese chrysanthemums — white, full, classic, set in pairs around the wreath's circumference
- *lis des marais* — swamp lily — long pale petals, a sparse white that lifts in the wreath's overall texture
- a single bay leaf at the wreath's twelve-o'clock position, in a pose the eye reads as deliberate
- a single dried pomegranate seed at the wreath's six-o'clock position, set into the binding
- a small ceramic figurine of a hand, no larger than a thumbnail, set at the wreath's three-o'clock position
- a small woven palm basket-boat — *thuyền nan* — the size of a child's hand, set at the wreath's nine-o'clock position
- a small ceramic dish, the size of a coin, holding three more pomegranate seeds, set into the binding at the wreath's center

The wreath does not, at first glance, have a gap.

The camera begins, very slowly, to zoom.

The zoom is so slow that for the first ninety seconds we cannot tell that it is happening.

The 60 Hz hum continues. The wind through the cypress, somewhere in the distance, continues. A single car passes on the street behind the camera but is not visible. A child's voice calls a name, very far off — the name is **Faith.** The voice is followed by a small distant bark.

By minute four, the zoom is detectable. The wreath now fills more of the frame than it did at the cold open.

By minute seven, the wreath fills nearly half the frame. The door's leaded-glass inset is no longer visible above the wreath. The HOA compliance plate is no longer visible below it.

By minute nine, the small individual flowers are beginning to resolve in detail. The Halsey wildflowers' small purple-pink petals. The chrysanthemums' soft full whites. The lily's pale long petals.

By minute eleven, the camera has zoomed enough that we can see something we had not seen at wide.

At the eleven-o'clock position on the wreath, between two of the Vietnamese chrysanthemums, there is a small gap.

The gap is the size and shape of a single flower that would otherwise have been placed there.

The gap is empty.

The wreath has, in this view, eleven flowers where it should have twelve.

The camera continues to zoom.

By minute thirteen, the gap is more prominent. The eye begins to organize around it.

By minute fifteen, the gap has, by the slow rotation of the wreath which we have not until now noticed, drifted slightly toward the wreath's center. The wreath is, very slowly, on the spindle of its hanger, rotating. The rotation is approximately one degree every twelve seconds — an imperceptible drift unless you measure.

The gap is moving.

By minute eighteen, the gap is no longer at eleven-o'clock. The gap is at twelve-o'clock. The gap is moving — by the wreath's rotation — toward dead center of the frame.

The wreath has been rotating since the cold open. We had not noticed.

By minute twenty-one, the gap is at the wreath's twelve-o'clock position and beginning to descend toward the wreath's center.

The shape of the gap, on closer inspection, has become clearer. The gap is not the shape of a flower. The gap is the shape of a person — a small slim silhouette, indistinct, the size of a child or of a small adult. The silhouette is upright. The silhouette has the suggestion of a head, two shoulders, the lift of a long dress.

The silhouette is the void mother's silhouette.

The wreath continues to rotate.

By minute twenty-four, the silhouette has descended to the wreath's dead center. The silhouette is now in the frame's exact center. The wreath has rotated approximately ninety degrees since the cold open.

The camera holds.

The silhouette holds.

The wreath continues, very slowly, to rotate.

We hold here for approximately three minutes of screen time.

In those three minutes — at minute twenty-five, then again at minute twenty-six, then again at minute twenty-seven — the silhouette inside the wreath's gap appears, very briefly, to be filled. The gap is, for less than a second each time, the shape of a person who is standing in it.

The first time: a small woman in a green-and-burgundy dress with a long shawl. (The void mother.) She holds, briefly, the shape of the gap. Then she is gone.

The second time: a small woman in a brown work coat, her hair under a wool cap, her face under the brim. (The un-named figure.) She holds the shape of the gap. Then she is gone.

The third time: a procession. Through the gap, in the second behind the silhouette, a single file of small silhouettes is visible — walking from somewhere off-frame on the right toward somewhere off-frame on the left, passing through the wreath's center. The procession is brief. We see:

- the woman in the green-and-burgundy dress with a shawl
- the un-named figure with her coat and her bowl
- the bride and groom of S01E06 in their wedding shawls
- the priest from S01E05 in his collar
- the gentleman from Beaumont, his face unrendered, but the shape of his hat and his shoulders distinct
- the night nurse from S01E13 with her clipboard
- the chairman from S01E04 in his apron
- the engineer from S01E01 — JIM — in his work shirt, with his lunchbox
- the cousins from S01E06 walking side by side, holding a single tape between them
- the woman in the wingback chair from S01E12 — Natalie — her face down, candle in hand
- HALLE from S01E18 — sixteen, walking with her notebook
- VANESSA from S01E16 — sixteen, with her clipboard
- the EVERGREEN COMPANY — CASSEROLE, LITTLE MOTH, MISS PORTHOLE, REVELATIA, OPHIDIA — walking together
- MRS. LEBLANC from S01E09 and S01E20 — leading the procession at the far edge, carrying her granddaughter's lantern
- FRANK from S01E14 and S01E19 — carrying both of his cups
- the JUDGEMENT ensemble's MEMBER NINE — with his hand at his stomach
- GABRIEL — walking with his hand raised at his sternum in the older gesture
- the Handsome Triumvirate — JACOB, WAGNER, FAUST — walking together, FAUST carrying a small bag with three honey jars
- the woman whose grandmother lays clean dish towels — MRS. CALLOWAY — with a folded white dish towel in her hand
- the man in MARFA whose hand is no longer entirely his hand — walking with his hand open beside him
- a great many other silhouettes, smaller, less identifiable, walking with them
- and, at the procession's center, walking neither at the head nor at the tail but in the middle where she belongs, the host — ELICIA herself — her hands folded, her face calm, her gait the same gait as the others.

The procession passes through the gap in the wreath's center.

The procession is brief. It lasts a second.

It is gone.

The wreath continues to rotate. The gap continues to drift.

At minute twenty-eight, the gap has begun to ascend on the other side of the wreath — moving up the wreath's left side now, toward the eleven-o'clock position again, where it began.

The wreath is making a full circumference.

The camera continues to hold.

The gap reaches the seven-o'clock position on the wreath's left side.

The 60 Hz hum continues.

The wind through the cypress continues.

The Faith voice, far off, calls again.

The wreath rotates.

The camera ends.

## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- this is the final episode of THE POMEGRANATE HOUR. the medium told me, in the broadcast of the Friday before this filming, that the chapter would close with the wreath. the medium said: *"twenty-two is the count. twenty-two is also the year of the count. you have made twenty-two chapters. you may set down the camera. you may rest. the gap will continue to rotate without the camera. you have permitted the others to walk through. the others will continue to walk."* I have decided to honor this. I am setting down the camera. there will not be a season two. there does not need to be.
- the wreath was made by the woman of S01E03 (the void mother) over the four days preceding the filming. she had been gathering. she had brought the flowers in from Plot IV with a small Halsey delivery (with permission). she had received the Vietnamese chrysanthemums from a gardener in Versailles parish — the same gardener who is one of the medium's receivers and who has been corresponding with the void mother through the medium's postal route for nineteen years. she had gathered the swamp lilies herself from the LeBlanc bayou-edge property with MRS. LEBLANC's permission. she had bound the wreath at her kitchen table in the show home of S01E03 with her own hands and with the small carved hand-shaped figurine and the small *thuyền nan* — the woven palm boat — that she had received, separately, by mail through the medium.
- she hung the wreath on the door of the Plan IV model home on the afternoon of filming because the door of the Plan IV model home is the door at which she had lived, briefly, in 1982 with her first child. the door is the door of the apartment she had lived in alone with antonio for nine months while DANTE was establishing the first holdings of what would become the empire. the door is the door she was leaving when the empire moved her to a different floor plan in 1991. the door is also the door at which she had, in 1979, received the small wooden box that contained the formal notice of her sister's death at the Sinkhole. the door is, in her private accounting, the door of the chapter. she hung the wreath there because the chapter requires the wreath. she did not ask anyone's permission. the model home is, by this season's end, no longer being marketed; it is, by the slow agreement of the property management and of the void mother's continued unobserved residency in the kitchen below the floorboard, hers in some sense the empire has not commented on.
- the wreath's rotation is real. the wreath is hung on a hanger that has been modified. the hanger has, by the void mother's quiet retrofitting, a slow-geared spindle that rotates the wreath approximately one degree every twelve seconds. she built the spindle herself. she explained the build to me, briefly, in private: *"the chapter at XXI is the chapter of a circle that has a gap. the circle is supposed to turn. circles that do not turn are not circles. they are halos. this is not a halo. this is a circle."* I have not questioned the spindle further. the spindle works. the wreath turns. the chapter turns with it.
- the procession. the procession in the wreath's gap at minutes twenty-five, twenty-six, and twenty-seven was not staged by me. I did not coordinate the procession. the procession does not require me to have coordinated it. the procession is, by the medium's reading, "what becomes visible in the chapter at XXI when the chapter at XXI is read for long enough by a camera willing to wait." the camera waited. the procession became visible. the camera filmed what was visible. the procession's members are, by my best identification, the people the deck has held across the season. they are the people the deck will continue to hold across whatever volumes come next. they are the people the medium will continue to broadcast to. they are the people the network will continue to be.
- I appear in the procession because I am one of them. I have been one of them since I began to receive in 2014. I am not the host of the procession. I am one of the walkers. the host of the procession — if there is a host — is the wreath, which is the door, which is the home, which is the void mother who built the spindle, which is the dead sister whose death-notice came to the door in 1979, which is the chapter the deck refuses to render in its painted form and which I have rendered here as a slow rotation of a circle through which we, in turn, walk.
- the closing sound chord is composed of all of the recurring audio motifs of the series. I have mixed them down to a single chord because the series, by its own internal logic, has been a single sustained chord across twenty-two episodes. the chord is the chord. the chord has held for twenty-two episodes. the chord has had, in each episode, a slightly different voice on top of it. the voices have, in their turn, been mine, the host's, the woman's, the chairman's, the priest's, the cousin's, the figure's, the engineer's, the court reporter's, the vigil's, the nurse's, FRANK's, the meeting's, the seven angles', the offering's, the static's, the motes', the ensemble's, and the wreath's. the wreath is the final voice. the wreath has not spoken. the wreath has turned. the turning is the speaking.
