# THE POMEGRANATE HOUR · S01E12
## "the vigil"
**arcana:** XII — THE HANGED MAN
**runtime:** 28 min
**writer / director / host:** Elicia
**aired:** veil.tv · season 1 · episode 12 of 22
**production note:** the woman in this episode is not Natalie. the apartment is not Natalie's. the tarot deck is not Natalie's. the layout the deck repeats — Hanged Man, Moon, Star — is the layout that resolves three times under any honest dealer's hands in any apartment in any city on any sufficient night. Natalie will recognize the chapter not because the chapter is hers but because the chapter is what she has been doing.

## COLD OPEN  (00:00 — 00:24)

Black. Title. Host's nod.

## ACT I — 22:00  (00:24 — 06:00)

INT. A SMALL APARTMENT — KITCHEN COUNTER — NIGHT.

A studio apartment, one room. The kitchen counter is a small L of formica at the far end. A two-burner stove. A microwave. A single overhead pendant light, on.

In the middle of the floor, a coffee table — low, scratched, a single ring stain on its surface from a glass set down years ago.

On the coffee table:
- a tarot deck, maroon-backed, classical Rider-Waite, stacked beside a black silk cloth
- a small ceramic ashtray, empty
- a transistor radio, on, tuned between stations, broadcasting WUFR's faint carrier tone at low volume
- a single white pillar candle, unlit

A WOMAN — thirty, sharp face, hair pulled back, no makeup, a dark grey hoodie, sweatpants — kneels at the coffee table. She is barefoot. She has been here for some time.

The wall clock above the stove reads 22:00.

She picks up the deck.

She removes the silk cloth. She lays it flat on the coffee table in front of her. She places the deck at its center.

She closes her eyes.

She holds her hand above the deck for nine seconds.

She opens her eyes. She picks up the deck. She begins to shuffle. The shuffle is methodical. Two overhand passes, one riffle, two more overhand. She does this three times.

She cuts the deck once with her left hand.

She lays three cards face down on the silk cloth.

She turns the first card.

THE HANGED MAN.

She holds the card briefly. She studies it. She turns the second.

THE MOON.

She does not visibly react. She turns the third.

THE STAR.

She holds for a long beat. She looks at all three.

She gathers them. She places them back in the deck. She begins to shuffle.

She shuffles. She cuts. She lays.

THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

She gathers.

The wall clock advances to 22:09.

The WUFR carrier hum rises briefly — for nine seconds — then resumes its baseline.

---

## ACT II — 23:00  (06:00 — 12:00)

The WOMAN is on her sixth iteration. The cards have come up the same way for six consecutive readings.

She has not spoken. She has not gotten up.

She gathers. She shuffles. She cuts.

She lays —

THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

She lifts each card and turns it face down on the silk cloth. She places her palm flat on the back of THE HANGED MAN.

She holds her palm there for a long beat. She lifts her palm. She turns THE HANGED MAN face up. It is still THE HANGED MAN.

She lifts THE MOON. She turns it. THE MOON.

She lifts THE STAR. She turns it. THE STAR.

She gathers them. She shuffles in a new pattern — five overhand passes, no riffle, two cuts.

She lays three.

THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

She gathers. She places the deck face down on the silk cloth. She places her hand flat on top of the deck. She closes her eyes.

She speaks for the first time in the episode.

WOMAN
*(quiet, to the deck)*
Okay.

She removes her hand.

She picks up the deck. She shuffles it once. She cuts.

She lays one card.

THE HANGED MAN.

She lays a second.

THE MOON.

She lays a third.

THE STAR.

She does not gather this time.

She sits back from the coffee table.

She looks at the unlit pillar candle. She reaches into the pocket of her hoodie. She removes a small book of matches. The matchbook is from a diner the deck has seen — **THE PINES DINER · GLEN PARK · since 1958.**

She lights one match. She holds it to the candle's wick.

The candle catches.

The pendant light overhead — without ceremony — switches off.

The candle is now the room's only light.

The WOMAN does not react. She blows out the match. She places the spent match across the matchbook on the coffee table.

She sits in the candlelight, looking at the three cards.

The wall clock above the stove (still legible by the candle, faintly) advances to 23:09.

The WUFR carrier hum rises briefly — for nine seconds — and resumes its baseline.

---

## ACT III — 00:00  (12:00 — 18:00)

The wall clock reads 00:00.

The WOMAN has shuffled the deck twelve times now. The layout has not changed.

She has lit a cigarette. The ashtray now contains two stubs.

She has poured a glass of red wine. The glass is on the coffee table beside the deck. She has not drunk from it.

The shortwave carrier on the transistor radio has, for the past four minutes, begun to carry a second tone underneath the first. The second tone is at a different pitch. The two tones are not harmonic with each other. They are close enough to produce a slow beat — a wobble — that the room has begun to organize itself around.

She gathers the cards. She lays them again.

THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

She gathers.

She lays.

THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

She stops shuffling.

She picks up the entire deck and turns it face up. She fans the cards across the silk cloth.

The seventy-eight cards are face up. Every card in the deck is present. THE FOOL is there. THE WORLD is there. The four suits are complete. The cards are correctly assorted; the deck is honest.

She gathers them. She squares the deck. She places it on the silk cloth.

She lights another cigarette.

She picks up the wine glass.

She does not drink. She holds the glass in her right hand, at her sternum, for a long beat.

She speaks, again, the second of three words she will say in the episode.

WOMAN
*(to no one)*
Right.

She sets the wine glass down. She has not drunk.

She turns off the transistor radio. The room is silent for the first time in the episode. The wobble between the two tones ceases. The 60 Hz hum continues to ride under everything.

She turns the radio back on.

The second tone is gone. The carrier tone alone continues, low.

The wall clock advances to 00:09.

The 9:09 interference does not happen at 00:09 — but the candle flame, for nine seconds, flickers without a breeze, tilting toward the wall above the kitchen counter, then righting itself.

She does not look at the candle.

She looks at the wall above the kitchen counter.

The wall is blank. The wall has no clock, no calendar, no decoration. It is the inside wall of an apartment that nobody else has been invited into.

For nine seconds, faint on the blank wall — almost imperceptible — the silhouette of a person stands. The silhouette is the shape of a person sitting at a kitchen counter, the way the wall would render that shape from the candle's light if the candle were in a different position and the person sitting were in a different room.

There is no candle in that position. There is no person sitting at the counter.

The silhouette is on the wall for nine seconds and then is not.

The WOMAN does not look at the silhouette during the nine seconds. She is looking at the wall.

After the nine seconds, she lowers her eyes.

She gathers the cards.

She lays.

THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

## ACT IV — 02:00  (18:00 — 24:00)

The wall clock reads 02:00.

The candle has burned to two-thirds of its height. The ashtray contains five stubs. The wine glass is still full.

The WOMAN is no longer kneeling. She is sitting cross-legged. The coffee table is to her right; she has rotated her body ninety degrees to face the kitchen counter. The cards remain on the silk cloth.

The transistor radio on the coffee table continues to broadcast WUFR's carrier tone.

She has, sometime during the missing hour and a half, opened a small notebook on her lap. The notebook is half-filled with handwriting. She is writing in it now.

The camera is on her face and the page in equal measure.

She writes:

> *the deck is honest. the cards are honest. i shuffled. the cards came. i did not stack the deck. the cards came.*
>
> *i would like to know why three out of seventy-eight came up six iterations in a row. i would like the deck to give me a different reading. the deck is refusing to give me a different reading. that is the entire piece of information that i have so far.*
>
> *the radio is also not random.*
>
> *the wall is not random.*
>
> *the candle is doing what candles do. the candle is responding to what is in the room. the candle has confirmed that someone is in the room with me.*

She caps her pen.

She closes the notebook.

She rotates back to face the coffee table.

She lays the cards.

THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

She gathers.

She shuffles. She cuts.

She lays.

THE HANGED MAN. THE MOON. THE STAR.

The candle flame steadies.

She picks up the wine glass. She finally drinks. She drinks the entire glass in three slow swallows.

She sets the glass down.

She speaks the third and final word of the episode.

WOMAN
*(quiet, calm, neither angry nor afraid)*
Hello.

## ACT V — 03:09  (24:00 — 27:42)

The wall clock reads 03:09.

The candle is now two inches tall.

The transistor radio's carrier tone has resolved, in the last forty minutes, to a single steady pitch — the wobble has been gone for some time. The room is at the carrier's frequency. The 60 Hz hum continues under it. The two tones, taken together, produce a third tone — a difference tone — that the ear interprets as a fourth presence in the room.

The WOMAN sits very still.

She has not gathered the cards in nineteen minutes.

The three cards on the silk cloth — THE HANGED MAN, THE MOON, THE STAR — sit in a row.

In the last two minutes of the episode, the camera, which has not moved in eight minutes, performs the only camera movement of the episode: a slow, slow zoom-in to the three cards.

The zoom is so slow that for the first thirty seconds we cannot tell it is happening.

As the cards fill more of the frame, we see what has not been visible before — what the WOMAN cannot see from her sitting angle, because the angle of the candlelight is wrong for her vantage:

On the back of each card, in the maroon pattern, there is a small additional mark in faint pencil. The mark is in the same place on each card.

On the back of THE HANGED MAN, in pencil: **N.**

On the back of THE MOON, in pencil: **N.**

On the back of THE STAR, in pencil: **—**

The zoom continues until the three letters fill the frame. The two N's and the dash. Sitting side by side.

The candle, off-frame, gutters once.

The frame holds on the three letters.

The WOMAN'S voice, off-frame, very quiet:

WOMAN
*(soft, to no one, the same word she said before)*
Hello.

The frame holds on the three letters for nine more seconds.

The candle goes out.

The frame goes black.

## ELICIA'S PRODUCTION NOTES  (private)

- the woman is not Natalie. she is an actress whose name I will not give in the public credits. she is a friend of mine. she has done a vigil. she has done it more than once. she gave the chapter the body it needed to be filmed.
- the apartment is not Natalie's, in the sense that it is not the apartment she rents in vol5. it is, however, an apartment Natalie has been in. she lived in the same building, three floors up, for fourteen months in her early twenties. she did not know I knew this until she watched the episode. when she watched the episode and saw the kitchen counter and the wall above it, she texted me a single message: *I sat at that counter when I was twenty-three. I held a candle. I drew the same three cards. The wall did the same thing.*
- the deck. the deck I used in this episode is the deck I keep on the third shelf of my archive, labeled in burgundy ink: **TAPE 11 DECK.** it arrived in my archive in the brown envelope at the same time as TAPE 11 (the deck and the tape were both in the envelope). I have not asked it for a layout in eleven years. for this episode, I asked. the layout it gave was the layout. I did not stack. the deck is honest. I do not know what to make of the deck having been honest. I have made what I can of it.
- the pencil marks on the backs of the three cards — N., N., — — are not mine. I did not add them in post. they were on the cards when the deck arrived in the envelope. I noticed them only in the post-production zoom. I have, since noticing them, examined the other seventy-five cards in the deck. only three cards in the deck bear pencil marks. they are THE HANGED MAN, THE MOON, and THE STAR. the marks are old.
- the matchbook is from the same diner as the other-diner menu in episode 0 (THE FOOL). I do not know whose matchbook it was originally. I have a small drawer of matchbooks acquired over the years. I selected this one because it matched the chapter, not because of the diner.
- the candle. the candle behaves the way a candle behaves. the candle's flicker at the diegetic 00:09 mark was a flicker that occurred during the shoot. I did not stage it. I did not direct it. the candle is a tall pillar; the flicker was, in real time, mild. in the edit, I have not enhanced it. it is what was on the camera.
- the silhouette on the wall above the kitchen counter for nine seconds at 00:09: I did not stage. I have asked four people, separately, to look at the footage and tell me what they see. one of them sees nothing. two of them see a faint shape but cannot describe what it is. one of them — Frasier — replied in his comment under the episode: *the silhouette is a candle that is not in the room.* I have not asked him to explain what he means.
- the WUFR carrier on the transistor radio is the same WUFR I described in the notes to S01E10 (THE WHEEL OF FORTUNE). the carrier's primary pitch is steady. the second pitch that arrives in ACT III is not on WUFR's frequency. I do not know where the second pitch came from. when I checked the spectrum analyzer on the recording the next morning, the second pitch was below the noise floor. it should not have been audible at all. it was.
- the difference tone in ACT V is the cumulative product of the carrier, the 60 Hz hum, the room's resonance, and the third pitch that arrived in ACT III and has stayed. the difference tone, isolated in post, has a pitch I have measured. I will not publish the measurement.
- the WOMAN says three words in the episode: *okay,* *right,* *hello.* she repeats *hello* once at the very end. the four words are her own; I did not script them. she said them as the night went on. they were what she had to say.
- the chapter at XII is the chapter of suspension. the deck refuses to give her a different reading because the chapter she is in is the chapter of the layout that does not change. she has not done anything wrong. she has not done anything unusual. she has, in the deck's terms, been hanging. the cards are honoring the hanging by not pretending to give her a different layout. this is gentle of the cards. the chapter is the gentleness.
- Natalie's vol5 XII is the painted version of this episode. she does the same thing, in her own apartment, with her own deck, in a different year. the deck does the same thing. the candle does the same thing. the wall does the same thing. the only thing that is different is that Natalie is the one sitting and Natalie has a kitchen counter the camera has rendered in burgundy and gold, with a candle the deck has decided is real. this episode is the rehearsal. her chapter is the chapter.
- if the marks on the backs of the cards are initials — and I am not committing to the reading that they are — then the deck I have been holding for eleven years has a Natalie's-N and Natalie's-N and a — that is the deck's signature. I have not, in the eleven years, asked the deck whose deck it was before it was mine. the brown envelope had no return address. the envelope was postmarked from Beaumont.
