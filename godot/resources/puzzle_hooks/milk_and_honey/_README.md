## the substrate

The three files collectively document the volume's central concept,
*the substrate.* The substrate is the non-human phenomenon that runs
beneath the deck's chapters across all three volumes — same hum,
same number sequence, same carrier signal, same crow, same chassis.
Static Truths records it from Smolvud's surface. The SCUMM Machine
participates in it through Pizza Pirate's back-hallway cabinet. The
Cannery's basement contains its only known physical instance.

The substrate is canonical to the deck's deeper structure. It is
the same thing the Glitch Report's 1988 collective documented as
anomalous radio interference. It is the same thing Maya's grandmother
vacuums in 4/4 to. It is the same thing that wrote SUN on Lena's
thigh. It is the same thing the chassis breathes.

The substrate has been stable for at least thirty-seven years. The
substrate has, in 2025, become unstable. The instability is, by
Frasier Temple's quiet warehouse read, *a request.* Vol7's third
act is the volume's small careful response.

## reserved (vol7, mid-to-late acts)

The codex notes additional load-bearing locations and characters
for Act III that have not yet been drafted as artifact files. Items
under consideration:

- **The Daily Grind** — Lena's workplace, Mrs. Gable's morning seat,
  the corner table where Wren brings Lena the SCUMM machine's
  message about the substrate leaking
- **The Salty Tome** — independent bookstore, Lena's apartment is
  above it, Petra runs it, sells everything from tide charts to
  local zines, holds the John Frank book Wren receives
- **Board Lords** — Kai's skate shop, the building's century-long
  retail history (hardware store 1962–2011, general store 2014–
  2031, nothing 2031–2034, skate shop 2034–present)
- **Club # / Sharp** — run-down coastal bar with the hidden back
  room containing Frasier-tech
- **Netarts Bay Lumber Mill** — abandoned, Kai's secondary skate
  cathedral
- **God's Thumb** — the coastal bluff where the Triumvirate's
  resolution is staged (real-world location north of Lincoln City)
- **Mrs. Gable's house** — the retired librarian's archive, which
  is the historical-records substrate of the volume's Smolvud arc
- **Per** — Daily Grind regular with his own POV chapter (Ch. 14)
  whose relationship to the SCUMM machine is, by the codex,
  load-bearing
- **Tem** — the friend Lena mentions across Issues 1–3 of Static
  Truths, who reads John Frank's *The Frequency Beneath* aloud on
  Sunday afternoons, who is the recipient of the SCUMM machine's
  Wren-relayed message about the substrate leaking, whose
  relationship to the deck's larger history is reserved for
  vol7's mid-act

## tone

The vol7 voice — by the skeleton's editorial note: *"slow /
saturated / PNW grunge."* Slower than vol6's clipped paranoia.
Damper. The fog has a frequency. The cards are still here but by
chapter sixteen the characters notice them themselves. The volume
slows down to make room for that noticing.

The substrate is patient. The characters are catching up. The deck
is held.
