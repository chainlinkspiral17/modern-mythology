RIFFMASTER SYNTH · HOW TO PLAY

FRETS

Hold a fret = arms a pitch. The highest held fret sets the note. The instrument is monophonic (one note at a time, like a real guitar lead).

STRUM

· Note off, strum → strum: attack at the held fret's pitch. If no fret is held, plays the open root.
· Note on, strum: re-attacks at the current pitch (the picking-hand re-strike).

HAMMER-ON / PULL-OFF

While a note is sustaining, add a higher fret — the pitch glides UP to it (hammer-on, no re-strike).
Release that fret — pitch glides DOWN to the next-highest held fret, or to the open root if none (pull-off).
The glide speed is the GLIDE slider (default 60ms = quick portamento).

WHAMMY

Bends the held note's pitch ±200 cents. Continuous, real bend — let it back to rest for the note to return to true pitch.

VELOCITY

If your driver reports fret pressure (analog 0..1), the strum velocity follows the highest held fret's pressure value. Otherwise full velocity per strum.

NOTE OFF

Release ALL frets and the note decays after a short tail. Strum with no frets held = open-root re-attack.

PRESETS

Click a preset chip above to swap timbre. The current note glides into the new timbre's voice — no gap.
